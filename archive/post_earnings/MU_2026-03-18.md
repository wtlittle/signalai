# MU (Micron Technology) -- Post-Earnings Notes
## Reported: March 18, 2026 (After Close)
## Quarter: Q2 FY2026 (ended February 26, 2026)

---

### The Setup

Micron entered the print as the purest play on the AI memory supercycle. The stock had rallied 350%+ over the prior year on surging HBM (High Bandwidth Memory) demand driven by Nvidia's AI chip ecosystem. Expectations were already elevated -- consensus called for $19.6B revenue and $8.80 EPS. The key question was whether the memory supply shortage was tightening further or starting to ease. Every AI infrastructure builder (hyperscalers, sovereign clouds, enterprise) had been scrambling for memory capacity.

Records set across revenue, gross margin, EPS, and free cash flow. This was not a modest beat -- it was a blowout across every metric.

### The Story of the Quarter

The AI memory supercycle entered a new phase in Q2. Revenue nearly tripled YoY ($23.86B vs $8.05B) and jumped 75% sequentially from $13.64B. The story was supply constraint meeting exponential demand:

**Cloud Memory BU** ($7.75B, +163% YoY): HBM demand from hyperscalers (Google, Microsoft, Amazon, Meta) remained insatiable. HBM3E shipments ramped aggressively. Gross margins hit 74%, reflecting premium pricing power.

**Core Data Center BU** ($5.69B, +211% YoY): Enterprise and sovereign AI buildouts drove demand for high-capacity DIMMs and SSDs. This was the fastest-growing segment, signaling AI spending broadening beyond the top 4 hyperscalers.

**Mobile and Client BU** ($7.71B, +245% YoY): The standout. LPDDR5X for AI-capable smartphones and laptops drove massive growth. Gross margins of 79% were the highest of any segment -- reflecting both tight supply and Apple/Samsung willingness to pay premiums for cutting-edge memory.

**Auto and Embedded BU** ($2.71B, +162% YoY): Recovery in automotive and IoT after a cyclical trough. Gross margins of 68% reflected improving content per vehicle as ADAS adoption accelerated.

CEO Sanjay Mehrotra's framing was notable: "Memory has become a strategic asset for our customers." This is a positioning shift -- memory moving from commodity to critical component, which supports structural margin improvement.

### Why the Stock Reacted

Despite the massive beat, MU shares fell ~5% on March 19. Why?

1. **Buy the rumor, sell the news**: After 350%+ gains over 12 months, the bar was not just consensus -- it was whisper numbers well above Street. Some investors had modeled $25B+ revenue.
2. **Capex ramp concerns**: The company is investing aggressively in HBM manufacturing capacity. Capital expenditures of $5.0B in Q2 (net) raised questions about future FCF sustainability even as current FCF hit records.
3. **Peak margin fears**: 74.9% gross margins are extraordinary for a memory company. The natural question is whether this is the peak, with normalization ahead as supply eventually catches demand.
4. **Valuation digestion**: At 350%+ appreciation, some profit-taking was inevitable regardless of the print quality.

The sell-the-news reaction deepened significantly in the days following. MU closed at $462 on earnings day (Mar 18), briefly spiked to $457 on Mar 19, then entered a sustained decline: $423 (Mar 20), $404 (Mar 23), $396 (Mar 24), $382 (Mar 25), $355 (Mar 26), $357 (Mar 27). The ~22% post-earnings drawdown from peak to trough reflects broader market risk-off (VIX >31) combined with profit-taking in the highest-beta AI names. Despite the selloff, the fundamental trajectory remains unambiguously strong.

### Guidance and Tone

Q3 FY2026 guidance was remarkable:
- Revenue: $33.5B (+/- $750M) -- implying 40%+ sequential growth and 300%+ YoY
- Gross Margin: ~81% (Non-GAAP) -- another significant expansion
- EPS: $19.15 (+/- $0.40) -- 57% above Q2's already-record level

Management tone was highly confident. Mehrotra cited "sustained strength" and "strategic value of memory in the AI era." The board approved a 30% dividend increase, signaling confidence in cash flow durability.

The guidance implies supply constraints are tightening further, not easing. The 800G/1.6T/3.2T datacenter interconnect transition is pulling forward HBM demand faster than capacity can ramp.

### Thesis Impact

**Strengthens the bull case significantly.** The results validate that:
- The AI memory supercycle has more legs than skeptics expected
- Micron's margin structure has fundamentally shifted (not a commodity cycle)
- HBM supply/demand imbalance persists into at least late 2026
- Free cash flow generation supports both capex and returns

The bear case (peak cycle, margin normalization, China risk) remains but is pushed further out. With Q3 guidance implying $19+ EPS on a single quarter, annualized earnings power exceeds $60/share.

**Risk to monitor**: Any signs of HBM supply easing, hyperscaler capex slowdowns, or China trade restrictions on memory.

### Follow-ups

1. Track Q3 report (expected June 2026) for margin trajectory -- does 81% hold or is it the peak?
2. Monitor competitor commentary (Samsung, SK Hynix) for supply additions
3. Watch hyperscaler capex commentary in April earnings (MSFT, GOOG, META, AMZN) for demand signals
4. Track capex/FCF trajectory -- is Micron investing sustainably or over-building?
5. China/trade policy developments -- any restrictions on memory exports would be material
