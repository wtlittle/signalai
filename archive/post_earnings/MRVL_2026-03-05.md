# Marvell Technology (MRVL) — Q4 FY2026 Post-Earnings Note
**Reported:** March 5, 2026 | **Fiscal Quarter Ended:** January 31, 2026 | **Updated:** March 23, 2026

---

## 1. The Setup

Going into the March 5 print, MRVL was a stock caught between two competing narratives. On one side: a company posting 37% year-over-year revenue growth in Q3 and guiding to 21% growth for Q4, with a custom silicon business that had gone from zero to over a billion dollars in just a few years. On the other side: a series of unsettling headlines about design-win risk that had been grinding the stock lower since its December post-earnings spike.

The December Q3 earnings call initially catalyzed optimism — management guided Q4 to $2.20B at midpoint, 1% above the then-consensus of $2.18B — but the enthusiasm was short-lived. In early December 2025, a combination of events hit the stock hard: reports surfaced that Microsoft was in discussions with Broadcom to co-develop future custom AI chips, and Benchmark analyst Cody Acree downgraded MRVL from Buy to Hold, stating he was "highly confident" that Marvell had lost both Amazon's Trainium 3 and Trainium 4 custom XPU designs to Alchip, a Taiwanese rival. The stock dropped ~9% in a single session. Then in early February 2026, MRVL fell another ~5% on the day the Celestial AI acquisition closed, as deal dilution weighed on near-term estimates. By early March, Morgan Stanley had cut its price target from $112 to $95 and moved to Equal Weight, citing the Amazon competitive risk — taking the stock down another 4.1% just two days before the print. 

Entering the earnings call on March 5, MRVL was trading around $75.50–$77.50, well off its 52-week high of $102.77 and roughly 25% below its peak. The stock had a consensus price target of ~$115 and a "Moderate Buy" rating, but sentiment had clearly rotated cautious. The core bear debate centered on three questions: (1) Had Marvell lost the Amazon Trainium 3/4 designs that underwrote the custom silicon growth story? (2) Was Microsoft defecting to Broadcom, threatening the "non-Google hyperscaler" narrative? (3) Was the Q4 guidance already embedding a slowdown — deceleration from 37% YoY in Q3 to "only" 21% in Q4 — suggesting the AI infrastructure cycle was maturing? The whisper on the quarter was modest: consensus EPS sat at $0.79 with a 4.3% downward revision over the prior 30 days, and data center revenue consensus was pegged at $1.63B. The street was not positioned for a blowout.

## 3. The Story of the Quarter

The quarter's surface-level numbers were a modest beat — $19M above guidance midpoint on revenue, $0.01 on EPS. But the quarter was a setup story, not a delivery story. What mattered was what happened on the call after 4:45pm PT, when Matt Murphy systematically dismantled every bear thesis that had compressed the stock into the print.

The data center segment delivered $1.651B, up 9% sequentially and 21% year-over-year, slightly ahead of the $1.63B consensus. More importantly, Murphy detailed that sequential growth was broad-based across all product lines — optical interconnects, custom silicon, switching, and storage — each posting gains in the quarter. The "broadness" of the beat matters: bears had worried the interconnect number was holding up while custom silicon was starting to weaken on the Amazon risk narrative. The Q4 print disproved that; custom silicon was up sequentially. The communications and other segment also came in at $567M, up 26% YoY and 2% sequentially, a stronger-than-feared recovery in carrier/enterprise that added another pillar to the revenue foundation.

The custom silicon story is the core of the MRVL thesis and it's where Murphy went long on the call. The custom business closed FY2026 at $1.5B, having doubled year-over-year — the first full year of volume production for major hyperscaler programs. Murphy was unambiguous: "In just a few years, we have scaled from zero revenue to $1.5 billion in fiscal 2026." More critically, he addressed the Amazon bear case without naming it directly. He confirmed that Marvell has "purchase orders covering the entirety of this fiscal year's forecast" for its lead XPU program's next-generation ramp — that's a covered book for FY2027 on what is almost certainly the Amazon relationship. The "new Tier 1 XPU program ramping into high volume production" with "firm volume requirements for all of next year" is widely interpreted as a new hyperscaler win, most likely Microsoft Maia 200, reinforcing that Marvell was gaining rather than losing hyperscaler footprint. Murphy also noted the company has "20-plus design wins or products that are either in production or going into production" — a number that by itself makes the bear thesis of binary customer-concentration risk increasingly hard to sustain.

On the competitive positioning front, Murphy's tone was explicitly confident without being arrogant. The "Switzerland of Silicon" positioning — Marvell as the neutral ASIC partner willing to serve any hyperscaler — got a refresh. He noted that custom compute is "proliferating across the hyperscale ecosystem" with inference-optimized hardware becoming a mainstream priority, driving "an unprecedented level of activity across multiple new engagements." On optical interconnect — where Marvell's competitive position is arguably its strongest and most defensible against both Broadcom and NVIDIA's networking ambitions — the update was equally bullish: 1.6T solutions entered production in H2 FY2026, AEC design wins secured with three Tier 1 U.S. hyperscalers, and DCI module supply relationships expanding to all five major U.S. hyperscalers in FY2027. The Celestial AI acquisition adds co-packaged optics (CPO) capability for scale-up connectivity starting FY2028, a technology vector that Marvell believes opens a massive incremental market. On margin: non-GAAP gross margin of 59.0% held steady, with Q1 guided to 58.25–59.25%, suggesting the product mix between higher-margin optical and custom silicon is stabilizing at a structurally attractive level.

## 4. Why the Stock Reacted the Way It Did

The 18% day-after surge was almost entirely a guidance reaction, not a results reaction. The after-hours move on March 5 was a modest ~2% on the quarter's narrow beat — the market was waiting to read the transcript and process the numbers. By the morning of March 6, that changed decisively. Q1 guidance of $2.40B was 5.7% above the $2.27B consensus and represented 27% year-over-year growth — a re-acceleration from the 22% posted in Q4. That's exactly what the market needed to hear: the fear was that Q4's 22% growth was the beginning of a deceleration cycle. Instead, Murphy explicitly said: "We expect year-over-year revenue growth to accelerate each quarter throughout fiscal 2027." That statement, backed by a concrete Q1 guide and a FY2027 forecast raised to "approaching $11B" from ~$10B in December, forced a violent short cover and multiple expansion simultaneously.

The FY2028 target of ~$15B was the clean "sell the bears on the stock" moment. Street consensus heading into the quarter had FY2028 at roughly $12.92B. Murphy raised that by $2B and called it a floor, not a ceiling — explicitly noting it was "based on demand we are seeing now and designs that are already in execution." Non-GAAP EPS guidance of "well over $5" for FY2028 was similarly above the then-consensus of ~$4.50–$4.75. That's not sandbagging language; that's Murphy telling the market the prior fear around Amazon/Microsoft was systematically wrong and the growth engine was intact. When a CEO with a consistent execution track record puts a $15B revenue stake in the ground and says "do you see me blinking?" on the earnings call, the market tends to believe it.

For context: the prior quarter (Q3, reported December 2025) saw MRVL initially spike on guidance before giving back gains as the Amazon/Microsoft headlines broke in the following days. That quarter's net move was roughly flat to slightly positive. This quarter's move was in a completely different league — structurally the market was short or underweight MRVL into the print given the bear narratives, and the sheer magnitude of the FY2027/FY2028 guidance upside forced a positioning flush. The Benchmark analyst who had downgraded to Hold on Amazon design-win concerns promptly reversed that downgrade to Buy with a $130 target the morning of March 6, acknowledging the growth drivers were broader than any single customer. Bank of America similarly reversed its Neutral rating to Buy. When the analysts most associated with the bear case capitulate within 12 hours of the print, that's an accelerant to the move.

## 7. Thesis Impact

For a buy-side analyst with a 1–3 year horizon, this print did something rare: it simultaneously validated the bull case *and* resolved the key bear case. The Amazon Trainium design-win concern — which had been the most credible short thesis on the stock for three months — got addressed obliquely but convincingly. If Marvell had truly lost Trainium 3 and 4, you cannot guide Q1 data center up 10% sequentially or raise FY2027 by $1B. The math doesn't work. The "new Tier 1 XPU program ramping into high volume production" with firm purchase orders for all of FY2027 effectively tells you Marvell's hyperscaler relationships are expanding, not contracting.

The thesis is now a two-part story with increasing durability. Part one is custom silicon: a business at $1.5B in FY2026 that doubles to ~$3B+ in FY2028, with 20+ design wins/sockets in production or en route, and diversification improving across all five major U.S. hyperscalers. The XPU attach opportunity in custom NIC and CXL ($2B+ in sight by FY2029) extends the durability of the custom segment beyond the initial XPU programs. Part two is optical interconnect: Marvell's most differentiated position, where it supplies DSPs and PAM4 chips for 800G and now 1.6T solutions, serves all five major hyperscalers for DCI modules, and has co-packaged optics (via Celestial AI) as a meaningful FY2028+ catalyst. The optical business is less sensitive to any single customer's ASIC design decisions — it lives in the network fabric regardless of who designs the compute. This is the risk diversification the bears were missing.

**Bull case (12–18 months):** MRVL at 25–28x FY2028 non-GAAP EPS of $5.25–$5.50 = $131–$154. The stock is trading at ~$89 at this writing. If management delivers on $11B in FY2027 and shows credible progress toward $15B in FY2028 over the next two or three quarterly prints, the market will be forced to price in FY2028 earnings — the multiple should expand, not compress. Morningstar's 22x FY2028 earnings fair value of $130 feels conservative; Goldman's $100 target is explicitly a Neutral based on execution uncertainty, not valuation theory. **Bear case (12–18 months):** Any material slowdown in hyperscaler CapEx, gross margin compression from mix shift (more custom silicon at potentially lower margins vs. optical), or execution risk on the $15B FY2028 target. If the new Tier 1 XPU program (Microsoft Maia 200) encounters delays similar to Amazon's Trainium 2 ramp, the FY2027/FY2028 guide is at risk. At 20x FY2027 consensus EPS of ~$3.82 = $76, roughly where the stock was before the print. The risk/reward is asymmetric to the upside: the upside scenario ($130+) is 45–55% above current levels; the downside scenario ($76) is ~15% below current levels on a realistic bear.

## 8. Follow-Ups (Specific and Actionable)

1. **Track the "New Tier 1 XPU Program" ramp cadence in Q1 FY2027 (May earnings).** Murphy confirmed purchase orders covering all of FY2027 for this program. The Q1 data center guide of +10% sequential should be seen as the minimum bar for validation. If Q1 data center comes in above $1.80B, the FY2027 $11B guide has credibility. If it misses, reassess.

2. **Monitor 1.6T optical interconnect revenue ramp.** Murphy said 1.6T entered production in H2 FY2026 and will "ramp very rapidly in fiscal 2027." This is a product cycle within a product cycle — 1.6T on top of 800G. Ask: is 1.6T visible in the Q1/Q2 data center revenue mix, and what is the implied ASP uplift vs. 800G? Interconnect growing >50% in FY2027 is management's forecast.

3. **Watch gross margin trajectory for evidence of custom silicon mix dilution.** Goldman Sachs flagged ~0.25% QoQ erosion through CY2026. Non-GAAP gross margin was 59.0% in Q4; Q1 guided to 58.25–59.25%. If margin walks toward the low end of guidance for two consecutive quarters, the operating leverage story weakens and the EPS forecast of $5+ for FY2028 needs revisiting.

4. **Track Amazon publicly surfaced commentary on Trainium 3/4.** Amazon has not confirmed Alchip as a design partner. Any AWS re:Invent or re:Mars disclosure about next-generation Trainium architecture or volume production would either validate or refute the Benchmark downgrade thesis that drove the pre-earnings weakness. This is the single biggest latent risk in the bull case.

5. **Track Celestial AI/XConn integration progress.** These acquisitions are expected to contribute ~$250M incrementally in FY2028. Murphy noted co-packaged optics (CPO) commercial deployment starting FY2028/FY2029. Channel checks on TSMC advanced packaging capacity allocation for CPO will be an early indicator of whether this revenue materializes on schedule.

6. **Review consensus estimates post-earnings.** FY2027 consensus EPS likely moving from ~$3.40 to ~$3.80–$3.90 (BofA now at $3.82, Goldman raised to $3.80). FY2028 EPS moving from ~$4.50 to ~$5.25+. The pace of estimate revisions over the next 4–8 weeks will signal how quickly the buy-side is marking up models — which feeds into further potential multiple expansion if the stock "cheapens" against rising EPS.

7. **Benchmark the MRVL vs. AVGO trajectory.** Broadcom reported AI-driven beats of its own in recent quarters and holds a dominant ~60% share of AI ASIC design services by some estimates. Watch the relative revenue growth trajectories: if MRVL's custom silicon grows 20%+ in FY2027 while AVGO's AI revenue continues compounding, it validates the "multiple hyperscaler partner" ecosystem dynamic rather than winner-take-all. If AVGO grows at 60%+ and MRVL trails, revisit market share assumptions.
