### Set-up

Nike trades at ~$52, near its 52-week low, down ~60% over five years and ~20% YTD. The stock is at 2017 valuation levels. CEO Elliott Hill (returned Oct 2024) is 16 months into a "Win Now" turnaround focused on five pillars: culture, product, marketing, marketplace, and in-person presence. The running category has grown 20%+ for two consecutive quarters. North American wholesale grew 24% in Q2 FY2026, global wholesale +8%. Q2 FY2026 EPS of $0.53 beat consensus ($0.37) by 43%. Dividend yield now exceeds 3%.

Key headwinds: China sales contracted 17% in Q2 (sixth consecutive quarter of decline), gross margins pressured by tariffs and inventory liquidation. A ~$300M pre-tax charge for employee severance is expected this quarter, inflating GAAP costs. The Supreme Court tariff ruling last month adds a new overhang. The turnaround is real but "won't be a straight line" per Hill.

### Key Debates and Variants

1. **China trajectory**: Bulls see a bottoming process; bears see structural share loss to local brands (Anta, Li-Ning). A 16-17% decline is baked in — any improvement is upside. World Cup 2026 in the U.S. could be a catalyst for brand heat.
2. **Gross margin trajectory**: ~200bps contraction expected from tariffs + liquidation. Key variant: has tariff mitigation ramped enough to stabilize? Management indicated mitigation accelerating in Q3.
3. **Wholesale reengagement sustainability**: 24% NA wholesale growth in Q2 was the headline. Nike is back on Amazon and expanding to Urban Outfitters, Aritzia. Can it sustain?
4. **FY2027 guidance timing**: Will Nike provide a forward framework or delay until June? Any signal on return to mid-single-digit revenue growth matters enormously for the multiple.
5. **$300M severance charge impact**: One-time but signals ongoing restructuring. How does this reshape the cost base going forward?

### What Matters This Print

1. **Revenue**: Consensus $11.2B (flat to slight decline YoY). Nike Brand revenue est. $10.98B. Any positive growth would be a major signal.
2. **EPS**: Consensus $0.28 (Zacks) to $0.29. Some analysts model higher based on Q2 beat pattern. Earnings ESP +5.26% (Zacks) suggests slight upside.
3. **Gross Margin**: Expected ~42% (down ~200bps YoY). Tariff mitigation commentary critical. Full-price selling ramp timing.
4. **China Revenue**: Consensus ~$1.2B (-16% YoY). Sequential improvement from Q2's $1.4B would be a positive signal. Focus on demand trends and brand positioning vs. local competitors.
5. **North America Wholesale**: Sustained 20%+ growth validates channel strategy. Footwear rev est. +31% in NA (per Visible Alpha).
