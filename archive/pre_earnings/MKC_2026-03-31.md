# MKC (McCormick) — Pre-Earnings Notes
## Earnings Date: March 31, 2026 (Before Open, 8:00 AM ET call)
## Quarter: Q1 FY2026 (Dec-Feb)

---

### Set-up

McCormick trades at ~$53, down 21% since its last earnings report and well below its 1-year high. Market cap ~$14.3B. P/E ~18.2x. The stock is a global flavor leader in both Consumer (retail spices/seasonings) and Flavor Solutions (food service/industrial). CEO Brendan Foley is guiding for organic sales growth of 1-3% and FY2026 EPS of $3.05-$3.13. Last quarter (Q4 FY2025): revenue $1.85B (+2.9% YoY), EPS $0.86 (missed $0.87 by $0.01). Net margin 11.54%, ROE 14.27%.

Insider selling notable: ~115,000 shares (~$7.95M) sold in the last quarter. Despite the selloff, McCormick historically delivers stable, defensive growth — the type of name that works in a Deflation Risk macro regime.

### Key Debates and Variants

1. **Volume recovery vs. price**: Organic sales growth guided at 1-3%. Is this primarily volume-driven (bullish for sustainability) or price-driven (less durable)?
2. **Flavor Solutions margin expansion**: Operating income est. $83.8M vs. $79M prior year (+6%). Can the B2B side accelerate as food service recovers?
3. **Consumer segment growth**: Revenue est. $1.05B (+14.5% YoY) — a significant step up. Is this driven by distribution expansion, new products, or McCormick de Mexico contribution?
4. **Tariff/inflation exposure**: Input cost inflation in spices and packaging. How much pricing power can McCormick exert without losing volume?
5. **McCormick de Mexico integration**: Acquired in FY2025. How is it contributing to top-line growth and margin profile?

### What Matters This Print

1. **Revenue**: Consensus $1.79B (+11.3% YoY). Consumer est. $1.05B, Flavor Solutions est. $724.8M (+5.7%). The revenue acceleration from Q4's 2.9% to 11.3% is largely McCormick de Mexico.
2. **EPS**: Consensus $0.61-$0.63 (+1.7% YoY). Earnings ESP +0.04% — nearly flat, suggesting in-line print likely. FY guidance $3.05-$3.13 implies need for back-half ramp.
3. **Gross Margin**: Q4 came in below expectations. Watch for sequential improvement. Input cost trends in spices and packaging are key.
4. **Volume/Mix vs. Price**: Breakdown of organic growth drivers. Volume growth validates consumer demand; price-only growth raises sustainability questions.
5. **FY2026 Guidance Reaffirmation**: Any narrowing or adjustment to the $3.05-$3.13 EPS range or 1-3% organic sales growth guidance.

### Scenario Grid

**Bull Case (Stock +5-8%, ~$56-57)**
- Revenue beat >$1.80B
- EPS beat >$0.65
- Volume-led organic growth above 2%
- Flavor Solutions margin expansion
- Guidance raised or narrowed to high end
- McCormick de Mexico accretive ahead of schedule

**Base Case (Stock -1% to +3%, ~$52-55)**
- Revenue in-line at $1.79B
- EPS $0.61-$0.63 per consensus
- Organic growth at low end of 1-3%
- Guidance maintained
- McCormick de Mexico contributing as expected

**Bear Case (Stock -5-8%, ~$49-51)**
- Revenue miss <$1.77B
- EPS miss <$0.60 (third miss in four quarters)
- Volume declines, growth all pricing
- Gross margin compression continues
- Guidance cut or lowered to bottom of range
- Input cost inflation commentary worsens
