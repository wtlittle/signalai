## The Story of the Quarter

JPMorgan delivered what may be the most balanced earnings report in the sector. The CIB segment earned $9.0B in net income on $23.4B in revenue (+19% YoY). Investment banking fees rose 28% on strong M&A and equity underwriting. Fixed income revenue surged 21% and equities revenue rose 17%, both reflecting elevated client activity during Q1 volatility.

Consumer & Community Banking (CCB) earned $5.0B on $19.6B in revenue (+7% YoY), with card NII growth and higher auto originations. Home lending originations jumped 46%.

Asset & Wealth Management (AWM) delivered $1.8B net income on $6.4B revenue (+11% YoY), with long-term net inflows of $54B -- a standout figure showing JPM's wealth franchise gaining share.

CET1 capital ratio of 14.3% -- well above requirements, providing ample buyback capacity.
