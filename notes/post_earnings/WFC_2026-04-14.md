## The Story of the Quarter

The headline story was NII stagnation. Net interest income rose 5% YoY to $12.1B but fell short of expectations, weighed down by net interest margin compression even as loan and deposit balances grew. Average loans grew 11% to $996B and deposits rose 7% to $1,415B -- showing the franchise is still expanding, but the yield curve isn't cooperating.

The positives were meaningful: noninterest income rose 8% to $9.4B on higher investment advisory fees and trading revenue. Wealth and Investment Management revenue jumped 14%, with client assets reaching $2.2T (+11%). The efficiency ratio of 67% showed positive operating leverage. A $4.0B share repurchase was aggressive.

The provision for credit losses rose 22% to $1.135B, reflecting economic uncertainty. Net charge-offs were stable at 45 bps.
