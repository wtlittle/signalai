## The Story of the Quarter

The revenue beat was real -- $11.16B represented 7.8% YoY growth, driven by Medical Devices (particularly FreeStyle Libre) and the new oncology diagnostics contribution from Exact Sciences. The product portfolio continues to show strong underlying demand.

However, the narrative was complicated by the Exact Sciences acquisition impact. GAAP EPS dropped from $0.76 to $0.61 as acquisition-related charges (amortization, integration costs) weighed on reported earnings. The adjusted EPS of $1.15 merely matched consensus, offering no upside surprise.

The guidance cut was the real negative. Management lowered full-year adjusted EPS guidance to $5.48 at the midpoint, down 3.4% from prior guidance. This reflects both integration costs and some margin pressure in the Nutrition segment.

---

## Why the Stock Reacted

ABT shares fell approximately 4% despite the revenue beat. The guidance cut and in-line EPS disappointed a market that wanted to see acquisition accretion, not dilution. The Nutrition segment weakness added to concerns. Investors are demanding proof that the Exact Sciences deal will create value.

---

## Guidance and Tone

Management guided to full-year comparable sales growth of 6.5-7.5%, which is healthy. However, the EPS guidance reduction signals that integration will be costlier or take longer than initially communicated. The tone was cautiously optimistic on the oncology diagnostics opportunity but realistic about near-term margin pressure.

---

## Thesis Impact

**Neutral.** The core Abbott franchise (Libre, medical devices) remains strong, and 7.8% organic growth is solid. However, the acquisition-driven EPS dilution and guidance cut create near-term overhang. This is a "digest and integrate" period -- the thesis is intact but the stock needs time to absorb the deal.
