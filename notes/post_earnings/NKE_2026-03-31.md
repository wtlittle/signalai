# NKE (Nike) -- Post-Earnings Notes
## Reported: March 31, 2026 (After Close)
## Quarter: Q3 FY2026 (Dec-Feb)

---

### The Setup

Nike entered the print at ~$53, near its 52-week low of ~$51, down roughly 60% over five years and ~20% YTD. The stock was trading at 2017 valuation levels. CEO Elliott Hill, 16 months into his "Win Now" turnaround, had delivered a surprise Q2 EPS beat ($0.53 vs $0.37 est) but sentiment remained cautious. North American wholesale had grown 24% in Q2, and the running category was up 20%+ for consecutive quarters -- but China was the open wound, contracting 17% in Q2 for the sixth straight quarter of decline. The options market was pricing a ~9% move. Consensus EPS ranged from $0.28 (LSEG/CNBC) to $0.31 (Bloomberg), with revenue expected at ~$11.24B.

The setup was classic turnaround: beat-and-raise would validate the Hill era, while a miss could send shares to decade lows below $48.

### The Story of the Quarter

This was a nuanced quarter that revealed both progress and persistent challenges in the Nike turnaround.

**What went right:**

North America was the bright spot, with revenue of $5.03B (+3% YoY). Wholesale continued to reaccelerate (+5% overall), validating the strategy of re-engaging wholesale partners -- Amazon, Urban Outfitters, Aritzia. The running category continued its 20%+ growth streak. Gross margin of 40.2% beat expectations despite 130bps of tariff headwinds, suggesting the full-price selling ramp is gaining traction and inventory liquidation is subsiding. Inventories at $7.5B (-1% YoY) are lean, which is structurally positive for margin recovery.

**The China puzzle:**

Greater China revenue of $1.62B was technically down 7%, but this was a dramatic improvement from -17% in Q2. More importantly, the segment EBIT of $467M crushed the $269.5M estimate, suggesting management aggressively cut costs and refocused on profitable channels. For bulls, this signals the beginning of a China bottom. For bears, the revenue decline -- even at a slower pace -- still reflects structural share loss to Anta, Li-Ning, and other domestic brands.

**What went wrong:**

Converse was the clear disappointment at $264M (-35%), well below expectations. The brand continues to struggle for relevance. Nike Direct revenue fell 4% to $4.5B, reflecting the strategic pullback from direct-to-consumer markdowns. On a currency-neutral basis, total revenue declined 3%, which is the real underlying trajectory stripped of FX tailwinds.

**The guidance bombshell:**

Despite the Q3 beat, it was the forward outlook that dominated. CFO Matt Friend guided Q4 revenue down 2-4%, versus Wall Street expectations of a nearly 2% increase. For the rest of calendar year 2026, Nike sees sales declining in the low single digits, with North America growth offset by China expected down ~20% in Q4. This was the catalyst for the selloff -- the turnaround timeline just got extended.

### Why the Stock Reacted

NKE stock fell ~10% on April 1 (first trading day after the after-hours report), dropping from $53 to approximately $47-48 in premarket trading, ultimately reaching lows near $47.41.

The reaction was driven by:

1. **Guidance cut, not the beat**: Q4 guide of revenue -2% to -4% vs. +2% consensus. Calendar year outlook of "low single-digit decline" was not in anyone's base case. This extended the turnaround timeline materially.
2. **China -20% in Q4 guide**: While Q3 China improved sequentially (-7% from -17%), management guiding -20% for Q4 suggested the improvement was temporary, not a trend. This undercut the narrative that China had bottomed.
3. **JPMorgan downgrade**: On April 1, JPMorgan downgraded NKE from Overweight to Neutral, cutting PT from $86 to $52. The analyst cited "gradual" recovery with revised earnings expectations below consensus for Q4, FY2027, and FY2028. A "relatively stagnant earnings performance over the next nine months" was the characterization.
4. **Converse drag intensifies**: -35% is alarming. Converse is becoming a value trap within Nike, and management offered no clear fix.
5. **Tariff overhang**: Gross margin had 130bps of tariff headwinds this quarter, and with no clarity on further escalation, investors priced in more pain ahead.
6. **Peak-to-trough pattern**: After the Q2 beat drove brief optimism, this report returned NKE to the "show me" category. At ~$47, the stock tested decade lows.

CFRA analyst Zachary Warring captured the mood: "They're in the middle of a turnaround... They're working on a lot of different things, which is why you've seen a muted reaction. There's just not much to get excited about."

### Guidance and Tone

- **Q4 FY2026**: Revenue down 2-4% (consensus had +2%). Tariff and restructuring headwinds continue.
- **Calendar Year 2026**: Revenue declines "low single digits" overall. North America growth offset by steep international declines. China expected down ~20% in Q4.
- **No FY2027 framework provided**: Management deferred comprehensive forward guidance, adding to uncertainty.
- **~$300M severance charge**: One-time restructuring cost signaling ongoing organizational changes.

Management tone was cautiously optimistic but realistic. CEO Hill reiterated that the turnaround "won't be a straight line" and emphasized product innovation (running, Jordan) and marketplace strategy. The lack of any acceleration signal -- particularly the guidance cut -- was the key disappointment.

### Thesis Impact

**Net negative despite the beat.** The results created a paradox: Q3 numbers were better than expected, but the forward outlook was worse. For a 1-3 year fundamental investor:

**Bull case arguments still alive:**
- Gross margin resilience (40.2% vs 39.8% est despite tariffs) suggests pricing power
- North America wholesale reengagement is working (+5%)
- Running category momentum sustained (20%+ growth)
- Stock at $47 prices in significant pessimism; at ~18-20x depressed earnings, a trough multiple on trough earnings
- China EBIT dramatically better suggests cost restructuring is effective even if top-line weak

**Bear case strengthened:**
- Guidance cut means turnaround timeline extends into FY2028
- China guide of -20% in Q4 undercuts the bottoming narrative
- Converse -35% with no fix in sight is a drag
- JPMorgan downgrade removes a key bull, price target compressed to $52 (near current price)
- Calendar year revenue decline means EPS power is limited near-term

**Net positioning**: The stock is now a deep value turnaround play, not a growth recovery story. At ~$45, shares are at 11-year lows and down 29% YTD (66% over five years). You're buying significant adversity with a 3%+ dividend yield as cushion. The investment horizon for a positive return has extended to 2+ years minimum. The Hill turnaround is real in product and wholesale, but the macro (tariffs, FX, China) is working against it. The breadth of downgrades (JPM, BofA both moved to Neutral) suggests Wall Street is resetting expectations materially lower.

### Follow-ups

1. Track the Q4 report (expected late June 2026) -- does the revenue guide of -2% to -4% hold, or worsen?
2. Monitor China sell-through data and competitor commentary (Anta, Adidas) for market share signals
3. Watch for additional analyst downgrades/PT cuts this week (April 1-4)
4. Track inventory levels at wholesale partners -- are they building or lean?
5. Monitor tariff policy developments -- any escalation is directly margin-negative
6. World Cup 2026 preparations -- brand heat catalyst in North America starting late 2026
7. Converse strategic review -- does Nike explore a divestiture or restructuring?
