# Nike, Inc. (NKE) -- Post-Earnings Note
**Reported:** March 31, 2026 (After Close) | **Quarter:** Q3 FY2026 (Dec–Feb) | **Day 8 Post-Earnings**

---

## The Setup

Nike entered Q3 FY2026 earnings at ~$53, already trading near 11-year lows, down roughly 60% over five years and ~20% YTD. The stock was at 2017 valuation levels. CEO Elliott Hill, 16 months into his "Win Now" turnaround, had delivered a surprise Q2 EPS beat ($0.53 vs $0.37 est), and North American wholesale had grown 24% in Q2, with running up 20%+. But China remained the open wound — contracting 17% in Q2 for the sixth straight quarter. Sentiment was cautious: the options market priced a ~9% move. Consensus EPS ranged from $0.28 (LSEG/CNBC) to $0.31 (Bloomberg), with revenue expected at ~$11.24B.

Short interest as of March 13, 2026 sat at 42.72 million shares (~2.91% of float), up sharply from 27.9M in December 2024 as the stock's persistent underperformance attracted bears. The setup was classic turnaround bifurcation: a beat-and-raise would validate the Hill era; a miss — or worse, a guidance cut — could push shares to decade lows.

## The Story of the Quarter

This was a quarter that revealed real but uneven turnaround progress, undercut by a guidance cut that reset Wall Street expectations materially lower.

**What went right:**

North America was the standout. Revenue of $5.03B (+3%) included wholesale growing 11% — driven by new distribution and lapping prior marketplace management actions. For the first time in two years, Nike drove positive growth across all channels (direct + wholesale) in North America simultaneously. Running grew 20%+ for a consecutive quarter, basketball was up high single digits. The NBA All-Star Weekend in Los Angeles drove full-price sell-through that CFO Matt Friend cited as a template for World Cup execution. Gross margin of 40.2% beat expectations despite 300bps of tariff impact in North America specifically (North America gross margin declined 360bps despite absorbing nearly 650bps of gross tariff headwinds — meaning underlying margins improved ~290bps ex-tariff).

**What was messy:**

Nike Direct declined 7% on a reported basis, with Nike Digital down 9%. The digital business remains too promotional; markdowns across the marketplace were still elevated at quarter-end. Sportswear — the Nike brand's heritage lifestyle category — declined "low double digits" and is a material drag, representing less than half the portfolio but weighing on reported growth. EMEA presented structural headaches: revenue down 7% currency-neutral, with Nike Digital declining 6%, inventory up double digits versus prior year, and sell-through tracking below expectations. Management flagged elevated inventory in EMEA entering Q4 due to softness in sportswear and marketplace promotional behavior.

**Greater China — the most nuanced read:**

Greater China revenue declined 10% on a currency-neutral basis (the note's prior -7% figure reflected reported basis). Nike Digital fell 21%, while wholesale declined 13%. The company is intentionally reducing sell-in to clean up the digital channel and remove aged inventory. Unit inventory declined more than 20% YoY, and partner inventory also fell double digits — suggesting the cleanup is progressing. Running grew double digits and Nike expanded its Nike store pilot to 100 doors including a House of Innovation in Shanghai, seeing sequential improvement in comp sales. But the structural headwinds are real: management noted that China marketplace management actions will continue throughout FY2027, with profitability expected to recover before revenues. New leadership is in place for Greater China.

**The $230M restructuring charge:**

During the pandemic, Nike over-invested in supply chain and technology to support a larger digital and direct business. As the model has shifted back toward a more balanced integrated marketplace, the fixed cost base became a drag on EBIT margins. The charge covers: (1) supply chain — lower costs, streamlined operations, reduced distribution capacity; (2) technology — workforce optimization, program rationalization, and leveraging new advanced capabilities; (3) Converse right-sizing. Management expects to continue evaluating additional supply chain actions but believes Q3 represented the largest financial impact. Benefits begin in FY2027.

**The guidance bombshell:**

Despite the Q3 beat, CFO Friend guided Q4 revenue down 2–4% (consensus had ~+2%). For the rest of calendar year 2026, Nike sees sales declining in the low single digits, with gains in North America offset by declines in Greater China and Converse. Greater China is guided down ~20% in Q4 — a significant re-acceleration of decline from Q3's -10% c/n — driven by accelerated marketplace cleanup actions. Q4 gross margin is guided down 25–75bps with 250bps of tariff impact. Q4 SG&A guided flat to slightly down. Management explicitly deferred full-year FY2026 and FY2027 guidance to an Investor Day in fall 2026.

One important strategic announcement: Hill confirmed a **Nike Investor Day at the Philip H. Knight Campus in Beaverton this fall**. This will be the first formal long-term financial framework under the Hill turnaround, and it is a significant catalyst to monitor.

## Why the Stock Reacted

NKE opened April 1 at $46.44, gapped down from the prior close of $52.83, traded as low as $44.56 intraday, and closed at $44.62 — a -15.5% decline on 8x normal volume (~114M shares). It then slipped further to $44.19 on April 2 (-0.99%). The stock hit a new 52-week intraday low of $43.17 on April 2.

The reaction was driven by a specific set of factors:

1. **Guidance cut, not the beat.** Q4 revenue -2% to -4% vs. +2% consensus was the decisive blow. The calendar year outlook of "low single-digit decline" was not in any sell-side model. This extended the turnaround timeline materially — beyond FY2026 and into FY2027.

2. **China -20% in Q4.** While Q3 China improved sequentially (less bad than Q2's -17%), the Q4 guide of -20% completely erased the bottoming narrative. Management was explicit: China marketplace cleanup continues throughout FY2027, and revenue will lag the profitability recovery.

3. **Converse -35% with no fix in sight.** At $264M, Converse is in freefall. Management's commentary on Converse was procedural rather than strategic — acknowledging it's "beloved" but offering no specific recovery timeline or plan.

4. **Wave of institutional downgrades.** Goldman Sachs (April 2) downgraded from Buy to Neutral, cutting PT from $76 to $52. JPMorgan (April 1) downgraded from Overweight to Neutral, cutting from $86 to $52. Bank of America (April 1) downgraded from Buy to Neutral, cutting from $73 to $55. Citigroup maintained Neutral, cutting PT from $65 to $53. Telsey Advisory cut to $55. The breadth of downgrades from the most influential houses removed institutional demand systematically.

5. **Tariff compounding.** North America tariffs created 300bps of gross margin headwind in Q3, growing to 250bps guided in Q4. With no clarity on further escalation and Iranian war driving oil above $100/bbl, input cost concerns compounded the direct tariff impact.

6. **EMEA inventory build.** Management guiding elevated EMEA inventory entering Q4 is an early warning sign for another promotional cycle in Europe — the region Nike most needs to clean up.

## Guidance and Tone

**Q4 FY2026 specific:**
- Revenue: Down 2–4% (vs +2% consensus)
- Greater China: Down ~20%
- FX: ~2-point tailwind
- Gross margin: Down 25–75bps; includes 250bps tariff impact
- SG&A: Flat to down slightly
- Other expense (net of interest income): $15M–$25M expense

**Calendar Year 2026 framework:**
- Revenue: Down low single digits overall
- North America: Growth
- Greater China: Ongoing declines driven by intentional sell-in reductions
- Tariffs: Material headwind through Q1 FY2027 (August–October 2026)
- Gross margin expansion: Begins Q2 FY2027 (November 2026+)
- Earnings: "Flattish" through calendar year 2026 as margins begin to inflect
- Full framework: Investor Day, fall 2026

**Management tone:** Hill was measured and realistic. His key phrase was that the turnaround "will not happen all at once" — a frank admission that the breadth of Nike's portfolio means some areas (North America, running) are ahead of others (China, Sportswear, Converse). The Investor Day announcement was the most forward-looking commitment, signaling management believes the organization is far enough into the work to make long-term projections. Friend's commentary on the Iran war and rising oil prices introducing "unplanned volatility" to consumer behavior and input costs was notable — a direct acknowledgment that macro forces are now a material variable.

**Net positioning:** NKE is now a deep-value turnaround with a 2–3 year investment horizon minimum. At $44.19 it prices in near-maximum adversity: declining revenues through FY2026, tariff drag through Q1 FY2027, and a China that remains structurally challenged into FY2028. The dividend provides ~3.7% while you wait, but payout sustainability is a genuine question. The stock is technically oversold (RSI ~20 per multiple analyses), which creates a bounce risk for Monday April 7 — but the fundamental trajectory requires patience. The Investor Day is the next genuine re-rating catalyst.
