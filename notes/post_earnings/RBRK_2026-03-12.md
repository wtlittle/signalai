**Stock Reaction:** Despite the massive Q4 beat, RBRK stock fell 6.08% on earnings day to $54.02. Selling has intensified further — shares closed at $49.28 on Mar 20, down 9% from the earnings-day close and 52% below the 52-week high of $103. YTD decline now exceeds -35%. - Subscription net retention rate: >120%
- Customers >$100K subscription ARR: up 25% YoY
- Subscription ARR contribution margin: improved 950+ bps YoY

- FY2026 free cash flow: $238M (10x+ vs prior year)
- Cash: ~$1.4B in cash, equivalents, and marketable securities
- Convertible debt: $1.1B

## Thesis Impact

**Bull case reinforced:**
- Revenue growth of 46% with a path to 27-28% in FY2027 -- still hyper-growth
- FCF inflection from negligible to $238M in one year is remarkable
- Net new ARR acceleration ($115M record) shows demand is strengthening
- Data security TAM expanding with AI governance, ransomware, and compliance drivers
- >120% NRR means existing customers are spending more
- Stock at ~$49 trades at a major discount to consensus PT of $90-105 — risk/reward increasingly compelling

**Concerns:**
- Stock has fallen 48% from 52-week high despite massive beat — severe multiple compression
- FY2027 guide implies ARR growth deceleration from 34% to 25-26%
- Revenue growth estimates for FY27/FY28 revised to 22%/21% — declining trajectory
- Q1 seasonal downturn in revenue (Q4 to Q1) may create near-term overhang
- Go-to-market leadership transition carries execution risk
- $1.1B convertible debt creates dilution risk
- Operating margin adjusted to -1.3% loss, down from previously expected gain

**Net assessment:** Rubrik delivered an outstanding Q4 on every metric. However, the post-earnings selloff has deepened to $49 (now -9% from earnings day), reflecting a market pricing in meaningful deceleration and demanding margin proof. The widening gap between stock price ($49) and consensus PT ($90-105) suggests either a significant re-rating opportunity or that street estimates need to come down further. At ~8x forward P/S, the risk/reward is increasingly attractive for investors with a 12-18 month horizon who believe the 25%+ ARR growth guide is conservative. The severity of the selloff despite a massive beat points to broad sector de-rating rather than company-specific issues.
