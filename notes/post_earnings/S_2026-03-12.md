- Q4 gross margin: 78%

## Thesis Impact

**Bull case strengthened:**
- $1B revenue milestone + first full-year profitability = inflection point
- 600bps operating margin improvement shows discipline and scale
- Record net new ARR of $64M suggests demand acceleration
- Options market activity (massive call buying) signals institutional re-rating potential
- Zero debt, $770M cash, active buyback = strong balance sheet
- FCF margin swing from -3% to +11% is the most important metric improvement
- Zacks #2 Buy with positive EPS revision trend — quantitative signal aligns with fundamental story

**Concerns:**
- Q1 FY2027 revenue guide implies 21% growth — deceleration from 22% in FY2026
- Non-GAAP EPS guide of $0.00-$0.02 for Q1 is thin
- Accumulated deficit exceeds $1.9B — long path to cumulative profitability
- Still trading at a significant discount to CrowdStrike; market needs several quarters of profitability to re-rate
- Competitive pressure from CRWD, PANW, FTNT in platform security
- Dual-class share structure limits broader shareholder influence

**Net assessment (updated Mar 22):** At $14.17 (Mar 20 close), SentinelOne trades at ~26% discount to the $18.64-$19.43 consensus PT range. The post-earnings analyst reaction was mixed but skewed negative on near-term PTs, with UBS, Scotiabank, Stephens, and others all cutting targets. The bull thesis remains intact (FCF positive, $1B milestone, 10% FY2027 operating margin guide), but the street is waiting to see Q1 FY2027 net new ARR data before re-rating. Institutional ownership near 90.9% provides a support floor. The Moderate Buy consensus with 40%+ holding a Sell/Hold view reflects a "prove it" posture heading into Q1.
