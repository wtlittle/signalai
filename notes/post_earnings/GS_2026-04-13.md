## The Story of the Quarter

The quarter was defined by two engines firing simultaneously. First, the Global Banking & Markets segment delivered record revenues of $12.74B, powered by a 48% surge in investment banking fees to $2.84B. Advisory was the standout within IB, jumping 89% to $1.49B, reflecting a wave of M&A completions. Equity underwriting and debt capital markets also contributed.

Second, the equities desk posted a record $5.33B in revenue (+27% YoY), driven by prime brokerage lending to hedge funds and robust cash equities volumes during the March volatility. This was a new all-time quarterly high for GS equities.

FICC was the relative weak spot at $4.01B (down 10% YoY), with softness in rates products, mortgages, and credit. However, FICC was up 29% sequentially, reflecting normal Q1 seasonality.

Asset & Wealth Management contributed $4.08B in revenue (+10% YoY, -14% QoQ), with the sequential decline reflecting the typical post-Q4 drop in incentive fees.
