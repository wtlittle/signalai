# Micron Technology (MU) -- Q2 FY2026 Post-Earnings Note
## Reported: March 18, 2026 (After Close)

---

## The Setup
Micron entered Q2 FY2026 riding an unprecedented AI memory demand cycle. The prior quarter (Q1 FY2026) had already delivered $13.64B in revenue with $4.78 EPS -- both strong beats. The market was expecting continued acceleration, with consensus at $8.60 EPS on ~$21B revenue. MU stock had rallied ~48% YTD ahead of the print. The key questions: how much further can HBM/AI demand push the revenue run rate, and how sustainable are the elevated gross margins?

Revenue of $23.86B was up 196% YoY and 75% sequentially -- a blowout across every business unit. EPS of $12.20 crushed consensus by over 40%. Gross margins expanded to 74.9% (up 18pp sequentially), driven by higher pricing and favorable HBM mix.

## The Story of the Quarter
This was the quarter where memory became a strategic asset, not a commodity input. Three dynamics converged:

1. HBM demand explosion: DRAM revenue hit a record $18.8B (+207% YoY), with HBM products sold out through 2026 and order books stretching into 2027. Micron signed its first-ever 5-year Strategic Customer Agreement (SCA), signaling the shift from short-term transactional relationships to long-term partnerships with hyperscalers.

2. NAND recovery: NAND revenue reached $5.0B (+169% YoY, +82% QoQ), with ASPs increasing in the high-70s percentage range sequentially -- an extraordinary repricing driven by data center SSD demand.

3. Universal tightness: Management described supply as fulfilling only 50-67% of customer demand across the portfolio. This is not a single-product bottleneck -- it is broad-based scarcity across DRAM, NAND, and HBM.

By business unit:
- Cloud Memory: $7.7B (hyperscaler AI training/inference)
- Core Data Center: $5.7B (enterprise AI)
- Mobile & Client: $7.7B (on-device AI expanding memory per device)
- Automotive & Embedded: $2.7B (growing but smaller)

The most striking metric: Q2 revenue of $23.9B exceeded Micron's entire FY2024 annual revenue.

## Why the Stock Reacted
Despite the record-shattering results, MU shares fell ~5% in after-hours and continued sliding ~4% in the days following. Two factors:

1. CapEx sticker shock: Micron guided FY2026 capex above $25B, a massive step-up that spooked investors worried about the classic memory cycle -- overinvestment leading to oversupply. The capital intensity raised questions about FCF sustainability.

2. Sell-the-news / profit-taking: With the stock up 48% YTD, institutional and retail investors took profits into the strong print. This pattern has repeated -- MU dropped 8% on Q1 FY2026 earnings day as well, despite beating estimates. Reddit sentiment on r/wallstreetbets collapsed from the 80s to the low teens within 24 hours of the print.

The disconnect between fundamentals and price action is a recurring MU phenomenon. Procyon Advisors increased its MU stake by ~393% on March 23, suggesting institutional money views the dip as disconnected from the underlying business trajectory.

## Guidance and Tone
Q3 FY2026 guidance was spectacular:
- Revenue: $33.5B +/- $750M (another massive sequential step-up)
- Gross margin: ~81% (further expansion from 75%)
- Non-GAAP EPS: $19.15 +/- $0.40

Management tone was exceptionally bullish. CEO Sanjay Mehrotra described memory as a "strategic asset" for customers and emphasized that supply-demand tightness will persist "beyond calendar 2026." The 30% dividend increase and first-ever 5-year SCA both signal management confidence in durability, not a cyclical peak.

Key forward-looking commentary:
- HBM4 adoption ramping alongside HBM3E
- Data center SSD demand accelerating
- AI content per server continuing to expand
- Supply constraints expected to persist through at least CY2027

## Thesis Impact
For a fundamental buy-side analyst with a 1-3 year horizon, this quarter strengthens the structural bull case materially:

BULL: Memory is re-rating from cyclical commodity to strategic AI infrastructure. The 5-year SCA is a paradigm shift in customer relationships. 81% guided gross margin is semiconductor-industry-leading territory, comparable to NVDA. If supply remains tight into 2027 as guided, the earnings power is significantly higher than what's priced in -- Q3 alone guides to ~$19 EPS, implying annualized run-rate EPS above $60.

BEAR: The $25B+ capex is real money and history shows memory companies chronically overinvest at cycle peaks. The stock's consistent sell-the-news pattern suggests the market is pricing in significant execution risk. Geopolitical risk (Middle East tensions, China trade dynamics) adds tail risk. At some point, AI capex moderation would hit MU disproportionately.

NET: The quarter confirms the structural demand thesis. The sell-off is a positioning event, not a fundamental one. The key monitoring point is whether Q3 FY2026 actually delivers on the 81% gross margin guide -- if it does, re-rating has much further to run.
