## The Setup

STZ entered Q4 with low expectations in its seasonally weakest quarter (Feb-ending). The street expected comparable EPS of $1.68-1.74 on revenue of $1.84-1.90B. The key narrative was whether beer momentum (particularly Modelo Especial) could offset volume softness, aluminum tariff headwinds, and the continued unwind of the Wine & Spirits portfolio post-divestitures. This was also Nicholas Fink's debut earnings as CEO, succeeding Bill Newlands. The macro regime at report time was Slowdown with WTI at $92.74 (having crashed from $115 in the Iran ceasefire unwind), adding a potentially positive tailwind via lower transportation/input costs and improved consumer sentiment from falling gas prices.

## The Story of the Quarter

The beer business remains the franchise. Modelo Especial held its position as the #1 dollar-share beer brand in the U.S., though depletions dipped just under -1% for the brand itself. The growth engines were Pacifico (+21%), Victoria (+17%), and Modelo Chelada (+5%) -- these three brands more than offset the Modelo Especial and Corona Extra (-6%) softness. Beer pricing was favorable and offset an unfavorable mix, delivering net sales growth of over 1%.

The margin compression story was anticipated but real: beer operating margin fell 340bps to 33.2%, driven by aluminum tariffs, unfavorable fixed cost absorption, and higher depreciation from the Veracruz brewery expansion. These are largely transitory headwinds (tariffs could change, Veracruz will achieve scale), but they weigh on near-term earnings power.

Wine & Spirits was a controlled demolition. Net sales fell 58% YoY on a 72.9% shipment decline, reflecting the SVEDKA and 2025 Wine Divestitures. The remaining portfolio (led by Kim Crawford, Meiomi, The Prisoner) actually outpaced the total wine category in Circana tracked channels, delivering 8%+ depletion growth in Q4. But the segment operating margin collapsed to 1.3% from 21.7% -- the remaining portfolio lacks the scale to absorb fixed costs. Management guided W&S operating margin to 5-6% for FY2027, suggesting some recovery but not a return to historical levels.

Cash generation was strong: $2.7B operating cash flow and $1.8B free cash flow for FY2026, exceeding expectations. Over $1.6B was returned to shareholders including $900M+ in buybacks. The board raised the quarterly dividend 1% to $1.03/share.

## Why the Stock Reacted

STZ fell ~0.8-2.2% in the sessions following the report, a muted reaction that reflects several crosscurrents:

1. **Beat was discounted.** STZ has a pattern of beating estimates with muted stock reactions. The market is looking past quarterly results to the forward outlook.

2. **FY2027 guidance was below consensus.** Comparable EPS guided $11.20-11.90 vs. consensus ~$12.36. Organic net sales guided -1% to +1% (flat). Beer margin guided 37-38%, a step down from prior expectations. This was the primary drag.

3. **FY2028 outlook withdrawn.** Management pulled its previously issued FY2028 guidance, citing a "dynamic operating environment" and limited visibility. This is never a good signal and introduces uncertainty about medium-term earnings trajectory.

4. **Marketing spend increase.** STZ plans to boost marketing to 9.5% of sales in FY2027 (from lower levels), an aggressive investment to maintain momentum. Good long-term but dilutes near-term margins.

5. **Macro offset.** The simultaneous WTI crash and Iran ceasefire progress created a generally positive macro backdrop that likely cushioned what could have been a larger selloff on the guidance miss.

**Withdrew FY2028 outlook.** Management cited dynamic operating conditions and limited near-term visibility. This is notable because STZ had previously given multi-year guidance, and the pullback suggests management sees real uncertainty around consumer demand durability and macro headwinds.

Tone was cautiously optimistic. Nicholas Fink emphasized "discipline" and "elements we can control." The language around consumer spending being more "intentional" and demand being "subdued" across alcoholic beverages is a warning that the U.S. consumer may be pulling back on discretionary alcohol spending.

## Thesis Impact

**For longs:** The beer franchise is intact but not accelerating. Modelo and Corona remain category leaders, and the growth brands (Pacifico, Victoria) are delivering. FCF generation is strong ($1.8B), buybacks are aggressive, and the balance sheet is manageable at ~3x net leverage. The question is whether flat organic revenue growth and a 37-38% beer margin represent a new normal or a trough that improves as Veracruz costs are absorbed and tariff headwinds fade.

**For shorts/skeptics:** Beer depletions of +0.6% barely positive, with Modelo Especial itself declining slightly. The W&S segment is subscale and margin-dilutive. FY2027 guidance miss of ~5% on EPS is meaningful. The FY2028 withdrawal raises questions about management's confidence in the medium-term path. Consumer "intentionality" around alcohol spending is a risk in a Transition/Slowdown macro.

**Net thesis impact:** Neutral to slightly negative. The beat was encouraging but the guidance miss and FY2028 withdrawal tilt sentiment cautious. The stock is likely range-bound ($140-160) until there is evidence of beer volume re-acceleration or margin inflection.
