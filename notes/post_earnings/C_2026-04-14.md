## The Story of the Quarter

Citi's transformation is gaining real traction. All five interconnected business segments posted revenue growth. Markets revenue surged ~19%, with equities up nearly 40% and fixed income up 13%. Banking fees rose 15%. Services -- the steady-state cash management and securities services business -- grew 17%, providing the predictable revenue base that the restructuring is designed to build around.

Wealth management and U.S. Personal Banking added further balance. The RoTCE improvement to 13.1% is a meaningful step toward management's medium-term target. Expenses remain elevated as transformation costs persist, but operating leverage is now clearly positive.

## Why the Stock Reacted

The stock reacted positively to the beat, reflecting genuine surprise at the magnitude of earnings improvement. Citi was the "cheapest" major bank heading into the quarter, trading below book value, and the results validated the turnaround thesis. The 42% net income growth and $1B+ revenue beat were hard to ignore.

Fraser highlighted "broad-based momentum" across all five segments. No material changes to FY guidance. The tone was the most optimistic Citi has struck in years.
