# MKC (McCormick) -- Post-Earnings Notes
## Reported: March 31, 2026 (Before Open, 8:00 AM ET Call)
## Quarter: Q1 FY2026 (Dec-Feb)

---

### The Setup

McCormick entered the print at ~$53, down 21% since its last report and near multi-year lows. The stock had been under pressure from a slight Q4 FY2025 EPS miss ($0.86 vs $0.87 est), slower organic growth, and insider selling (~115K shares, $7.95M). Market cap was ~$14.3B, P/E ~18.2x. The consensus was cautiously positioned: $0.59-$0.63 EPS, $1.79B revenue. The key question was whether the McCormick de Mexico acquisition could accelerate top-line growth and whether volume trends would recover. In a Deflation Risk macro regime, McCormick's defensive Consumer Staples profile offered appeal, but the stock needed to deliver to justify its valuation.

### Headline Results vs. Expectations

| Metric | Consensus | Actual | Surprise |
|--------|-----------|--------|----------|
| Revenue | $1.78-1.79B | $1.87B | +5.1% beat |
| EPS (Non-GAAP) | $0.59-0.61 | $0.66 | +10.9% beat |
| GAAP Net Income | -- | $1.02B | -- |
| GAAP EPS | -- | $3.77 | Large one-time gain likely |
| Operating Margin | ~14% prior year | 12.1% | Down ~190bps YoY |
| Revenue Growth (YoY) | +11.3% est | +16.7% | Stronger than expected |

A clean double-beat on both revenue and EPS. The $1.02B GAAP net income ($3.77/share) includes a significant non-recurring gain (likely related to the McCormick de Mexico consolidation accounting). Adjusted EPS of $0.66 is the operative figure.

### The Story of the Quarter

McCormick delivered a strong Q1 with revenue of $1.87B (+16.7% YoY), meaningfully ahead of the $1.79B consensus. The acceleration from Q4's 2.9% growth to 16.7% was driven primarily by the McCormick de Mexico acquisition, which contributed significant inorganic revenue. Organic growth was present but more modest -- sales volumes were flat YoY (vs. 2.2% growth in Q1 FY2025).

CEO Brendan Foley emphasized the "strength and resilience" of the business, pointing to growth in both Consumer and Flavor Solutions segments supported by the acquisition, organic growth, and disciplined cost management. The adjusted EPS of $0.66 represented a meaningful beat driven by acquisition accretion and cost discipline, even as operating margins contracted 190bps to 12.1% -- likely reflecting integration costs from McCormick de Mexico and elevated input costs.

Free cash flow margin declined to 1% from 4.9% in the prior year, signaling working capital absorption from the acquisition integration. This is worth monitoring but expected in the early quarters post-deal.

### Why the Stock Reacted

MKC stock rallied ~5.5% to $56.66 in early trading. The reaction was driven by:

1. **Clean revenue beat**: $1.87B vs $1.79B consensus is a solid beat for a consumer staples name, validating the McCormick de Mexico acquisition strategy.
2. **EPS beat with quality**: $0.66 vs $0.59-0.61 consensus. The beat was driven by acquisition accretion and cost discipline, not one-time items (on an adjusted basis).
3. **Relief rally from oversold levels**: MKC was down 21% since its last report and trading at 2020 valuation levels. Any positive surprise from these levels had outsized impact.
4. **Guidance reaffirmation**: Full-year adjusted EPS guidance of $3.05-$3.13 maintained. No cut despite macro headwinds.
5. **Defensive appeal in risk-off environment**: In a Deflation Risk macro regime with VIX ~30, a staples name delivering beats is exactly what defensive-oriented investors seek.

### Guidance and Tone

Management reiterated full-year guidance:
- Adjusted EPS: $3.05-$3.13 (midpoint $3.09)
- Organic sales growth: 1-3%
- Sell-side analysts expect 11.8% revenue growth over next 12 months (acceleration vs. 3.6% 3-year CAGR)

Tone was constructive and confident. Foley's emphasis on "strength and resilience" and "investing for future growth" suggests no near-term concerns about demand or margin trajectory. The lack of a guidance raise despite the Q1 beat may reflect management conservatism (typical for MKC) or recognition that integration costs could weigh in subsequent quarters.

### Thesis Impact

**Modestly strengthens the bull case.** The results validate:
- McCormick de Mexico acquisition is accretive and contributing ahead of expectations
- Pricing power and cost discipline remain intact
- The defensive characteristics work well in the current macro regime
- Stock was genuinely oversold at $53

However, some caution warranted:
- Flat organic volumes suggest underlying demand hasn't inflected yet
- Operating margin contraction of 190bps is meaningful
- FCF margin decline to 1% needs monitoring
- Guidance maintained, not raised -- implies management sees headwinds ahead

For a fundamental buy-side analyst with a 1-3 year horizon, MKC remains a quality defensive holding. The stock has a regime-aligned profile (Consumer Staples favored in Deflation Risk). The risk is that organic growth remains sluggish even as acquisition-driven topline improves optically.
