# Adobe Inc. (ADBE) -- Q1 FY2026 Post-Earnings Note

|--------|--------|----------|--------|

**Stock Reaction:** Essentially flat day-1 (-0.08%); drifted lower to ~$252 by Mar 16 from ~$270 pre-earnings. Market digesting mixed signals between top-line beat and concerns about AI monetization cadence.

---

## Key Operating Metrics

- Record Q1 revenue of $6.40B, up 12% YoY (11% in constant currency)
- Non-GAAP operating margin: 47.4%
- GAAP operating margin: 37.8%
- ARR: ~$26.06B (+10.9% YoY)
- AI-first ARR: 3x YoY growth
- Firefly ARR: >$250M
- Creative freemium MAU: 80M (+50% YoY)
- Total MAU: 850M (+17% YoY)
- Non-GAAP EPS of $6.06 grew 19% YoY

---

## Guidance and Tone

**Q2 FY2026 Guide:** $6.43B-$6.48B revenue (consensus ~$6.46B) -- in-line
**Full-Year FY2026:** Maintained prior guidance ranges
**Tone:** Confident on AI integration across Creative Cloud, Document Cloud, and Experience Cloud. Management emphasized Firefly adoption, AI Assistant in Acrobat, and GenStudio enterprise pipeline. Noted that enterprise deals take longer to close but see strong pipeline.

---

## Thesis Impact

**Bull case reinforced:**
- Revenue growth reacceleration continues (12% YoY vs 10-11% prior quarters)
- Non-GAAP EPS growth of 19% shows operating leverage
- Firefly/AI monetization is scaling -- ARR 3x YoY
- 850M MAU provides massive distribution for AI features

**Concerns:**
- Stock sold off despite beat -- market wants faster AI revenue ramp
- Valuation re-rating requires sustained acceleration above 12%
- Competition from Canva, Figma (now public), and AI-native tools
- GAAP margins compressed by stock-based compensation

**Net assessment:** Solid quarter. The beat on revenue and non-GAAP EPS confirms AI tailwinds are real but the stock's post-earnings drift lower suggests the market needs to see even faster Firefly/GenStudio conversion. Maintain watch -- the next quarter's AI ARR trajectory will be key.
