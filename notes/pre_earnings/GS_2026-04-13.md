# Goldman Sachs Group, Inc. (GS) -- Pre-Earnings Note
**Earnings Date:** April 13, 2026 (BMO) | **Quarter:** Q1 FY2026 | **Days Until Report:** 5

## Set-up

GS closed at $863.04 on Apr 2 (last trading day before the Easter weekend closure). The stock is down approximately **5.6% YTD** from its Jan 2 open of $914.34, with an intra-quarter peak near $975 in mid-January following the blowout Q4 2025 print, followed by a broad market sell-off that pushed GS to a YTD trough of $782 in mid-March before recovering. The 52-week range is **$439.38 – $984.70**, and the stock is trading at 16.8x trailing EPS ($51.35 TTM). Market cap: ~$256B.

**Monday Apr 7 open update:** The Iran ceasefire narrative is the dominant market driver at the Monday open. Pakistan's army chief (Field Marshal Asim Munir) brokered an overnight framework — the so-called "Islamabad Accord" — proposing a 45-day ceasefire between the US and Iran, with Strait of Hormuz reopening built into phase one. The proposal was received by both sides on Apr 5–6 via Pakistan as the sole communication channel, per Reuters/Axios/Al Jazeera. The S&P 500 opened +0.3% on ceasefire hopes. **Brent has pulled back to $108.42** (from a ~$115 peak), and **VIX has declined to 24.88** (from 27.61 on Apr 2). The backdrop is meaningfully less acute than the Apr 2 close but remains elevated — ceasefire talks are fragile (Iran declined to reopen Hormuz under a "temporary" ceasefire and rejected the US's 15-point plan) and macro uncertainty persists ahead of March CPI (due Friday April 11 — the single biggest macro event of the week). NFP came in at 178K with unemployment at 4.3%, a strong labor market reading that is broadly positive for bank credit quality.

**Macro update (Apr 7):** Regime shifted from Deflation Risk to **Slowdown** (Growth -0.50, Inflation -0.06 Neutral, Policy -0.56 Restrictive, Sentiment -0.75 Risk-Off). VIX 25.58, S&P 500 6,611.83, WTI $115.34 (Iran war premium), Gold $4,677.60, 10Y yield 4.337%. Slowdown regime favors Healthcare (ELV, TMO, UNH), Consumer Staples (MKC, KDP) — banks and financials face increased caution. Iran ceasefire talks via Pakistan ongoing. March CPI due Friday April 11 — key data point for all bank earnings reads.

The stock enters the print approximately 12% off its 52-week high. GS itself has been constructive on the macro setup: on Apr 2, GS strategists issued a bullish April note flagging that the recent market sell-off has "cleared the decks" and improving the risk/reward for equities, with a 12% S&P 500 earnings growth forecast for Q1 2026. Separately, GS raised its 12-month recession probability to **30%** (from 20%) earlier in Q1 — an acknowledgment that persistent high energy prices create growth-shock risk even as the base case remains no recession. This environment is **structurally favorable for Goldman's trading franchise** — elevated rate volatility, geopolitical hedging demand, and commodity dislocations all amplify FICC flow volumes. However, the deal-making and IPO pipeline that Goldman expected to materialize in 2026 remained under pressure through Q1.

Q4 2025 provides the launch pad: GS reported $13.45B in net revenues (slight miss vs. $14.5B consensus) but blew past EPS at $14.01 vs. $11.77 estimate (+$2.24 beat), with strong equity trading and debt underwriting. Management guided optimistically on IB fee recovery and M&A pipeline going into 2026, citing a nascent "M&A super-cycle." AWM management fees hit a record $3.09B in Q4, and AUM rose to $3.61T. GS raised its AWM pre-tax margin target to 30% medium-term (from mid-20s). The Q1 deal-freeze from tariff escalation and Iran conflict represents the key narrative risk to the Q1 print.

**Sector context:** Markets closed Apr 3–5 (Good Friday + weekend); Monday Apr 7 is the first session. GS prints first among the six major banks on Apr 13, setting the tone for the week's earnings. The ceasefire-driven relief open means the worst of the Liberation Day tariff/Iran gap-down fear has partially been priced.

## Key Debates and Variants

- **FICC trading windfall vs. consensus:** Elevated rate volatility (10Y UST range of ~75bps in Q1, oil price surge, EM FX dislocations) is the single largest swing factor. GS's FICC desk historically over-earns in high-vol macro environments. Buy-side is debating whether Q1 FICC comes in closer to $4.5B (blue sky, ~30% above Q4's depressed level) or $3.5B (consensus). The Iran war oil spike in late Feb–March was particularly impactful for commodities desks. JPM's COO guided to "low double digit" growth in trading revenues in mid-February; GS management has been quiet but consensus implies a substantial Q1 step-up. With Brent now off its $115 peak but still at $108, the benefit to commodities desks was concentrated in the Feb–Mar window — fully captured in Q1.

- **IB fee recovery: how much of the pipeline pulled through vs. got shelved?** Goldman's Q4 guidance flagged strong M&A/ECM pipeline conversion into Q1. January deals were robust, but February–March froze as tariff escalation and Iran war disrupted CEO confidence. GS itself noted on Apr 2 that the sell-off may have "cleared the decks." Advisory fees consensus: ~$1.1–1.2B, range $0.9B–$1.5B. ECM was likely softer than hoped given the March equity correction; DCM/leveraged finance should have held up better.

- **Equity trading: can GS sustain the Q4 momentum?** Q4 equity trading was exceptionally strong at $4.31B (record per Reuters). Q1 2026 saw heavy client rotation out of US tech/growth into value and international (DeepSeek shock in January, tariff/Iran repricing through Feb–March). This is a high client-activity environment. Consensus expects equity revenues of ~$3.8–4.0B. The risk is whether prime brokerage clients faced drawdowns that reduced flow revenues in March.

- **Asset & Wealth Management (AWM) fee growth:** AWM management fees hit a record $3.09B in Q4 2025. GS raised its medium-term AWM pre-tax margin target to 30%. Q1 equity market weakness may weigh slightly on fee accruals, but private credit and alternatives fundraising remain resilient. AUM was $3.61T at Q4-end; any meaningful compression from equity market weakness is a modest headwind.

- **GS macro research vs. its own trading results:** The market will note the tension between GS's April constructive macro call (12% S&P earnings growth, "sell-off cleared the decks") and its own elevated recession probability (30%). GS's trading franchise is best positioned when this kind of uncertainty creates high client activity — both directions of that tension are commercially favorable.

- **Credit quality and loan losses:** GS's consumer exit (Marcus wind-down) has significantly de-risked the credit book. Strong NFP (178K) and 4.3% unemployment reduce near-term corporate credit risk in GS's private credit portfolio. Provision for credit losses consensus: ~$320M, relatively benign.

## What Matters This Print

- **EPS:** ~$15.92–$16.14 consensus (MarketBeat: $15.92; Barchart: $16.14; range: $14.50–$18.00). Prior quarter Q1 2025 actual: $14.12. The ~14% YoY bar reflects FICC trading assumptions and IB fee recovery. **Note: consensus has drifted slightly lower vs. the $17.42 figure from the prior note — the tariff/Iran deal freeze has caused some downward estimate revisions.** FY2026 EPS consensus: $57.70 (+12.4% vs. $51.32 in FY2025).
- **Net Revenues:** ~$16.66B consensus (MarketBeat $16.66B). Q4 2025 actual was $13.45B; Q1 2025 actual was $15.06B.
- **FICC Revenues:** Consensus ~$3.8–4.0B. Q4 2025 was $2.85B (depressed by year-end quiet); Q4 2025 FICC was up ~12% YoY per Reuters. A print above $4.2B would be solidly positive; above $4.5B would be a beat.
- **Equities Revenues:** Consensus ~$3.8–4.0B. Q4 2025 record: ~$4.31B. The client rotation and volatility in Q1 suggest the bar is achievable, with upside possible.
- **Investment Banking Revenues (Advisory + Underwriting):** Consensus ~$2.2–2.4B. IB fees rose 25% YoY in Q4 2025. Q1 disruption from deal freeze tempers expectations but the January window was active.
- **AWM Revenues & Management Fees:** Watch for management fee run-rate; record $3.09B in Q4 2025. Any step-down from equity market AUM compression is a modest negative.
- **Provision for Credit Losses:** Consensus ~$320M. Strong labor market (NFP 178K, UE 4.3%) reduces near-term tail risk here.
- **CET1 Ratio:** Expected ~13.5–14.0% (Q4 2025 was ~14.6%). Watch for any capital return update; GS has been aggressively buying back shares.
- **ROTCE:** Full year 2026 consensus implies ~17–18% ROTCE. Q1 print should be tracked against this run-rate.
- **Macro commentary:** CEO David Solomon's tone on the IB pipeline, M&A super-cycle narrative, and response to the ceasefire/tariff backdrop will shape the stock's reaction at least as much as the raw numbers.
