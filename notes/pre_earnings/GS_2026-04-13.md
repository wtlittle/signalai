# Goldman Sachs Group, Inc. (GS) -- Pre-Earnings Note
**Earnings Date:** April 13, 2026 (BMO) | **Quarter:** Q1 FY2026 | **Days Until Report:** 8

---

## Set-up

GS closed at $863.04 on Apr 2 (last trading day before the Easter weekend closure). The stock is down approximately **5.6% YTD** from its Jan 2 open of $914.34, with an intra-quarter peak near $975 in mid-January following the blowout Q4 2025 print, followed by a broad market sell-off that pushed GS to a YTD trough of $782 in mid-March before recovering. The 52-week range is **$439.38 – $984.70**, and the stock is trading at 16.8x trailing EPS ($51.35 TTM). Market cap: ~$256B.

The stock enters the print approximately 12% off its 52-week high — a moderate discount that reflects broader market anxiety rather than GS-specific concerns. The macro backdrop is highly relevant: the Iran-US military conflict (escalating since February-March 2026) has driven Brent crude to ~$115/bbl, VIX to 27.61 as of Apr 2, and gold to $4,910. This environment is **structurally favorable for Goldman's trading franchise** — elevated rate volatility, geopolitical hedging demand, and commodity dislocations all amplify FICC flow volumes. However, the flip side is that the deal-making and IPO pipeline that Goldman expected to materialize in 2026 is under pressure: tariff uncertainty and geopolitical risk are keeping corporate boardrooms on pause.

Q4 2025 provides the launch pad: GS reported $13.45B in net revenues (a slight miss vs. $14.5B consensus) but blew past EPS at $14.01 vs. $11.77 estimate (+$2.24 beat), with strong equity trading and debt underwriting. Management guided optimistically on IB fee recovery and M&A pipeline going into 2026, citing a nascent "M&A super-cycle." That narrative has come under siege from tariff-induced deal freezes in Q1.

**Sector context:** Markets closed Apr 3–5 (Good Friday + weekend); Monday Apr 7 is the first session for price discovery. Expect GS to gap sharply on Apr 7 with the broader financial sector, as tariff-driven recession fears that materialized on Apr 2 (Liberation Day tariff escalation) will be priced in cold. Banks likely face a -5% to -10% sector open on Apr 7 before the Apr 13 GS print.

## Key Debates and Variants

- **FICC trading windfall vs. consensus:** Elevated rate volatility (10Y UST range of ~75bps in Q1, oil price surge, EM FX dislocations) is the single largest swing factor. GS's FICC desk historically over-earns in high-vol macro environments. Buy-side is debating whether Q1 FICC comes in closer to $4.5B (blue sky, ~30% above Q4's depressed level) or $3.5B (consensus). The Iran war oil spike in late Feb–March was particularly impactful for commodities desks. JPM's COO guided to "low double digit" growth in trading revenues in mid-February; GS management has been quiet but consensus implies a substantial Q1 step-up.

- **IB fee recovery: how much of the pipeline pulled through vs. got shelved?** Goldman's Q4 guidance flagged strong M&A/ECM pipeline conversion into Q1. However, the tariff escalation beginning in late January and geopolitical uncertainty have frozen significant deal flow. The debate is whether GS captured the pre-tariff window (January deals were robust) or whether the March slowdown dominates. Advisory fees consensus is ~$1.1-1.2B, but the range is wide ($0.9B–$1.5B). ECM was likely softer than hoped given the March equity market correction.

- **Equity trading: can GS sustain the Q4 momentum?** Q4 equity trading was exceptionally strong at $3.5B. Q1 2026 was an unusual quarter — heavy volatility, massive rotation out of US tech/growth into value and international, driven by AI/DeepSeek shock in January and then tariff/Iran repricing through February-March. This is a high client-activity environment. The question is whether GS's prime brokerage and cash equities desks monetized the rotation or were on the wrong side of client deleveraging. Consensus expects equity revenues of ~$3.8-4.0B.

- **Asset & Wealth Management (AWM) fee growth:** GS has been building AWM toward $10B+ in management fees. Equity market weakness in Q1 (GS estimates AUM likely compressed somewhat vs. all-time highs) may weigh on fee accruals, but private credit and alternatives fundraising should be resilient. The buy-side watches whether management fees trajectory supports the longer-term $3B+ operating income AWM target.

- **Credit quality and loan losses:** GS's consumer exit (Marcus wind-down) has significantly de-risked the credit book. However, the deflation risk macro regime and Iran-driven stagflation threat raise questions about corporate credit quality in GS's private credit portfolio and mid-market lending books. Watch provision for credit losses — consensus expects ~$320M, relatively benign.

## What Matters This Print

- **EPS:** $17.42 consensus (range: $15.60–$19.00). Prior quarter Q1 2025 actual: $14.12. The +23% YoY bar reflects FICC trading assumptions and IB fee recovery. Given the Iran/tariff macro setup, there is meaningful upside risk if FICC outperforms.
- **Net Revenues:** $16.77B consensus. Q4 2025 actual was $13.45B; Q1 2025 actual was $15.06B. The quarter-over-quarter step-up is large and depends heavily on trading.
- **FICC Revenues:** Consensus ~$3.8-4.0B. Q4 2025 was $2.85B (depressed by year-end quiet). Q1 2025 was ~$4.3B. A print above $4.2B would be solidly positive; above $4.5B would be a beat that drives outperformance.
- **Equities Revenues:** Consensus ~$3.8-4.0B. Q4 2025 run-rate implied ~$3.5B. The client rotation and volatility in Q1 suggest upside potential.
- **Investment Banking Revenues (Advisory + Underwriting):** Consensus ~$2.2-2.4B. The debate is whether advisory holds $1.1B+ or falls short given deal freeze. ECM and DCM should be reasonable given January/February activity window.
- **AWM Revenues & Management Fees:** Watch for management fee run-rate; consensus ~$2.0B for the segment, with operating leverage the key metric for the multi-year AWM story.
- **Provision for Credit Losses:** Consensus ~$320M. Any significant reserve build against private credit or consumer residuals would be a negative surprise.
- **CET1 Ratio:** Expected ~13.5-14.0% (Q4 2025 was ~14.6%). Watch for any capital return update; GS has been aggressively buying back shares.
- **ROTCE:** Full year 2026 consensus implies ~17-18% ROTCE. Q1 print should be tracked against this run-rate.
