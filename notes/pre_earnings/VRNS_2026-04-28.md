## Set-up

VRNS prints Q1 FY2026 tonight as one of the most contested names in security. The $63 → $32 single-day gap on 2025-10-28 (-48.7%) wiped out roughly $3.8B of market cap when management telegraphed weaker conversions and a slashed Q4/FY ARR setup, and the stock has compounded that pain — down -20.8% YTD 2026 and -55% from October highs into a $20.19 low on 2026-04-10 — before a +19.9% 30-day bounce into print. This is the first quarter under the new SaaS-only KPI framework: management explicitly retired total-ARR guidance in favor of "SaaS ARR ex-conversions" (guided 27–28% Q1 2026, 18–20% FY) plus a wide-banded conversions range to capture the residual ~$105M non-SaaS ARR base that is being end-of-life'd by year-end. Sentiment going in is bifurcated — short interest sits at 7.39% of float (8.29M shares as of 2026-04-15, +21% MoM), a securities class action is active over the October disclosure, while sell-side stays a 13/2/0 bullish/neutral/bearish split with avg PT $40.67 (high $68 JP Morgan, low $28 Barclays cut from $38 on 2026-04-13). The setup is binary: the print either validates that Q3 2025 was a one-time GTM execution stumble masked by an acceleration of the EOL plan, or it confirms that conversion economics + non-SaaS attrition are structurally worse than the new framework lets the Street price.

## Key Debates & Variant Perception

- **Net new organic SaaS ARR — the only number that matters this quarter.** Guide implies $120M of net-new ex-conversion SaaS ARR for FY26 vs. $109.5M actual in 2025 — i.e., management is asking the Street to underwrite a +9.6% acceleration in organic SaaS ARR adds despite 2025's GTM disruption. Bulls (Piper, Cantor, JPM legacy) argue the Q3 2025 air-pocket was conversion-driven and the underlying SaaS demand signal is intact (32% YoY SaaS ARR ex-conv in Q4 2025); bears (Barclays cut to $28) think the 27–28% Q1 SaaS ex-conv guide is the high water mark and decelerates from here as the easy comp lapses.
- **Conversion economics — uplift or just retention?** Management explicitly said in Q4 it is "focusing less on uplift" on the remaining ~$105M non-SaaS book and assumed "no uplift" in the conversion bridge. That is a tacit admission the remaining single-threaded on-prem cohort cannot absorb a SaaS premium — the question is whether the $50–75M of expected 2026 conversions actually closes at flat ARR or slips into churn. The $30–50M FCF/contribution-margin headwind in FY26 guide is the cost of buying retention.
- **Renewal rate stratification.** Blended renewal stayed >90% in 2025, but management flagged non-SaaS renewals "slightly below historical." Going forward they will only disclose SaaS renewal rate, removing the most uncomfortable disclosure precisely when the on-prem cohort is being squeezed. The Street has no clean way to monitor leakage.
- **AllTrue.ai/Altru integration as platform vs. distraction.** The ~$150M February acquisition extends the platform from data security into AI agent governance/TRiSM — strategically logical given the AI security narrative, but it dilutes a story that was already complicated by transition optics and adds 2026 OpEx into a year where break-even op income is the guide.
- **FCF durability question.** $131.9M of FCF in 2025 (vs. $108.5M) was a bull anchor; 2026 guide is $100–105M, a deliberate $30–50M reset. Bulls call this the "kitchen sink" trough that re-rates in 2027; bears note that FCF *was* the only clean positive in the model and now even that is in retreat for a year.

## What Matters This Print

- **SaaS ARR ex-conversions absolute dollars and growth rate.** Q1 guide: 27–28% YoY ex-conv. Anything <26% raises real questions about the 18–20% FY guide. Anything ≥29% with maintained FY puts the bull case back in play.
- **Conversion ARR amount + commentary on the residual $105M.** Q4 2025 converted $65M (1/3 of remaining non-SaaS) — pacing the expected $50–75M FY26 conversions matters. A heavy front-loaded Q1 conversion (>$25M) implies the EOL motion is working; a low Q1 with no commentary on visibility suggests conversions slip right.
- **Total revenue vs. company guide $164–$166M (-7% sequential from Q4 $173.4M).** Guide is essentially conceded already; the question is whether revenue in-lines or beats the upper end.
- **FY26 guidance maintained vs. raised vs. lowered.** Company guide: revenue $722–$730M (+16–17%), SaaS ARR $805–$840M (+26–32%), SaaS ARR ex-conv +18–20%, FCF $100–$105M, op income breakeven–$4M, EPS **$0.06 – $0.10**. Maintaining all five is the bull bar; touching FCF or SaaS ARR ex-conv low end is the bear trigger.
- **Renewal rate (now SaaS-only) commentary.** Whether the lawsuit/disclosure overhang materially impaired Q1 renewal motion is observable here.
- **AllTrue.ai/Altru contribution + bookings cadence.** First quarter of integration — any explicit dollar contribution or pipeline color sets the bar for 2H acceleration narrative.

**Guidance Watch:** Q1 FY2026 company guide: total revenue **$164M – $166M** (+20–22% YoY), SaaS ARR ex-conv growth **27–28%**, non-GAAP operating loss -$11M to -$10M, EPS **-$0.06 – -$0.05** vs. consensus **-$0.02**. FY26 EPS guide **$0.06 – $0.10** vs. consensus **$0.21**. The fact that Street consensus EPS sits 2.5x company guide on the year is the single biggest reason this print is a wide-band event — either Street capitulates to company numbers (multiple compression continues) or company beats and raises (Street comes in).

**Options-Implied Move:** ATM straddle (May 15 monthly, 17 DTE, $25 strike) mid = **$4.60** on $25.41 spot ⇒ **±18.1% implied move**. Skew is meaningfully negative: $20 OTM put IV 119.73% vs. $30 OTM call IV 102.64% (~17vol pts), confirming the market is paying up disproportionately for downside protection. Last 8 quarters' realized abs move: **mean 12.6% / median 9.1%** (range +14.6% to -48.7%, distorted by Q3 2025 -48.7% gap; ex-Q3 2025 mean 7.4%, median 6.4%). **Implied is ~rich** to median historical realized but justified given (a) the live securities-class-action / disclosure overhang, (b) the binary FY EPS reset (Street $0.21 vs. company $0.06–$0.10), and (c) a thin float event-day liquidity profile. **PM takeaway: long premium is expensive but defensible; selling the straddle into print is asymmetric vs. tail risk if conversions disappoint.
