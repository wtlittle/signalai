## 1. Set-Up

**Current price:** $94.78 | **52-week range:** $84.59–$108.29 | **Market cap:** ~$18.5B

**YTD performance:** ZBH has declined from ~$89.92 at year-end 2025 and is approximately flat-to-down for the year. The stock traded as high as $98.44 in late February but has sold off amid medtech sector de-rating, tariff anxiety, and softer-than-expected orthopedic procedure volume data. ZBH is trading near its 52-week low, approximately 12% below the 52-week high of $108.29.

**Valuation:** ZBH trades at a forward P/E of approximately 11.3x on FY2026 consensus EPS of $8.39. EV/EBITDA on a trailing basis is ~11.0x. This represents a significant discount to the medtech peer group (Stryker trades at 20–22x forward earnings, Globus Medical at 18–20x). The discount is attributable to: (1) ongoing knee market share concerns, (2) elevated leverage (Net Debt/EBITDA ~2.9x), (3) tariff uncertainty, and (4) a slower robotics adoption cycle than initially expected. FCF margin was ~14.2% in FY2025. Analyst sentiment is cautious — consensus rating is **Hold** (17 of 27 analysts at Hold, 3 at Strong Sell, only 6 at Strong Buy). Average price target: $98.83, implying just 4% upside from current levels.

**Key context:** Q4 2025 was modestly reassuring — adjusted EPS of $2.42 (beat of $0.04), revenue $2.24B (beat of ~$20M). The knee segment grew only 2.1% in Q4, below peers. Q2 2025 saw -15% single-day stock decline after Q3 2025 missed revenue by ~$10M and ZBH disclosed U.S. knee market share loss. The company received FDA clearance for ROSA Knee with OptimiZe in November 2025, an enhanced version offering personalized surgical planning — but commercial launch is still in targeted/early rollout phase ([Zimmer Biomet Press Release](https://investor.zimmerbiomet.com/news-and-events/news/2025/11-14-2025-123022448)). ZBH also completed the ~$1.1B acquisition of Paragon 28 (foot & ankle) in Q1 2025, which is now contributing to growth but pressuring margins.

**Tariff backdrop:** ZBH disclosed in May 2025 that tariffs would impact operating profit by $60–80M for FY2025, later revised down to ~$40M as U.S.-China trade tensions partially de-escalated ([MedTech Dive](https://www.medtechdive.com/news/zimmer-biomet-earnings-tariff-impact/747229/)). For FY2026, the tariff impact is more uncertain — China tariffs have re-escalated in 2026, but ZBH's U.S.-manufacturing base provides relative insulation vs. peers (Stryker called out $175–200M tariff impact). **Tariff quantification for 2026 guidance will be a key question on the call.**

## 2. Key Debates and Variants

**Debate 1: U.S. knee market share trajectory**
Zimmer lost meaningful knee share in Q1 and Q3 2025 — the Q2 2025 recovery (3% growth) and Q4 2025 stability suggest stabilization, but analysts remain unconvinced this is a durable turn. [BTIG noted](https://www.marketbeat.com/stocks/NYSE/ZBH/earnings/) that ZBH has "lost market share in its U.S. knees business." With Stryker's Mako robotic system continuing to gain attach rate and Smith & Nephew investing in digital surgery, the structural competitive dynamic is unfavorable. Bulls argue the ROSA Knee with OptimiZe launch (targeted release in late 2025, commercial in 2026) is the inflection catalyst that restores attach-rate competitiveness. Bears argue the robotic cycle is structurally behind.

**Debate 2: ROSA robotics conversion and ASC penetration**
The bull case for ZBH is a multi-year ROSA robot placement cycle driving premium implant pull-through and insulating margin. The question is pace — how many incremental ROSA placements are occurring per quarter, and what is the utilization rate driving implant pull-through? Management has been opaque on robot unit placements. Additionally, the shift to ambulatory surgery centers (ASCs) is a structural tailwind for orthopedics (less insurance friction, lower cost settings) — ZBH's ASC workflow capabilities showcased at AAOS 2026 in February are positive, but the revenue impact is gradual.

**Debate 3: Tariff risk and 2026 EPS guidance revision**
FY2026 consensus EPS is $8.39 — this seems to assume a manageable tariff impact similar to 2025's $40M. However, the re-escalation of China tariffs in April 2026 introduces downside risk. J.P. Morgan noted the tariff impact is "more questions than answers" for full-year magnitude. A guidance cut to the $7.90–8.10 range (similar to 2025's initial cut) would be a ~5% EPS headwind and likely drive meaningful stock weakness given the low-multiple, low-sentiment setup.

**Debate 4: Paragon 28 integration and foot/ankle growth**
Paragon 28 added meaningful revenue to the growth rate but depressed margins due to integration costs and lower-margin product mix. If Paragon is tracking ahead of plan, management may highlight accelerating contribution — a potential positive surprise in total revenue growth.

**Post-earnings move context:** ZBH has historically been one of the more volatile medtech names on earnings, with an average 1-day post-earnings move of ~9.15% over the past 5 quarters. Notably, ZBH fell ~15% after Q3 2025 on the revenue miss and share loss disclosure, and ~11.6% after Q1 2025. However, Q4 2025 and Q2 2025 were both positive reactions (+1.9% and +8%, respectively). Options are pricing ~6.0% for the upcoming print, which appears potentially underpriced given the history.

**Investment view (1–3 year horizon):** ZBH is a classical "self-help + robotics ramp" story that has underdelivered on both dimensions. At 11x forward earnings — a multi-year valuation low — much disappointment is priced in. The ROSA Knee with OptimiZe launch, ASC penetration, and Paragon 28 optionality provide pathways to +20–25% EPS growth over 3 years if execution improves. However, this print is high-risk given the elevated bear probability (tariff uncertainty, knee share, leverage). We would look to add on any tariff-driven sell-off if knee share stabilizes. The UBS Sell thesis ($86 target) centers on structural competitive disadvantage in U.S. knees — watching this print to assess whether that view is gaining validation.
