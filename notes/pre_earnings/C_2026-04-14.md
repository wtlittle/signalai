## Key Debates and Variants

- **Transformation progress — is the organizational restructuring yielding tangible cost savings?** This is the central Citi debate. Jane Fraser's headcount cuts (~20,000 reduction underway) and organizational simplification target the efficiency ratio toward 60% by 2026 from ~65%+. EBIT margin has expanded from 28.2% (FY2023) to 35.3% (FY2025), with consensus projecting 39.8% in FY2026 as transformation costs roll off. Q1 2026 is the first full quarter of the new simplified five-division structure. Any evidence that run-rate expenses are declining sustainably is the most important qualitative signal.

- **NII trajectory — 5–6% growth guide for FY2026:** Citi guided FY2026 NII (ex-Markets) +5–6% YoY. The Services business (TTS + Securities Services) generates sticky, high-quality fee and NII revenues. Higher-for-longer rates are a net positive for Citi's institutional deposit base. The Barclays card portfolio closing (~April) adds consumer revolving balances, providing an additional NII tailwind for Q1/Q2.

- **Markets revenue: mid-teens growth confirmed by Fraser on March 10:** Fraser explicitly confirmed "mid-teens percentage growth" in markets revenue for Q1 2026 at the RBC conference. Citi's FICC franchise (historically #2–3 globally) benefits from rates, FX, and energy commodity volatility — all elevated in Q1. CFO Mason's earlier "mid single digit" guidance from early March was conservative; the Mar 10 Fraser confirmation of "mid-teens" is the more current and specific signal. With Brent at $108 (off $115 peak) and VIX at 24.88, FICC conditions remain elevated even into Q2.

- **Investment Banking: advisory freeze vs. solid DCM:** Fraser flagged client paralysis ("no one is taking bets") on IB advisory, consistent with peers. DCM should hold up given refinancing demand. Citi gained 30bps of IB wallet share in 2025 and participated in 15 of the 25 largest global transactions — a market share gain pace that exceeded JPM and GS. Watch IB fee trajectory for guidance on Q2 pipeline conversion. Fraser's "mid-teens" IB fees statement on Mar 10 implies Q1 IB fees were tracking well despite the advisory freeze.

- **ROTCE path to 11–12% by end-2026:** Q4 2025 ROTCE was ~8.8% (clean basis: ~7.5–8%). EPS growing from $7.53 (FY2025) to an expected $10.16 (FY2026) is a 35% step-up — the highest earnings growth trajectory of the six banks. Q1 2026 ROTCE should track toward 9–10% on a clean basis. The May 7 Investor Day will be the next venue for management to re-guide the 11–12% target. Any language suggesting the 11–12% target could be exceeded would be a meaningful re-rating catalyst.

- **Credit quality in the consumer and institutional books:** The Barclays card portfolio adds consumer exposure at a time when NCOs are normalizing. Strong NFP (178K) and 4.3% UE is a positive backstop. Watch card NCO rate vs. Q4 2025 baseline.

- **Macro-specific Citi dynamics:** The Iran ceasefire, if it holds, is particularly positive for Citi given its large EM and cross-border banking footprint. The Strait of Hormuz situation directly affects Citi's energy sector clients and commodity trading flows. Brent at $108 (off the $115 peak) and VIX at 24.88 still represent an elevated client-activity environment that should be positive for markets revenues.

## What Matters This Print

- **Adjusted EPS:** Consensus ~$2.60 (range $2.44–$2.80; Zacks consensus per prior note). Q1 2025 actual was $1.96. The +32.7% YoY growth bar reflects: (1) lapping Russia exit losses; (2) transformation savings; (3) NII growth; (4) markets revenue step-up. FY2026 EPS consensus: $10.16 (+35% vs. FY2025's $7.53).
- **Net Revenue:** $23.31B consensus. Q1 2025 actual was $21.60B (+8% YoY implied). Note: Citi's Q4 2025 revenues were depressed by the Russia charge; Q1 2026 should be a clean quarter.
- **Net Interest Income (NII):** Implied ~$14.0–14.5B quarterly (FY2026 guide suggests ~$56–58B annual). Watch for any update to the 5–6% FY2026 NII growth guidance.
- **Markets Revenue (FICC + Equities):** Fraser confirmed "mid-teens" growth on Mar 10. FICC consensus ~$4.0–4.5B; equities ~$1.3–1.5B. The "mid-teens" guidance implies FICC ~$4.3–4.6B vs. Q1 2025 ~$4.0B. Any print above $4.5B in FICC would be a beat.
- **Services Revenue (TTS + Securities Services):** Consensus ~$5.0–5.3B. This is Citi's crown jewel — sticky, high-quality fee revenue. Any acceleration vs. expectations is an important re-rating catalyst.
- **Investment Banking Fees:** Fraser confirmed "mid-teens" growth on Mar 10. Consensus ~$0.9–1.1B. Mid-teens growth from Q1 2025 (~$0.9B) implies ~$1.0–1.05B.
- **Provision for Credit Losses:** Consensus ~$2.2–2.5B. Any significant reserve build signals consumer credit stress concern. Strong labor market is a tailwind.
- **CET1 Ratio:** ~13.5–14.0% expected. Over $13B in buybacks completed; Barclays card portfolio will consume some capital but is accretive to earnings.
- **ROTCE (quarterly annualized):** Target 11–12% by year-end; Q1 should show ~9–10% on a clean basis.
- **May 7 Investor Day preview:** Any pre-guidance on what will be revealed at the May 7 Investor Day — particularly if the ROTCE target is revised above 11–12% — is the most important upside catalyst.
