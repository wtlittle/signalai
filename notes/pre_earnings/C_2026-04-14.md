# Citigroup Inc. (C) -- Pre-Earnings Note
**Earnings Date:** April 14, 2026 (BMO) | **Quarter:** Q1 FY2026 | **Days Until Report:** 9

---

## Set-up

C closed at $115.24 on Apr 2, down approximately **3% YTD** from its Jan 2 open of $118.70 — the relative outperformer among large-cap banks YTD. The 52-week range is $55.51–$125.16; the stock hit its 52-week high in late January before retreating to the $105-115 range during the February-March sell-off, and has partially recovered. Current P/E of ~16.5x trailing EPS ($6.99 TTM). Market cap: ~$202B.

Citigroup is in the middle of its multi-year transformation under CEO Jane Fraser — exiting 13 international consumer markets, streamlining from five segments to five simplified divisions, and targeting a ~$51-53B expense run-rate by 2026 alongside an 11-12% ROTCE target by year-end. The Q4 2025 print (Jan 14, 2026) was messy: adjusted EPS of $1.81 beat the $1.65 estimate, but reported revenue missed on the Russia exit charge ($1B after-tax loss), sending the stock down 3.3%. Stripping out the Russia charge, underlying operations showed strong 11% NII growth in 2025 and continued progress on Citi's Services (Treasury & Trade Solutions, Securities Services) franchise.

The macro setup — Iran war, Brent ~$115, rate volatility elevated, VIX ~28, gold $4,910 — plays differently for Citi vs. peers. Citi's global footprint means it has significant exposure to EM central bank flows, cross-border payments, and FX volatility — all highly active in Q1. Citi's CFO Mark Mason guided in early March that Q1 markets revenues were on track to rise "mid single digits," with "good momentum in FICC, particularly in rates and FX, as well as in equities driven by derivatives and prime balances." That comment pre-dates the early April Liberation Day tariff shock; the actual result may well be stronger given the late-quarter volatility surge. On the IB/advisory side, CEO Fraser warned in Q1 commentary that "most clients are pausing their plans. No one is taking bets in the market right now" — a cautious signal for advisory fees.

Citi reports on the same day as JPM and WFC (Apr 14), creating a bank sector triple-header. Markets will be interpreting all three simultaneously.

## Key Debates and Variants

- **Transformation progress — is the organizational restructuring yielding tangible cost savings?** This is the central Citi debate. Jane Fraser's headcount cuts (~20,000 reduction underway) and organizational simplification are supposed to drive the expense efficiency ratio toward 60% by 2026 from ~65%+. Buy-side watches every quarter for evidence that expenses are coming down sustainably, not just through headcount, but in run-rate operational costs. FY2025 expenses came in above some targets; Q1 2026 is the first full quarter of the new simplified structure.

- **NII trajectory — 5-6% growth guide for FY2026:** Citi guided FY2026 NII +5-6% YoY after 11% growth in 2025. The Services business (TTS + Securities Services) generates high-quality, sticky fee and NII revenues that are somewhat rate-insensitive. The bigger question is deposit repricing in a higher-for-longer environment (positive for Citi, given their institutional deposit base) vs. loan demand softening. Higher oil prices and Iran-related energy sector activity could boost Citi's commodities and energy banking revenues, given their historical energy sector exposure.

- **Markets revenue: can Citi's FICC franchise monetize Iran/rate vol?** Citi is historically the #2 or #3 FICC franchise globally (after JPM). Q2 2025 FICC was $4.27B (+20% YoY). Q1 2026 should benefit from rates, FX, and energy commodity volatility — all of which are in hyperdrive given the Iran war and tariff backdrop. Mason's "mid single digit" growth guidance was likely conservative given the Apr 2 escalation of market volatility (the last two weeks of Q1 would have seen significantly elevated client activity as traders repositioned).

- **Investment Banking: advisory freeze vs. solid DCM:** Citi's IB franchise is mid-tier vs. Goldman/JPM but has been gaining share. Advisory fees were disrupted by the corporate deal freeze in Q1; Fraser flagged client paralysis explicitly. DCM should hold up better as issuers refinanced ahead of potential tariff-driven credit spread widening. Watch Citi's IB fee trajectory for guidance on how Q2 might develop if/when deals unfreeze.

- **ROTCE path to 11-12% by end-2026:** Q4 2025 ROTCE was ~7.5-8% (depressed by Russia charge). The market wants to see a credible path to double-digit ROTCE. Q1 2026 ROTCE should track toward 9-10% on a clean basis. Any guidance that the 11-12% target is achievable or at risk is the most important qualitative signal from management.

- **Credit quality in the consumer and institutional books:** Citi has meaningful consumer credit exposure in the US (cards, personal loans). Consumer stress among lower-income borrowers has been rising; the tariff/stagflation environment could accelerate delinquency normalization. Watch net charge-off rate on the card business vs. the Q4 2025 baseline.

## What Matters This Print

- **Adjusted EPS:** $2.60 consensus (range: $2.44–$2.80). Q1 2025 actual was $1.96. The +32.7% YoY growth bar reflects: (1) lapping Russia exit losses; (2) transformation savings flowing through; (3) NII growth; (4) markets revenue step-up. This is a high-stakes beat/miss quarter given the transformation narrative.
- **Net Revenue:** $23.31B consensus. Q1 2025 actual was $21.60B (+8% YoY implied). Note: Citi's Q4 2025 revenues were depressed by the Russia charge; Q1 2026 should be a clean quarter.
- **Net Interest Income (NII):** Implied ~$14.0-14.5B quarterly (FY2026 guide suggests ~$56-58B annual). Watch for any update to the 5-6% FY2026 NII growth guidance — higher-for-longer rates are a net positive.
- **Markets Revenue (FICC + Equities):** FICC consensus ~$4.0-4.5B; equities ~$1.3-1.5B. Mason's "mid single digit" guide implies ~$4.1-4.3B in FICC (Q1 2025 actual ~$4.0B). The Iran/rate volatility tail risk is to the upside.
- **Services Revenue (TTS + Securities Services):** Consensus ~$5.0-5.3B. This is Citi's crown jewel — sticky, high-quality fee revenue. Any acceleration vs. expectations is an important re-rating catalyst.
- **Investment Banking Fees:** Consensus ~$0.9-1.1B. Watch for advisory fee guidance and any commentary on pipeline thaw.
- **Provision for Credit Losses:** Consensus ~$2.2-2.5B. Any significant reserve build signals consumer credit stress concern.
- **CET1 Ratio:** ~13.5-14.0% expected. Citi is in capital build mode; share buybacks have been modest relative to peers.
- **ROTCE (quarterly annualized):** Target 11-12% by year-end; Q1 should show ~9-10% on a clean basis.
