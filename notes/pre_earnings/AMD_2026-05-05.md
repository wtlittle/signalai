## Key Debates and Variant Perception

1. **Data Center revenue magnitude — the single most important number.** Consensus is **$5.55B** (vs. $3.67B Q1 2025; +51.5% YoY) per Zacks/StreetAccount. The bull case is that the MI300/MI325X transition into **MI355X production volume** is driving an inflection — Susquehanna's $17B FY26 GPU revenue model implies ~$4.0–4.5B of GPU revenue alone in Q1 (the rest EPYC server CPUs). The bear case is that **MI355X is only fully ramped late Q2**, and Q1 will reflect transition-quarter softness as customers pause pre-MI355X (similar to the Q3 2025 air pocket). Anything **below $5.3B** would be read as negative; **above $5.8B** would validate the >60% YoY trajectory Lisa Su outlined at the November 2025 Analyst Day.

2. **Gross margin trajectory — Mix-driven inflection or moderation.** Consensus gross margin is **55.1%** (vs. ~55% guide and 54.0% Q4 2025). Bulls argue MI355X's higher ASPs and improving yield drive sequential improvement to 55–56%; bears note the ramp of new product carries the typical 100–200bps drag, plus the MI355X has a higher BOM (HBM3e at scale-up cost) that compresses near-term margin. **Anything sustainably above 56% would be a meaningful thesis catalyst**, suggesting MI400-series (HBM4, TSMC 2nm) has structural margin upside in 2027.

3. **Q2 revenue guide — Consensus is $10.52B; the variant question is whether AMD guides above $10.75B.** Q2 is the seasonal step-up quarter and the first quarter that should reflect MI355X at scale. Susquehanna's whisper number is ~$10.8–11.0B; sell-side AI-focused analysts (Wells, Cantor) are at $10.9B. A guide **at $10.5–10.6B** would be read as conservative-disappointing and likely triggers a 5–8% selloff. A guide **above $10.9B** would extend the rally with $400+ targets becoming consensus.

4. **MI400/MI455X / Helios rack commentary — the strategic variable.** AMD's MI400 series moves to **TSMC 2nm** (first GPUs on 2nm) anchored by the **OpenAI 6GW deal**. The Helios rack will house **72 MI455X GPUs with 31TB HBM4 and 2.9 ExaFLOPS FP4**. The Q1 call will be parsed for: (a) tape-out and validation timing (must be on track for H2 launch), (b) any incremental large-customer commitments beyond OpenAI/Meta, (c) HBM4 supply commitments, and (d) any commentary on **Helios rack pricing/economics** vs. NVDA's Vera Rubin. Lisa Su has previously projected "tens of billions" in AI GPU revenue by 2027 and >60% annual data-center growth; reaffirmation is needed.

5. **Software/ROCm stack — the durable bear argument.** SemiAnalysis's December 2024 study found MI300X achieves **<30% of theoretical FLOPS in real training workloads** vs. Nvidia's ~40% MFU, with H100 outperforming MI300X by **10–25% in multi-node training**. AMD has invested heavily in ROCm 7+ in 2025–2026 — but software maturity remains the primary barrier to share gain in training (vs. inference, where MI355X has a clearer TCO advantage). The Q1 call will be watched for any new partnership announcements (PyTorch, vLLM, Anthropic, Cohere) that signal incremental developer-mindshare wins.

6. **Embedded and Gaming — the small-but-dilutive segments.** Embedded ($855M consensus, +3.9% YoY) is grinding; Gaming ($658M consensus, +4.2%) remains supported by next-gen console socket wins. These segments are not the thesis but a Gaming **upside surprise** (>$700M) would help offset any data-center caution and provide cushion. Embedded weakness (<$830M) would suggest broader industrial/auto demand softness.
