## The Set-up

Baker Hughes enters Q1 2026 as one of the most direct beneficiaries of the Iran conflict and the resulting energy price surge. WTI crude spiked from ~$74 in late February to $115+ in early April before crashing back to ~$92-98 on ceasefire progress -- but Q1 average realized prices were significantly higher than Q4 2025. The oilfield services sector broadly benefits from elevated energy prices as E&P operators accelerate drilling programs and increase completions activity. BKR's Industrial & Energy Technology (IET) segment, which includes LNG equipment and gas technology services, is a structural growth engine as global LNG capacity expansion continues. The company also has a pending acquisition of Chart Industries, positioning it as a larger diversified energy technology platform.

**Macro context (Transition regime):** The regime shift from Slowdown to Transition reflects improving risk appetite. WTI at $97.56 remains elevated (though off the $115 peak), which supports upstream spending. The weak DXY is a tailwind for international operations. VIX at 19.23 suggests normalized risk pricing.

## Key Debates and Variants

1. **Iran conflict revenue upside:** Did elevated commodity prices in Q1 drive incremental activity that flows through BKR's order book? International drilling activity in the Middle East (ex-Iran), offshore, and deepwater are the key vectors.

2. **IET margins:** The Industrial & Energy Technology segment has been expanding margins on the back of LNG equipment orders and service contract renewals. Can IET margins sustain 20%+ as the mix shifts toward higher-margin services?

3. **Chart Industries acquisition integration:** The proposed acquisition would add industrial gas, cryogenic equipment, and hydrogen capabilities. Watch for timing updates, regulatory approvals, and any deal-related charges.

4. **Oil price sensitivity:** Q1 captured a broad range -- from ~$74 to $115+. BKR has less direct commodity exposure than E&P companies, but activity levels and customer sentiment are highly correlated with oil prices. The late-quarter WTI crash could moderate Q2 outlook commentary.

5. **International vs. North America mix:** International revenue has been the growth engine, offsetting North America softness. Any shift in this dynamic matters for margin trajectory.
