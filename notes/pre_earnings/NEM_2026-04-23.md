At current spot, Newmont's implied margin per ounce — against guided 2026 AISC of $1,680/oz — exceeds $1,600+/oz, a figure without precedent in the company's history.

## 2. Key Debates and Variants

**Debate 1: Production trough vs. gold price tailwind**
Newmont guided 2026 attributable production at ~5.3Moz, down from 5.9Moz in 2025, driven by deliberate pit resequencing at Cadia, Tanami Expansion 2, and other Tier-1 assets. The bears argue this is a multi-quarter production trough that crimps operating leverage at exactly the wrong time. The bulls counter that at $3,300+ gold, the absolute dollar margin per ounce is so wide that volume matters less than in prior cycles — $1,600+/oz margin on 5.3Moz still generates well north of $8B in annual EBITDA. **The key question for Q1:** Did weather/operational issues further reduce Q1 production below the ~1.3–1.4Moz implied run rate?

**Debate 2: AISC and cost control credibility**
The 2026 AISC guidance of $1,680/oz shocked some analysts who had modeled closer to $1,780/oz; post-Q4 the market re-rated favorably on this. However, Q1 is typically the seasonally weakest quarter for AISC (fewest ounces produced, fixed costs absorbed over less volume). The variance between actual Q1 AISC and the $1,680/oz guidance trajectory will be closely scrutinized. [Wedbush noted](https://investor.wedbush.com/wedbush/article/marketminute-2026-3-24-mining-giants-under-pressure-newmont-and-barrick-gold-hit-by-production-troughs-and-rising-costs) that labor shortages and equipment inflation could pressure AISC above guidance; bulls argue the $100/oz efficiency program underscores management's confidence.

**Debate 3: FCF yield and capital return framework**
With $7.3B in annual FCF and a net cash position, the debate is whether Newmont will accelerate buybacks beyond the $1B floor. Management confirmed that all FCF above $1B will go to buybacks — but the magnitude and pace will hinge on Q1 cash generation. At gold near $3,300/oz and full-year FCF guidance likely to be revised upward, forward FCF yield at current prices is approaching 8–10%.

**Debate 4: Copper optionality mispricing**
Newmont's copper production (Cadia, Red Chris) is projected to grow above 135,000 tonnes in 2026. With copper pricing elevated, the copper credit meaningfully offsets AISC. Most gold investor models underweight this. The copper story could be a re-rating catalyst if disclosed more granularly.
