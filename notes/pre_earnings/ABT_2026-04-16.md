## Key Debates and Variants

1. **FreeStyle Libre trajectory and CGM competitive dynamics**: FreeStyle Libre drove 16% YoY growth to $1.83B in Q1 2025, but Dexcom's G7 Ultra and new entrants are intensifying competition in the CGM space. The critical debate: can Libre sustain double-digit growth internationally (particularly in Europe) while fending off market share erosion in the US? Libre 3+ launch trajectory in 2026 is the key product catalyst.

2. **Diagnostics normalization -- how much drag remains?**: Q4 2025 diagnostics revenue dropped to ~$2.05B, missing expectations of $2.19B. Post-COVID testing demand continues to normalize. The buy-side wants to see: (a) core laboratory and molecular diagnostics stabilizing, (b) rapid test business comping easier YoY in Q1 2026, and (c) any pullthrough from new point-of-care platforms. Bears argue another sequential miss here could cascade into a second downgrade wave.

3. **Medical devices segment breadth beyond Libre**: EP (electrophysiology/Volt PFA catheter), structural heart (Navitor TAVI), neuromodulation, and vascular divisions collectively represent meaningful revenue. The Q4 2025 weakness was partly offset by these sub-segments. Can EP and structural heart accelerate in Q1 to provide incremental upside even if diagnostics disappoints again?

4. **Tariff exposure and supply chain**: Abbott has significant international manufacturing -- Mexico, EU, and Asia. With US tariff escalation ongoing, the buy-side is scrutinizing gross margin guidance for any read-through. Medical device companies are somewhat insulated by USMCA carveouts, but raw materials and component sourcing remain an exposure. Brent at $108 and WTI at $110 (down from the war peak but still elevated) add logistics and freight cost headwinds.

4b. **Exact Sciences acquisition impact (new as of Mar 23)**: Abbott closed its $21B acquisition of Exact Sciences on March 23, 2026. The deal is expected to add ~$3B of incremental sales in 2026 and accelerate 2026 sales growth by ~0.5%. However, it is EPS-dilutive by ~$0.20 in 2026 due to integration expenses and debt interest. Q1 2026 results will be the first to reflect any contribution, though the close date (Mar 23) means the impact will be minimal -- only ~one week of consolidated revenue. Management's commentary on integration progress and the Cologuard salesforce crossover into Abbott's primary care network will be closely watched. BTIG lowered PT to $131 from $140 on Mar 31 to incorporate Exact Sciences model updates (maintains Buy).

5. **Full-year 2026 guidance validation vs. cut risk**: At Q4 2025, management reiterated FY2025 guidance (organic sales growth 7.5-8.5%, adj. EPS $5.05-$5.25 for FY2025) and provided forward color. For FY2026, the street projects EPS of $5.68 (+10.3% YoY from $5.15 in FY2025) and revenue of ~$49.1B. Any cut to full-year guidance -- even modest -- would validate the bear case and likely push shares toward $95-98. Reaffirmation is the base case; upward revision drives the bull case.

## What Matters This Print

1. **EPS (Adj. Diluted)**: Consensus $1.14-$1.15 (finance_earnings_history shows $1.14 estimate; Barchart shows $1.15). Compares to $1.09 in Q1 2025 (+5.5% YoY). ABT has matched or beaten EPS for four straight quarters (beat by $0.02 in Q1 2025, in-line in Q2-Q4 2025). Options market implies ~4.9% move. The bar is low enough that an in-line print may disappoint on its own.

2. **Revenue**: Consensus ~$11.00B (finance estimates: $10.997B for Q1 2026). Q1 2025 actuals were $10.36B, so this implies +6.2% YoY organic growth. Abbott missed revenue in Q4 2025 by $341M -- the buy-side needs a clean top-line print to restore confidence.

3. **FreeStyle Libre revenue**: The single most important line item. Q1 2025 = $1.83B (+16% YoY). Street likely embedding ~$2.05-$2.10B for Q1 2026 (+12-15% YoY). Any inflection above $2.15B is bullish; below $1.95B triggers re-rating concerns about CGM competitive dynamics.

4. **Diagnostics revenue**: Q1 2025 = $2.05B (already weak, down 7.2% YoY). Consensus for Q1 2026 likely near $2.0-2.1B. This is the "prove it" segment -- needs to show stabilization, not further decline.

5. **FY2026 Guidance (adj. EPS)**: Consensus $5.68 for FY2026. Management provided Q2 guidance of $1.23-$1.27 adj. EPS at Q4 2025 results; the street will want a full-year reaffirmation and ideally a raise to rebuild credibility after the Q4 revenue miss.
