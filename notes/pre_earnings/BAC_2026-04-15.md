## Set-up

BAC closed at $49.38 on Apr 2, down approximately **11.7% YTD** from its Jan 2 close of $55.95. The 52-week range is $33.07–$57.55; the stock made a 52-week high in late December 2025/early January 2026 before a broad deterioration driven by macro anxiety. P/E of ~13.0x trailing EPS ($3.81 TTM). Market cap: ~$354B — the largest bank by market cap after JPM.

**Monday Apr 7 open update:** Pakistan's army chief brokered an overnight framework — the "Islamabad Accord" — proposing a 45-day ceasefire between the US and Iran, with Strait of Hormuz reopening in phase one (per Axios/Reuters/Al Jazeera, Apr 5–6). **Brent has pulled back to $108.42** (from ~$115), and **VIX is 24.88** (from 27.61 on Apr 2). S&P 500 opened +0.3% on ceasefire hopes. Ceasefire talks remain fragile — Iran has declined to reopen Hormuz under a "temporary" deal — but the directional shift is a meaningful positive for BAC's consumer and corporate sentiment narrative. **March CPI is due Friday April 11** — key macro event of the week. **NFP 178K / unemployment 4.3%** — strong labor market, the most direct positive input for BAC's consumer credit quality. BAC is the most "consumer banking + trading" hybrid — strong employment data directly backstops its card credit book.

BAC's Q4 2025 was broadly solid: revenues of $28.37B beat the $27.76B consensus (+$606M surprise), EPS of $0.98 beat the $0.96 estimate (+2%). But the stock fell 3.8% as investors focused on: (1) IB pipelines ("healthy but waiting for clarity"); (2) modest NII guidance of 5–7% for FY2026; (3) continued drag from legacy AFS portfolio losses. Q1 2025 actual was $0.90 EPS on $27.37B revenues.

BAC reports at 6:45am ET on Apr 15 — the earliest of the six banks, before markets open and before MS. BAC's print will set the very first data point of the Apr 15 bank sector day.

**Material new guidance and analyst activity since prior note:**

- **Management (mid-March update):** BAC management updated guidance suggesting NII will grow by **at least 7% year-over-year** in 2026 — the high end of, and now explicitly upgraded from, the prior 5–7% range. This was stated in context of the Fed's decision to hold rates steady in the face of persistent energy-driven inflation (per FinancialContent/MarketMinute Apr 3 preview). This is a meaningful NII guide upgrade.
- **Co-President Dean Athanasia (Q1 management commentary):** Confirmed that Global Markets experienced an increase in the **low double-digit range** YoY, and that investment banking revenues rose by approximately **"10%, in double digits"** YoY (per Seeking Alpha, Mar 10). This is specific pre-quarter guidance from a named executive — BAC has explicitly confirmed double-digit IB and markets growth before the Q1 print.
- **Jefferies Financial Group:** Moved BAC to **"Strong Buy"**, citing the bank's superior capital position and its ability to capture a larger share of the rebounding IPO market. Jefferies cited high-profile tech debuts (Databricks, Canva) hitting public markets in Q1, boosting BAC's advisory fees with a "double-digit percentage increase."
- **Zacks (Apr 1):** BAC Earnings ESP of **+1.44%** (most recent Most Accurate Estimate above consensus), indicating analysts have recently become bullish. Zacks Rank #3 (Hold). Average beat over prior 4 quarters is consistent and includes the Q3 2025 beat of +12.77%. Another beat is probable.
- **Barchart (Mar 25):** EPS consensus **$0.99/share** (+10% vs. $0.90 Q1 2025 actual). BAC has beaten in each of the last 4 quarters. FY2026 EPS consensus: **$4.32/share** (+13.4% vs. $3.81 in FY2025); FY2027: $4.95/share (+14.6%).
- **NII trajectory:** BAC's Q4 2025 NII was $15.89B (+9% YoY). Management's "at least 7%" FY2026 NII guide implies ~$62–63B for FY2026 (~$15.5B/quarter average with growth into H2), modestly above prior guidance.
- **16th consecutive quarter of YoY trading revenue growth** expected (per FinancialContent), driven by commodities and currencies from the oil/Iran volatility.

## Key Debates and Variants

- **NII trajectory and AOCI recovery:** BAC guided for "at least 7%" NII growth in FY2026 (raised from 5–7% in mid-March). The higher-for-longer rate environment is net positive for BAC as its fixed-rate asset book matures and reinvests at higher yields. BAC's sensitivity table implies ~$1.4B of annual NII benefit per 25bps of rate increase — with rates at 3.5–3.75% and no imminent cuts, this is a structural tailwind. Strong NFP / higher-for-longer (CPI due Apr 11) extends this tailwind. Q1 NII guidance consensus ~$14.5B; anything above $15.0B would be a beat.

- **Global Markets: 16th consecutive YoY trading revenue growth quarter expected:** BAC's Co-President Athanasia explicitly confirmed "low double-digit" Global Markets growth in Q1 on Mar 10. BAC's Q4 2025 NII was strong at $15.89B; Global Markets FICC/equities are tracking to extend the 15-quarter streak. FICC benefited from Iran-war-driven commodity and FX volatility; equities benefited from the rotation out of US tech into value/international. The Seeking Alpha Mar 10 report is the most direct piece of Q1 guidance.

- **IB fees: healthy pipeline, double-digit growth confirmed:** Athanasia's Mar 10 statement of "10%, in double digits" for IB revenue growth is the key datapoint. Jefferies' Strong Buy upgrade explicitly cited double-digit advisory fee growth from Databricks/Canva IPOs and the IPO market rebound. BAC's IB franchise ranks #3–4 globally. Consensus IB fees: ~$1.4–1.6B. Management commentary on Q2 IB pipeline conversion is critical — the ceasefire narrative could accelerate deal thaw.

- **Consumer credit quality and the bifurcated consumer:** CEO Moynihan has highlighted BAC's shift toward "secured lending and prime credit customers." He explicitly flagged that lower-income consumers show "more stress" while affluent consumers remain strong. The NFP print (178K, 4.3% UE) is a direct positive for this debate — a strong labor market directly backstops BAC's consumer card credit quality. Watch net charge-off rates on the card portfolio vs. Q4 2025 baseline; any acceleration above $1.5B in provision would be a bearish signal.

- **Expense discipline — efficiency ratio and ROTCE target:** BAC targets efficiency ratio below 60% and ROTCE of 15–16% for 2026. Q1 compensation accruals (driven by strong trading) could push expenses up, but BAC has been a consistent cost manager. Moynihan's operating leverage goal (revenue growing faster than expenses) is the key metric. FinancialContent noted BAC is delivering "200 basis points of operating leverage" in 2026.

- **Capital return / dividend:** BAC CET1 ratio sits near 11.4% (per FinancialContent Apr 3 review), with Jefferies citing this as a "superior capital cushion." Any update on buyback cadence or dividend policy is a meaningful signal given the stock's YTD underperformance.

- **Ceasefire implications for IB pipeline:** The Iran ceasefire narrative (45-day Islamabad Accord proposed Apr 5–6) is directly relevant to the "deal winter is over" thesis cited by both Jefferies (Strong Buy) and FinancialContent's Apr 3 preview. If a ceasefire holds, Moynihan's "healthy but delayed" IB pipeline could thaw more quickly in Q2.

## What Matters This Print

- **EPS:** $0.99–1.00 consensus (Barchart $0.99, FinancialContent $1.00–1.01; range $0.92–$1.10). Q1 2025 actual was $0.90. The ~10–11% YoY growth bar is achievable with NII step-up and trading strength. Zacks ESP +1.44% signals a beat is likely.
- **Net Revenue:** $29.78B consensus. Q4 2025 was $28.37B; Q1 2025 was $27.37B. YoY growth driven by NII + trading.
- **Net Interest Income (NII):** Consensus ~$14.5–15.0B quarterly. Q4 2025 NII was $15.89B (Q4 NII often elevated from market NII). Management's "at least 7%" FY2026 guide implies the quarterly run-rate builds through the year. **Watch for Q1 NII + any upward revision to the "at least 7%" language**.
- **Global Markets Revenue (FICC + Equities):** FICC consensus ~$3.0–3.5B; Equities ~$1.8–2.0B. Athanasia's "low double-digit" growth guidance (Mar 10) implies combined Global Markets of ~$4.8–5.2B (vs. Q1 2025 Global Markets of $6.58B per RoboForex). Watch for confirmation of the 16th consecutive YoY trading revenue growth quarter.
- **Investment Banking Fees:** Consensus ~$1.4–1.6B. Athanasia's "10%, in double digits" IB growth guidance (Mar 10) is the benchmark. Jefferies' strong buy cited double-digit advisory fee growth from Databricks/Canva IPOs. Q1 2025 was ~$1.5B — double-digit growth implies ~$1.65B+.
- **Provision for Credit Losses:** Consensus ~$1.5–1.8B. Q4 2025 was ~$1.5B. Strong NFP (178K, 4.3% UE) is a backstop; any build above $2B would be a bearish signal on consumer credit.
- **CET1 Ratio:** ~11.4–12.0% expected. Watch for buyback acceleration commentary. Jefferies cited 11.4% CET1 as a "superior capital cushion."
- **ROTCE:** Target 15–16%; Q1 should track at ~14–15% depending on trading. "200bps of operating leverage" is the stated 2026 goal.
- **Merrill Lynch Wealth Management:** Watch for fee revenue and NNA trends. Equity market compression in Q1 is a mild headwind; strong consumer confidence and affluent client resilience are tailwinds.
