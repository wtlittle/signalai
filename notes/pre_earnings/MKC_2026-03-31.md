# MKC (McCormick) -- Pre-Earnings Notes
## Earnings Date: March 31, 2026 (Before Open, 8:00 AM ET)
## Quarter: Q1 FY2026

---

### Set-up

McCormick is a defensive consumer staples name with steady but unspectacular growth. The stock benefits from the current Deflation Risk macro regime (favored sector: XLP Consumer Staples). The company reports Q1 FY2026 on March 31 before the open. McCormick has been executing on pricing power and volume recovery after several quarters of consumer pushback on higher prices. FY2026 EPS guidance range is $3.05-$3.13.

The stock has shown relative resilience in the recent selloff given its defensive profile. As a buy-side holding, the key question is whether volume trends are sustainably positive or if consumers are trading down to private label.

### Key Debates and Variants

1. **Volume vs. price mix**: After years of price-led growth, are volumes finally turning positive? Private label share gains in spices/seasonings remain a concern.
2. **Flavor Solutions segment**: This B2B segment (restaurants, food manufacturers) is the higher-margin growth driver. Is foodservice demand holding up as consumer spending softens?
3. **FY2026 guidance confidence**: Will management reaffirm or adjust the $3.05-$3.13 EPS range? Any FX headwind commentary given dollar strength?
4. **Input cost trajectory**: Spice commodities, packaging, and logistics costs. Are they tailwinds or headwinds this cycle?

### What Matters This Print

1. **EPS**: Consensus $0.61-$0.63, up 1.7% YoY from $0.60. Modest expectations -- any beat likely drives a positive reaction.
2. **Revenue**: Consensus ~$1.79B. Organic growth rate and volume/price decomposition are more important than the headline number.
3. **Volume trends**: Positive organic volume would be a bullish signal that the consumer is not trading down from McCormick brands.
4. **Flavor Solutions growth rate**: This segment's performance signals broader foodservice/QSR health.
5. **FY2026 guidance reaffirmation**: Maintaining or raising the $3.05-$3.13 range would be supportive.

### Scenario Grid

**Bull Case (Stock +4-7%)**
- EPS beat >$0.65
- Positive organic volume growth
- Flavor Solutions accelerating
- FY2026 guidance raised or reaffirmed with confidence
- Input cost tailwinds emerging

**Base Case (Stock -1% to +3%)**
- EPS in-line at $0.61-$0.63
- Flat to slightly positive volumes
- Guidance reaffirmed
- FX headwind commentary but manageable

**Bear Case (Stock -5-8%)**
- EPS miss <$0.58
- Negative volume trends (consumer trade-down)
- Guidance lowered or language softened
- Flavor Solutions weakness (foodservice demand concerns)
- Margin compression from input costs
