# McCormick & Company (MKC) -- Q1 FY2026 Pre-Earnings Note
## Reports: Tuesday, March 31, 2026 (Before Open)
## Days Until: 4

---

## Set-up
McCormick enters Q1 FY2026 amid significant corporate activity. Unilever confirmed talks on March 20 regarding a potential McCormick offer for its food business -- a transformative M&A event if consummated. Meanwhile, JPMorgan cut its price target from $78 to $67 (maintaining Overweight) on March 25, implying ~30% upside from current levels. The stock has been under pressure alongside the broader consumer staples sector.

Key context:
- Unilever M&A talks confirmed March 20 -- potential acquisition of Unilever food business
- JPM PT cut to $67 (from $78), maintains Overweight -- implies structural undervaluation
- Q4 FY2025 showed steady execution in core flavoring business
- Consumer spending environment remains cautious with trade-down pressures
- Consensus Q1 EPS: $0.61 (up 1.7% from $0.60 in prior year)

## Key Debates and Variants

1. Unilever M&A impact: Will management address the confirmed food business acquisition talks? How would this be financed? What does it mean for leverage and margins?

2. Volume vs. pricing: Can McCormick drive volume growth alongside modest pricing? Consumer staples have faced volume pressure industry-wide.

3. Input cost trajectory: Spice and seasoning input costs (pepper, vanilla, garlic) -- are they easing or creating margin headwinds?

4. China/International growth: International has been a steady contributor. Any acceleration or deceleration?

## What Matters This Print

1. EPS: Consensus $0.61. Modest beat expected given MKC's track record (beat 3 of last 4 quarters).
2. Organic revenue growth: Consensus expects low-single-digit growth. Volume contribution vs pricing mix matters.
3. Gross margin: Any expansion from pricing and mix optimization would be positive.
4. Full-year guidance: Management typically reaffirms or adjusts FY guidance on Q1 call. Watch for any change given Unilever talks.
5. M&A commentary: Any detail on the Unilever food business discussions will dominate the call.
