## Key Debates & Variants

### 1. McCormick de Mexico: Accretive or Dilutive to Near-Term EPS Optics?

**Bull:** The $750M acquisition (closed January 2, 2026) brings McCormick's stake in the JV from 50% to 75%, consolidating revenues previously carried as "unconsolidated income." Mexico contributed ~$750-800M in revenues (estimated) on a full-year basis. In Q1 FY2026, this is the first full quarter of consolidated revenues, adding an estimated **$185-200M to the top line** and boosting Consumer segment volume/mix. The JV was also highly profitable — management called it "accretive to net sales, adjusted operating margin, and adjusted EPS" and noted it would contribute 11-13% of the 13-17% FY2026 reported sales growth guide. The Mexico business anchors leadership in mayonnaise (high-velocity, high-loyalty) with a FIFA World Cup 2026 activation pipeline.

**Bear:** The deal adds meaningful gross debt (~$750M financed via commercial paper/available cash). Net debt post-deal is estimated at ~$4.65B vs. $3.9B pre-close, and interest expense headwinds offset a portion of the operating income accretion. Additionally, the **elimination of the 25% minority interest** in Mexico net income attributable to Grupo Herdez reduces reported earnings attribution. Management was explicit on the Q4 call that "year-over-year EPS growth is reduced by the elimination of the 25% minority interest." This mechanical drag is expected to compress EPS leverage in 2026 and 2027 before wrapping in 2028. Consensus still does not appear to fully agree on the net EPS impact of the consolidation.

**The $0.61 vs. $0.806 consensus divergence:** Multiple platforms show adj. EPS consensus at **$0.61** for Q1 (Yahoo Finance, Quiver Quantitative, Finnhub), while one aggregator (Perplexity Finance consensus tool) shows $0.806. The $0.61 figure aligns with the street's view of Q1 adj. EPS growing ~$0.01 above Q1 FY2025's $0.60 — a modest beat-of-comp print. The Q1 FY2025 non-GAAP adj. EPS was $0.64 (consensus at the time was $0.64, actual came in at $0.60, a $0.04 miss). For this preview, we anchor to **$0.61 adj. EPS** as the operative consensus bar.

### 2. Tariff Exposure & Gross Margin Recovery

McCormick guided for an incremental **~$50M gross tariff impact** in FY2026 (Jan. 22 call), flagging mid-single-digit total inflation from tariffs + commodities — similar to 2025. CFO Marcos Gabriel stated he expects to "mitigate the vast majority" through productivity savings (CCI), alternative sourcing, and surgical pricing. However, with ~50% of incremental tariffs on McCormick items still in place post-recent reductions, and consumer sentiment declining (particularly for lower-/middle-income households), the pricing lever is under scrutiny. If tariff escalation accelerates (e.g., a renewed trade flare with China or Latin America), the $50M estimate could prove too low — and gross margin recovery guidance could be at risk.

**Key variable:** Vanilla commodity pricing (meaningful cost component), pepper, and packaging costs have been volatile. The company's "broad basket" approach to commodities means no single input drives the story, but cumulative pressure matters.

### 3. Organic Growth Quality: Volume vs. Price

McCormick delivered **seven consecutive quarters of Consumer volume growth** through Q4 FY2025 — a key management anchor and differentiation point. However, volume growth in Q4 FY2025 decelerated to just +0.2% while pricing stepped up to +1.9%, as September 2025 pricing actions took effect. The market is concerned that volume elasticity will be worse in 2026 as the consumer continues to trade down. FY2026 organic guidance of **1-3%** is modest — implying pricing of ~1-2% and volume/mix of ~0-1%.

The bull case requires Consumer segment volumes to re-accelerate in Q1 as pricing laps begin to moderate. The bear case: price-sensitive consumers reduce frequency on spices/condiments, and volume goes negative for the first time in two years. Any volume decline in Consumer would be a significant negative read-through, particularly given the stock's valuation.

### 4. Flavor Solutions Recovery vs. Structural Softness

Flavor Solutions (historically ~44% of revenue, now ~40% with Mexico Consumer consolidation) has been pressured by softness in large CPG customer volumes and sluggish branded food service foot traffic. Q4 FY2025 adj. operating income was $85.8M at an **11.9% margin** — below the Consumer segment's 20.5%. For FY2025 as a whole, Flavor Solutions grew operating income 9% (11% cc), showing real margin recovery despite top-line headwinds.

Management expects Flavor Solutions performance to **strengthen in 2026** as:
- Latin America inventory reset (Q4 2025 headwind) normalizes
- Flavors customer pipeline has doubled YoY
- QSR volume remains resilient in Americas and Asia Pacific
- Large CPG volumes stabilize

**Debate:** Is the CPG customer softness structural (GLP-1 impact, private label penetration) or cyclical (inventory drawdown + macro)? The answer affects the long-term revenue model meaningfully.

### 5. CAGNY Messaging and Long-Term Algorithm Credibility

At CAGNY 2026 (February 17), management **reaffirmed the long-term algorithm** of 4-6% net sales growth, 7-9% operating income growth, and 8-11% EPS CAGR. Critically, they framed 2026 as a "bridge year" — above-normal cost headwinds (tax rate normalization, interest expense, minority interest elimination) constrain EPS growth to 2-5%, but the underlying business algorithm resurfaces in 2027/2028 as these headwinds wrap. Post-CAGNY, the stock initially rallied, then sold off again — suggesting skepticism about whether the cost bridge is a one-year phenomenon or the new baseline. This print needs to validate the bridge narrative with clean execution.

## What Matters This Print

### 1. Adjusted EPS
**Consensus: $0.61 | Prior Year: $0.60 | Implied Growth: +1.7%**

The bar is extremely low — essentially flat growth. A $0.01-$0.02 beat is probably baked in as a "hold" scenario. An in-line print at $0.61 with cautious language would likely be negative at these technical levels. Watch for the Q1 tax rate: management guided Q1 to exceed the full-year 24% level — any adverse disclosure around tax (e.g., discrete item reversal) could depress adjusted EPS.

### 2. Net Revenue
**Consensus: ~$1.807B | Prior Year: $1.606B | Implied Growth: +12.5%**

The entire revenue step-up is driven by McCormick de Mexico consolidation (est. +$180-200M in Q1). Organic revenue growth expectation: +1-3%, equating to roughly **$16-48M** above Q1 FY2025 on a constant-currency organic basis (~$1.622-$1.654B organic). Actual reported will be ~$1.79-1.81B with Mexico. Any organic growth miss (volume decline in Consumer or FS shortfall) will be read through sharply.

### 3. Adjusted Gross Margin
**Expected direction: Expanding vs. Q1 FY2025's 37.6%**

Management explicitly guided for full-year gross margin expansion — Q1 is the first test. Key moving parts:
- **Positive:** Mexico business consolidation (higher-margin Consumer product), CCI savings, modest pricing contribution
- **Negative:** ~$50M full-year tariff headwind (front-half weighted?), elevated commodity costs, step-up in inventory to fair value for Mexico (a GAAP-only impact)
- **Watch:** Adjusted gross margin vs. both Q1 FY2025 (37.6%) and Q4 FY2025 (39.5% adjusted). Any sequential decline from Q4 would be a negative data point, even if YoY improving.

Expect management to report adjusted gross margin (excluding inventory step-up from Mexico). Target zone for a "good" print: **38.5-39.5%** adjusted. Consensus EBITDA for Q1: ~$318M, implying EBITDA margin of ~17.8%.

### 4. Consumer Segment Organic Growth (Volume Specifically)
**Prior quarter: +3% organic (+1% volume, +2% price)**

Volume growth is the brand-health barometer. Management has highlighted seven consecutive quarters of Consumer volume growth. A sequential step-down to flat or negative volume as pricing actions (September 2025) fully anniversarize would be a significant concern. Watch for:
- **Americas volume:** Q4 was +1%; any improvement here in Q1 would be positive given the pricing headwind
- **EMEA:** Eighth consecutive quarter of volume growth in Q4; running at +1-2%
- **China:** Management guided for "gradual" China consumer recovery in 2025 and called it on-track; Q1 2026 should show progress

**Bull signal:** Consumer organic growth >3% with any volume component ≥1%
**Bear signal:** Consumer organic <1%, especially if driven by volume decline

### 5. FY2026 Guidance Reaffirmation (Or Raise)
**Current guide: Net sales +13-17%, adj. EPS $3.05-$3.13**

The key question: does management maintain, narrow-to-top, or raise the FY2026 guide? At CAGNY (Feb. 17), management **explicitly reaffirmed** all components of the guide. A reaffirmation with Q1 execution visibility would be the baseline. Any upside surprise in Mexico integration progress or tariff mitigation outperformance could support a guide-raise narrative. 

Organic sales guidance specifically (1-3%) will be scrutinized — if management narrows the range to the top half (2-3%), it would be constructive. Any reduction in the organic guide would be a major negative given that the reported growth rate is already ~90% acquisition-driven.
