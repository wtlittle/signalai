# VRTX — Pre-Earnings Note

*April 20, 2026 | Price: $439.91 | Analyst: Pre-Earnings Research*

## 1. Set-Up

**Price & Trading Context**

VRTX trades at $439.91, down -2.9% YTD from a December 31, 2025 close of $453.36, and well below both the 52-week high of $510.77 and the post-Q4 reaction spike to $491.47 on February 13. The 52-week low is $362.50, establishing a roughly $148 range. The stock de-rated through March and April, pressured by broader market turbulence — it is sitting in the lower tercile of its one-year range despite no company-specific negative catalysts.

**Valuation**

At the current price, VRTX trades at approximately 28.7x trailing GAAP P/E and a 2025 EV/EBITDA of ~25x, consistent with its recent trading history (29.6x P/E, 25.2x EV/EBITDA at FY2025 year-end per company ratio data). Forward consensus puts FY2026 at ~$13.0B revenue and ~$4.5B net income, implying a ~24–25x forward earnings multiple — a modest premium to large-cap biopharma peers (Regeneron, Biogen) but reasonable given Vertex's near-durable franchise and expanding pipeline optionality. Free cash flow was $3.19B in FY2025 (26.6% FCF margin), supporting the valuation floor.

**CF Franchise Context**

Trikafta/Kaftrio remains the dominant revenue engine: AInvest pegged full-year 2025 CF product revenue at ~$10.3B, with total company revenue of [$12.0B](https://news.vrtx.com/news-releases/news-release-details/vertex-reports-fourth-quarter-and-full-year-2025-financial), up 9% YoY. Q4 2025 revenue was $3.19B, up 10% YoY, slightly beating the $3.18B consensus per [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/healthcare-pharmaceuticals/vertex-pharma-forecasts-2026-revenue-in-line-expects-growth-cystic-fibrosis-2026-02-12/). Management's FY2026 revenue guidance of $12.95–$13.1B implies continued 8–9% growth, with Q1 flagged at ~7% YoY. Alyftrek (vanzacaftor/tezacaftor/deutivacaftor), the next-gen "2.0" once-daily CFTR modulator, obtained FDA label extensions on April 1, 2026 expanding coverage to ~95% of all CF patients, and is actively transitioning patients from Trikafta in both the U.S. and Europe ([EMPR](https://www.empr.com/news/alyftrek-and-trikafta-now-approved-for-broader-cystic-fibrosis-use/)).

**New Launch Overlay**

Casgevy (gene therapy, sickle cell/beta thalassemia) generated $116M in FY2025 and $54M in Q4 alone, with 64 total patients infused for the year and 30 in Q4. Journavx (suzetrigine, non-opioid acute pain), launched in Q1 2025, generated $60M in its first 8 months of commercial availability. Combined non-CF 2025 revenue was ~$176M. Management has guided for ≥$500M in non-CF revenue in FY2026 — a 185% step-up from the 2025 base that defines the key upside/downside narrative entering Q1.

## 2. Key Debates & Variants

**Debate 1: Casgevy — Ramp Rate vs. Structural Ceiling**
The central question is whether Casgevy's Q4 momentum (30 infusions, $54M) is the beginning of an exponential ramp or a soft plateau. Bulls argue: 111 new patient initiations in Q4 creates a backlog, ATC network is operational across the U.S. and Europe, Italy's landmark reimbursement covers ~5,000 beta-thalassemia patients, and >90% Medicaid/commercial access has been secured. Bears counter: the CAR-T/gene therapy precedent suggests infusion capacity constraints are stubborn, that 64 annual infusions implies $1.8M/patient revenue but the logistics pipeline is lumpy, and that CRISPR Therapeutics recently disclosed mixed data on competing approaches. The $500M+ non-CF target (~$400M of which would need to come from Casgevy/Journavx acceleration) is highly back-half weighted — Q1 alone will not resolve this debate, but trajectory matters.

**Debate 2: Journavx Gross-to-Net — When Does Revenue Catch Up to Scripts?**
Vertex guided that >550,000 Journavx scripts were filled in 2025 and expects to >3x that number in 2026 (i.e., ~1.7M scripts). But gross-to-net was severely compressed in 2025 due to the patient support program (PSP) — $60M on 550k scripts implies ~$109/script realized vs. a ~$31/pill list price suggesting massive patient subsidy. Management indicated the PSP "sunsets" in late 2026/early 2027 as commercial coverage normalizes. The debate: does revenue inflect meaningfully in H2 2026 (bull case) or does payer friction persist longer than expected, keeping gross-to-net suppressed into 2027 (bear case)? Q1 commentary on payer mix, formulary wins, and the PSP utilization rate will be the key signal.

**Debate 3: Trikafta Patent Cliff — Is Alyftrek a Structural Bridge or a Cliff Defender?**
Trikafta's composition-of-matter patents expire in the mid-2030s in the U.S., but method-of-treatment and formulation patents provide layered protection. Alyftrek's launch is strategically designed to transition patients to a new IP stack with a cleaner expiry profile. The bear case is that Alyftrek transition is slower than expected (Q4 transcript noted "strong patient loyalty to Trikafta") and generic competition eventually compresses the CF franchise. The bull case: Vertex's 5-drug CF portfolio, serial innovation pipeline (VX-828, VX-581, VX-522 for the ~5,000 patients who can't benefit from modulators), and expanding pediatric approvals create durable pricing power.

**Debate 4: Povetacicept / Renal — The $10B Next Vertical**
Management has characterized the renal franchise as potentially "rival[ing] the scale of the CF business." Pove is in rolling BLA submission for IgAN (breakthrough designation, priority review voucher used, targeting >330,000 U.S./European patients). Phase II/III adaptive study in membranous nephropathy is on track for Phase III advancement this summer. This pipeline optionality is not well-priced in current consensus estimates — approval could represent a $3–5B+ revenue event over a 5-year ramp, comparable to Trikafta's own launch trajectory.

**Consensus Error Risk**
Q1 2025 was the only quarter in the last 8 where Vertex missed revenue estimates — missing by -3.2% ($2.77B vs. $2.86B estimate) — which drove a -10% single-day reaction. The "beat cadence" reestablished in Q2–Q4 2025 supports the current modest beat expectation, but the Q1 seasonality (historically soft for CF given insurance resets and patient deductible dynamics) makes this a key watch item.
