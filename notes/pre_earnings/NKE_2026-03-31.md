# Nike (NKE) -- Q3 FY2026 Pre-Earnings Note
## Reports: Tuesday, March 31, 2026 (After Close, 5:00 PM ET)
## Days Until: 4

---

## Set-up
Nike is mid-turnaround under CEO Elliott Hill's "Win Now" strategy, reporting amid $1.5B in annualized tariff headwinds and a stock near 52-week lows (~$53, down >50% from 2021 highs). Q2 FY2026 showed revenue of $12.4B (-8% YoY), EPS of $0.53 (beat $0.37 estimate), and gross margins contracted 300bps to 40.6%. The turnaround is gaining traction in North America but China and wholesale rebuilding remain uncertain. BofA maintains Buy with $73 target; JPM and others are more cautious.

Key context entering the print:
- Rebuilding wholesale partnerships (reversing prior DTC-first strategy)
- 17-18% price hikes across ~3,300 SKUs to offset tariffs
- Shifting sourcing from China (16% -> high single digits) to Vietnam/Indonesia
- Facing $1.5B annualized tariff cost (~320bps gross margin drag, partially mitigated to ~120bps)
- Stock trading near 52-week low of $52.17

## Key Debates and Variants

1. North America trajectory: BofA sees "continued progress" in NA, but total sales still expected to decline slightly (-0.4% total, with 300bps FX benefit). Is the turnaround real or just less bad?

2. China deterioration: Analysts expect China sales to contract ~16% YoY (similar to Q2). Is this stabilizing or worsening? Any signs of recovery?

3. Gross margin path: Consensus expects ~200bps contraction. With tariff mitigation ramping, is there upside to margins this quarter? BofA thinks gross margins "may stabilize."

4. FY2027 outlook timing: Management expected to delay fiscal 2027 guidance until June. Will they provide any directional commentary that gives the market comfort?

## What Matters This Print

1. Revenue: Consensus $11.23B (slight decline YoY). Any beat signals turnaround gaining momentum.
2. EPS: Consensus $0.29-0.32 (down ~60% YoY). Low bar, but quality of the beat matters.
3. Gross margin: Consensus ~200bps decline. Watch for tariff mitigation progress (currently offsetting ~120bps of 320bps headwind).
4. North America comparable sales: Key indicator of whether brand heat is returning under Hill's leadership.
5. China revenue: $1.5-1.6B expected. Any stabilization would be a positive catalyst.
