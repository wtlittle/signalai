## Elliott Hill Turnaround — State of Play

CEO Elliott Hill (appointed October 2024) is approximately 18 months into his **"Win Now" turnaround**. Key structural actions taken through Q3 FY2026:

**1. Wholesale Re-engagement (the most visible early win)**
Hill reversed the prior DTC-at-all-costs strategy and began aggressively rebuilding relationships with key wholesale partners. Q2 FY2026 wholesale revenues grew **+8% YoY to $7.5B**, including **+24% growth in North America wholesale** — the best indicator that sell-in is recovering. Order books globally are improving season-over-season. Football/soccer order books for 2026 are running **~40% above World Cup 2022 levels**. ([Metaintro](https://www.metaintro.com/blog/nike-turnaround-elliott-hill-hiring-layoffs-2026), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/12/18/nike-nke-Q2-2026-earnings.html))

**2. Classics Franchise Reset**
Nike is deliberately shrinking its Classics business (Air Force 1, Dunk, Jordan retro). Matt Friend guided that classic footwear franchises are on track to decline from peak levels by **more than $4B by FY2026 year-end**. This is creating a ~$550M/quarter top-line headwind but is a necessary brand health measure. Running grew +20%+ for the second consecutive quarter in Q2 FY2026, up double digits across all channels. ([Q2 FY2026 transcript via Perplexity Finance](https://perplexity.ai/finance/NKE))

**3. Sport Offense Restructure**
Hill introduced the "Sport Offense" framework — sport-by-sport, athlete-centered product innovation scaled country by country. All geographies now report directly to Hill. CFO Matt Friend was given oversight of sales and Nike Direct. A new COO, Venkatesh Alagirisamy, was appointed to drive end-to-end technology integration. ([Q2 FY2026 transcript via Perplexity Finance](https://perplexity.ai/finance/NKE))

**4. Cost Structure Reset**
- Nike filed an SEC disclosure on March 5, 2026, confirming approximately **$300M in pre-tax restructuring charges** for the nine months ended February 28, 2026 — primarily severance from the January elimination of **775 distribution center jobs** and the ongoing Converse corporate realignment. ([TIKR](https://www.tikr.com/blog/nike-stock-outlook-heres-why-analysts-see-potential-80-upside))
- Management is targeting a return to **double-digit EBIT margins** from the current ~8.0% operating margin (Q2 FY2026 operating margin: ~8.1%).

**5. Greater China — The Lagging Front**
China remains the most significant overhang. Q2 FY2026 Greater China revenue fell **-16% YoY to $1.42B** (Nike Digital -36%, wholesale -15%). Hill acknowledged: changes "are not happening at the level or the pace we need to drive wider change." A fresh approach to China's digital-first, monobrand footprint is being developed. EBIT in Greater China fell -49% to $191M. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/12/18/nike-nke-Q2-2026-earnings.html), [Q2 transcript via Perplexity Finance](https://perplexity.ai/finance/NKE))

**6. Tariff Headwind**
$1.5B in annualized incremental product costs from higher U.S. tariffs. This represents a **~320bps gross headwind in FY2026**; Nike has implemented mitigation measures (supply chain diversification, sourcing shifts) to reduce net impact to approximately **120bps**. North America gross margins declined only 330bps despite a 520bps tariff impact — evidence the underlying margin recovery is working. ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/higher-tariffs-production-costs-nike-141100889.html))

**7. Amazon Return**
Nike is reportedly making progress toward a return to the Amazon marketplace — reversing another key strategic error of the prior regime. This could be a meaningful DTC supplement without fully ceding control. ([Metaintro](https://www.metaintro.com/blog/nike-turnaround-elliott-hill-hiring-layoffs-2026))

## Key Debates and Variants

### Bull Case — "The Setup is the Story"
The bull thesis is that NKE stock is pricing in sustained deterioration when the underlying business is bottoming. The core arguments:

- **North America is working.** +24% wholesale growth in Q2 FY2026 validates that partners are re-engaging. Dick's Sporting Goods' Q4 commentary and Jefferies channel checks point to robust product sell-through and demand from new Running launches. ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/nike-nke-investment-story-shifting-010948982.html))
- **Gross margin ex-tariff is inflecting positively.** Management's own math: excluding the 315bps tariff headwind, gross margin would be *expanding* in Q3. That is the signal bears are ignoring.
- **Classics reset is finite.** The $4B+ decline in classics revenue has an endpoint (likely FY2027). Once the comp base normalizes, the total revenue trajectory becomes additive.
- **Valuation at decade lows.** EV/Revenue at ~2x is the lowest in at least a decade. If margins recover to 10% EBIT (vs. ~8% today), the stock has 50-80% upside on a 2-3 year view. Morningstar fair value: ~$76.42; RBC target: $78; BTIG: $90. ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/nike-nke-investment-story-shifting-010948982.html), [TIKR](https://www.tikr.com/blog/nike-stock-outlook-heres-why-analysts-see-potential-80-upside))
- **Hill has institutional credibility.** He is a 32-year Nike veteran. He understands the wholesale model. He has restructured the org in a year more than any prior regime. The Dick's acquisition of Foot Locker is explicitly a "vote of confidence" in Hill. ([Retail Dive](https://www.retaildive.com/news/dicks-sporting-goods-foot-locker-acquisition-big-bet-nike-turnaround/751420/))

### Bear Case — "The Turnaround Is Taking Longer Than Expected"
- **China is not recovering.** BNP Paribas maintains an Underperform with a $35 target, citing Pou Sheng (key Nike retailer in China) profit warnings and potential restructuring risk. Greater China EBIT collapsed -49% in Q2. There is no visible near-term catalyst to reverse local brand perception issues compounded by competitor gains (Anta, Li-Ning). ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/nike-nke-investment-story-shifting-010948982.html))
- **Q4 guidance likely below consensus.** UBS channel checks (March 19) suggest Q3 EPS roughly in-line, but Q4 EPS outlook of only **$0.03–$0.18 vs. Street at $0.23**. If confirmed on March 31, that is a meaningful negative guide. ([Investing.com](https://www.investing.com/news/analyst-ratings/ubs-cuts-nike-stock-price-target-on-weak-sales-momentum-93CH-4569851))
- **DTC declining structurally.** Nike Direct was -8% in Q2 FY2026 (Nike Digital -14%). Moving product back to wholesale channels creates a structural mix-shift to lower-margin wholesale — the opposite of the efficiency gains the prior DTC strategy promised.
- **Tariff risk remains.** A 15% global tariff was mooted in late February. If enacted broadly, the $300–$320bps headwind could compound. Nike sourced ~40-50% of US product from Vietnam and other Asia-Pacific nations — these are precisely the exposed countries. ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/did-nikes-dividend-amid-tariff-101149639.html))
- **Macro and brand saturation.** Bank of America diagnosed a "change of cycle" — the casualization/athleisure trend that drove Nike's decade of growth is exhausting. Consumers face a saturated closet. Hoka grew 19%, On grew strongly — structural share shift to premium performance brands is real. ([AInvest](https://www.ainvest.com/news/nike-win-strategy-fire-tariffs-discounting-threaten-margin-resilience-2603/))
- **EBIT margin compressing further.** TIKR models project EBIT margin at just **6.3% in FY2026** (vs. 12.3% in FY2024). The path to double-digits requires both revenue recovery *and* tariff normalization — two variables neither management nor the street can forecast with conviction. ([TIKR](https://www.tikr.com/blog/nike-stock-outlook-heres-why-analysts-see-potential-80-upside))

### 1–3 Year Horizon Framework (Buy-Side Lens)

For a long-duration holder, the Q3 print matters mainly as a signal, not a driver of thesis. The investment case rests on:

1. **Can Nike return to $50B+ revenue by FY2028?** TIKR estimates FY2026 at $46.7B flat; consensus FY2027 implies ~$48.5B. Management itself has guided toward $50.7B by FY2028 (3.1% CAGR).
2. **Can EBIT margins recover to 10%+?** From ~8% today. At $50B revenue and 10% EBIT, operating income would be ~$5B vs. ~$3.5B currently — roughly $1.5B of annual EBIT expansion. At a 20x EBIT multiple (reasonable for a turnaround complete), that is $100B+ market cap vs. $77B today.
3. **Does Hill have the time?** The board appears committed. The dividend is protected ($0.41/quarter). The balance sheet, while carrying restructuring charges, is not stressed.

At $52, the stock offers a **3–5 year asymmetric risk/reward for patient capital**, with the primary risks being a sustained China collapse and tariff permanence. The base case is that both resolve over 18–36 months.
