# NKE (Nike) -- Pre-Earnings Notes
## Earnings Date: March 31, 2026 (After Close, ~4:15 PM ET)
## Quarter: Q3 FY2026 (Dec-Feb)

---

### Set-up

Nike trades at ~$53, near its 52-week low of $52.17, down ~60% over five years. The stock is at 2017 valuation levels. CEO Elliott Hill (returned Oct 2024) is 16 months into a "Win Now" turnaround focused on five pillars: culture, product, marketing, marketplace, and in-person presence. The running category has grown 20%+ for two consecutive quarters. North American wholesale grew 24% in Q2 FY2026, global wholesale +8%. Q2 FY2026 EPS of $0.53 beat consensus ($0.37) by 43%. Dividend yield now exceeds 3%.

Key headwinds: China sales contracted 17% in Q2 (sixth consecutive quarter of decline), gross margins pressured by tariffs and inventory liquidation, total revenue growth still sluggish despite category wins. The turnaround is real but "won't be a straight line" per Hill.

### Key Debates and Variants

1. **China trajectory**: Bulls see a bottoming process; bears see structural share loss to local brands (Anta, Li-Ning). A 16% decline is baked in by BofA -- any improvement is upside.
2. **Gross margin trajectory**: ~200bps contraction expected from tariffs + liquidation. Key variant: has the tariff mitigation ramped enough to stabilize margins? Management indicated mitigation accelerating in Q3.
3. **Wholesale reengagement sustainability**: 24% NA wholesale growth in Q2 was the headline. Can it sustain? Nike is back on Amazon and expanding to Urban Outfitters, Aritzia.
4. **FY2027 guidance timing**: Will Nike provide a forward framework or delay until June? Any signal on a return to mid-single-digit revenue growth matters enormously for the multiple.
5. **Running category durability**: 20%+ growth for 2 straight quarters. Is this category momentum durable, or is it a temporary share grab?

### What Matters This Print

1. **Revenue**: Consensus $11.23B (flat to -0.4% YoY). Prior quarter was $12.4B. Any positive growth surprises would be significant.
2. **EPS**: Consensus $0.28-$0.32 (range reflects divergent analyst views). Prior quarter beat by 43%. Some analysts model $0.40 based on turnaround momentum.
3. **Gross Margin**: Expected ~42% (down ~200bps YoY). Watch for commentary on tariff mitigation progress and timing of full-price selling ramp.
4. **China Revenue**: Consensus implies ~$1.2B (-16% YoY). Any sequential improvement from Q2's $1.4B would be a positive signal.
5. **North America Wholesale**: Sustained 20%+ growth would validate the channel strategy pivot.

### Scenario Grid

**Bull Case (Stock +10-15%, ~$58-61)**
- Revenue in-line or slight beat ($11.3B+)
- EPS beat >$0.35 (similar to Q2 pattern)
- China stabilizing (decline narrowing to single digits)
- Gross margin better than feared (>42.5%)
- Constructive FY2027 framework provided
- Running category sustaining 20%+

**Base Case (Stock -2% to +5%, ~$52-56)**
- Revenue roughly in-line at $11.2B (-0.4%)
- EPS of $0.28-$0.32 per consensus
- China still weak (-15-17%)
- Gross margin contracts 200bps as expected
- FY2027 outlook delayed to June
- Turnaround commentary remains positive but no acceleration

**Bear Case (Stock -10-15%, ~$45-48)**
- Revenue miss (<$11.0B)
- EPS miss (<$0.25)
- China deterioration accelerates (>-20%)
- Gross margin compression >250bps
- Cautious tone on wholesale reengagement or running momentum fading
- No FY2027 visibility; tariff concerns elevated
