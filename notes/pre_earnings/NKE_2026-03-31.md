## Elliott Hill Turnaround — State of Play

CEO Elliott Hill (appointed October 2024) is approximately 18 months into his **"Win Now" turnaround**. Key structural actions taken through Q3 FY2026:

**1. Wholesale Re-engagement (the most visible early win)**
Hill reversed the prior DTC-at-all-costs strategy and began aggressively rebuilding relationships with key wholesale partners. Q2 FY2026 wholesale revenues grew **+8% YoY to $7.5B**, including **+24% growth in North America wholesale** — the best indicator that sell-in is recovering. Order books globally are improving season-over-season. Football/soccer order books for 2026 are running **~40% above World Cup 2022 levels**. ([Metaintro](https://www.metaintro.com/blog/nike-turnaround-elliott-hill-hiring-layoffs-2026), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/12/18/nike-nke-Q2-2026-earnings.html))

**As of March 22, 2026:** Simply Wall Street notes that wholesale rebound signals are continuing, with management repairing retailer relationships and using channel to stabilize volumes as consumer conditions remain under pressure. The key watch item is whether wholesale sell-through and markdown activity are improving — evidence that demand (not just supply push) is healthy. ([Simply Wall St, March 22, 2026](https://simplywall.st/stocks/us/consumer-durables/nyse-nke/nike/news/nike-wholesale-rebound-offers-new-clues-on-turnaround-progre))

**2. Running Category Outperformance**
Truist channel checks dated **March 19, 2026** indicate that **Nike running shoes continue to sell strongly at Dick's Sporting Goods**, providing a direct counterpoint to UBS's softer broader channel checks. This is the most recent sell-side channel check data available heading into March 31. Running grew +20%+ for the second consecutive quarter in Q2 FY2026, up double digits across all channels. This is the turnaround's clearest proof point. ([Investing.com/Truist, March 19, 2026](https://www.investing.com/news/stock-market-news/truist-checks-indicate-nike-running-shoes-continue-strong-sales-at-dicks-sporting-goods-93CH-4570746), [Q2 FY2026 transcript via Perplexity Finance](https://perplexity.ai/finance/NKE))

**3. Classics Franchise Reset**
Nike is deliberately shrinking its Classics business (Air Force 1, Dunk, Jordan retro). Classic footwear declined ~20% in Q2 FY2026 and is on track to decline from peak levels by **more than $4B by FY2026 year-end** vs. peak. This is creating a ~$550M/quarter top-line headwind but is a necessary brand health measure. ([Q2 FY2026 transcript via Perplexity Finance](https://perplexity.ai/finance/NKE))

**4. Sport Offense Restructure**
Hill introduced the "Sport Offense" framework — sport-by-sport, athlete-centered product innovation scaled country by country. New products in the pipeline: **Nike Mind** (neuroscience-based footwear platform for competitive preparation) and **Structure 26** (stability running). A $4.9B marketing budget for FY2026 supports the 2026 FIFA World Cup cycle (North America-hosted). ([Invezz](https://invezz.com/news/2026/03/24/nike-stock-price-is-nearing-a-make-or-break-level-is-it-a-buy/), [Chronicle-Journal](http://markets.chroniclejournal.com/chroniclejournal/article/finterra-2026-3-17-the-swoosh-pivot-a-deep-dive-into-nikes-nke-2026-rebuilding-era))

**5. Cost Structure Reset**
- Nike filed an SEC disclosure on March 5, 2026, confirming approximately **$300M in pre-tax restructuring charges** for the nine months ended February 28, 2026 — primarily severance from the elimination of **775 distribution center jobs** and the ongoing Converse corporate realignment. ([TIKR](https://www.tikr.com/blog/nike-stock-outlook-heres-why-analysts-see-potential-80-upside))
- Management is targeting a return to **double-digit EBIT margins** from the current ~8.0% operating margin (Q2 FY2026 operating margin: ~8.1%).
- **NikeSkims** (collaboration with Skims for women's technical athleisure) is targeted for spring 2026 full retail release — a high-margin, high-visibility product designed to capture the women's performance-lifestyle category.

**6. Greater China — The Lagging Front**
China remains the most significant overhang. Q2 FY2026 Greater China revenue fell **-16% YoY to $1.42B** (Nike Digital -36%, wholesale -15%). Hill acknowledged: changes "are not happening at the level or the pace we need to drive wider change." A fresh approach to China's digital-first, monobrand footprint is being developed; store pilots have shown "encouraging results" (better traffic, comp sales growth) but are not yet at scale. EBIT in Greater China fell -49% to $191M. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/12/18/nike-nke-Q2-2026-earnings.html), [Q2 transcript via Perplexity Finance](https://perplexity.ai/finance/NKE))

**7. Amazon Return**
Nike has rejoined the Amazon marketplace (after exiting in 2019), deepening DTC optionality without fully ceding brand control. This could provide a meaningful incremental revenue channel in FY2027. ([Awalyt, March 24, 2026](https://awalyt.com/insights/nike-stock-turnaround-or-value-trap-q3-earnings-march-2026))

## Key Debates and Variants

### Bull Case — "The Setup is the Story"
The bull thesis is that NKE stock is pricing in sustained deterioration when the underlying business is bottoming. The core arguments:

- **North America is working.** +24% wholesale growth in Q2 FY2026 validates that partners are re-engaging. Dick's Sporting Goods' Q4 commentary and Truist channel checks (March 19) point to robust sell-through in running — the most durable consumer sub-category. ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/nike-nke-investment-story-shifting-010948982.html))
- **Gross margin ex-tariff is inflecting positively.** Management's own math: excluding the 315bps tariff headwind, gross margin would be *expanding* in Q3. That is the signal bears are choosing to ignore.
- **Tariff relief scenario.** Section 122 has a 150-day clock — it expires around mid-July 2026. If the administration does not extend or replace it, Nike could see meaningful gross margin relief in Q4 FY2026 and Q1 FY2027. This is unpriced optionality.
- **Classics reset is finite.** The $4B+ decline in classics revenue has an endpoint (likely FY2027). Once the comp base normalizes, the total revenue trajectory becomes additive.
- **Valuation at decade lows.** EV/Revenue at ~2x is the lowest in at least a decade. If margins recover to 10% EBIT (vs. ~8% today), the stock has 50-80% upside on a 2-3 year view. Morningstar fair value: ~$76.42; RBC target: $78; BTIG: $90. ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/nike-nke-investment-story-shifting-010948982.html), [TIKR](https://www.tikr.com/blog/nike-stock-outlook-heres-why-analysts-see-potential-80-upside))
- **Insider conviction.** CEO Hill, Tim Cook, and Robert Swan collectively bought ~75,000+ shares at $57–$62 in December 2025. The stock is now 10%+ below those purchase prices — raising the stakes for the March 31 print but also signaling board-level conviction. ([MarketBeat](https://www.marketbeat.com/instant-alerts/nike-nysenke-reaches-new-52-week-low-following-analyst-downgrade-2026-03-20/))
- **Hill has institutional credibility.** He is a 32-year Nike veteran. He has restructured the org in under 18 months more than any prior regime. The Dick's acquisition of Foot Locker is explicitly a "vote of confidence" in Hill. ([Retail Dive](https://www.retaildive.com/news/dicks-sporting-goods-foot-locker-acquisition-big-bet-nike-turnaround/751420/))

### Bear Case — "The Turnaround Is Taking Longer Than Expected"
- **China is not recovering.** BNP Paribas maintains an Underperform with a $35 target, citing Pou Sheng (key Nike retailer in China) profit warnings and potential restructuring risk. Greater China EBIT collapsed -49% in Q2. There is no visible near-term catalyst to reverse local brand perception issues compounded by competitor gains (Anta, Li-Ning). ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/nike-nke-investment-story-shifting-010948982.html))
- **Q4 guidance likely below consensus.** UBS channel checks (March 19) suggest Q3 EPS roughly in-line, but Q4 EPS outlook of only **$0.03–$0.18 vs. Street at $0.19** (consensus Q4 EPS per finance tools is $0.189). If confirmed on March 31, that is a meaningful negative guide. ([Investing.com](https://www.investing.com/news/analyst-ratings/ubs-cuts-nike-stock-price-target-on-weak-sales-momentum-93CH-4569851))
- **DTC declining structurally.** Nike Direct was -8% in Q2 FY2026 (Nike Digital -14%). Moving product back to wholesale channels creates a structural mix-shift to lower-margin wholesale.
- **Section 122 tariff adds new layer of uncertainty.** The Supreme Court's IEEPA ruling in February briefly created relief, but Trump's invocation of Section 122 (15% global surcharge through July 2026) partially restored the tariff burden. Nike faces an environment where trade policy can shift on short notice — making any gross margin guidance range inherently wider than normal. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/20/nike-swings-exemplify-the-markets-uncertainty-about-trump-tariffs-ruling.html), [MakeMine](https://www.makemine.com/blog/vietnam-tariffs))
- **Price hikes risk demand destruction.** Projected 11–17% price increases on core shoe models may meet consumer resistance, particularly in a macro environment where cautious consumer spending has already made $150+ sneakers a harder sell. On Running and Hoka continue to gain share at premium price points without carrying Nike's inventory/margin baggage. ([Chronicle-Journal, Feb 23, 2026](http://markets.chroniclejournal.com/chroniclejournal/article/marketminute-2026-2-23-the-15-surcharge-nike-and-gap-slump-as-global-tariffs-hit-us-ports))
- **EBIT margin compressing further.** TIKR models project EBIT margin at just **6.3% in FY2026** (vs. 12.3% in FY2024). The path to double-digits requires both revenue recovery *and* tariff normalization. ([TIKR](https://www.tikr.com/blog/nike-stock-outlook-heres-why-analysts-see-potential-80-upside))
- **Technical deterioration.** Five consecutive weekly declines. Stock below all moving averages. A close below $52.25 would negate the double-bottom pattern and open the path to $50/$45. ([Invezz, March 24, 2026](https://invezz.com/news/2026/03/24/nike-stock-price-is-nearing-a-make-or-break-level-is-it-a-buy/))

**Key non-consensus risk (UBS-led bear):** Q4 EPS guidance of $0.03–$0.18 vs. consensus $0.19 is already being floated. If confirmed, the base case slides toward the lower bound of the bear scenario. At ~$47–$50, NKE would trade at sub-2x EV/Revenue.

**Key non-consensus risk (bull):** If Hill provides any explicit FY2027 growth commentary or signals the Converse restructuring is resolved, multiple expansion from depressed levels could be swift. There is meaningful short interest at current levels; any positive surprise could produce an outsized short squeeze.

### 1–3 Year Horizon Framework (Buy-Side Lens)

For a long-duration holder, the Q3 print matters mainly as a signal, not a driver of thesis. The investment case rests on:

1. **Can Nike return to $50B+ revenue by FY2028?** Finance tool consensus for FY2027 is ~$48.4B (sum of quarterly estimates). Management itself has guided toward $50.7B by FY2028 (3.1% CAGR).
2. **Can EBIT margins recover to 10%+?** From ~8% today. At $50B revenue and 10% EBIT, operating income would be ~$5B vs. ~$3.5B currently — roughly $1.5B of annual EBIT expansion. At a 20x EBIT multiple, that is $100B+ market cap vs. $79B today.
3. **Does Hill have the time?** The board appears committed. The dividend is protected. The balance sheet is not stressed. Insider buying at $57–$61 (now 10%+ above current price) suggests conviction from those with the most information.

At $53, the stock offers a **3–5 year asymmetric risk/reward for patient capital**, with the primary risks being a sustained China collapse, tariff permanence beyond the 150-day Section 122 window, and consumer demand destruction from 11–17% price hikes. The base case is that all three risks moderate over 18–36 months.
