## Set-up

Amazon stock is approaching its earnings report near all-time highs, up over 8% YTD -- the second-best performance among mega-cap tech behind only Broadcom. The rally has been driven by accelerating AWS growth expectations and the narrative that AI infrastructure spending is a multi-year tailwind for Amazon's cloud business. Investor sentiment is bullish but the bar is elevated.

Q1 2026 guidance: Revenue $173.5-178.5B, Operating Income $16.5-21.5B. Consensus EPS ~$1.55. AWS growth was 24% in Q4 2025 and analysts expect acceleration toward 30%+ as AI workloads ramp.

## Key Debates & Variant Perception

- AWS growth trajectory: Can AWS accelerate from 24% to 30%+ in Q1? The AI workload ramp is the key driver, but competitive pressure from Azure and GCP is intensifying. Some analysts (Truist) see potential for 38% growth.
- $200B capex plan: Amazon's aggressive AI infrastructure spend is the largest among hyperscalers. Bulls see it as securing competitive advantage; bears worry about return on investment timeline and potential overcapacity.
- Union ruling impact: Recent labor rulings could increase costs in fulfillment operations. How material is the financial impact?
- Tariff exposure: Amazon's first-party retail and third-party marketplace both have significant China-sourced inventory. Tariff escalation could pressure margins.
- AI "seat compression" in enterprise SaaS: Amazon is both a beneficiary (AWS AI services) and potential disruptor (AI replacing SaaS subscriptions).
