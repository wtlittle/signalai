# STZ (Constellation Brands) -- Pre-Earnings Notes
## Earnings Date: April 8, 2026 (After Close)
## Conference Call: April 9, 2026 (8:00 AM ET)
## Quarter: Q4 FY2026 + Full Year (Year ended Feb 28, 2026)

---

### Set-up

Constellation Brands reports its full fiscal year and Q4 FY2026 results on April 8 after close. STZ trades at ~$153, trading near the lower end of its recent range ($144.61-$167.63 since last earnings). The stock is in the consumer staples universe but has a more growth-oriented profile through its premium beer portfolio (Modelo, Corona, Pacifico). The wine/spirits divestiture thesis is largely played out -- the focus now is beer execution and capital allocation.

This is a full-year report, which carries more weight for annual guidance. The current Deflation Risk regime favors defensive names, but STZ straddles the line between staples safety and discretionary exposure (alcohol spending is somewhat elastic).

### Key Debates and Variants

1. **Beer depletion trends**: Modelo Especial has been the #1 beer brand in the US. Are depletions sustaining 6-8% growth, or is the post-Bud Light share gain normalizing?
2. **Pricing power in a softening consumer**: Can STZ maintain pricing in its premium beer portfolio as consumers face macro headwinds?
3. **Wine & Spirits divestiture**: Status of remaining divestitures and use of proceeds (buybacks vs. debt reduction).
4. **FY2027 guidance**: Initial guidance framework for the new fiscal year is the most important output of this report. Beer growth, margins, and EPS trajectory.
5. **Tariff/Mexico exposure**: Nearly all of STZ's beer is brewed in Mexico. Any tariff developments could be material.

### What Matters This Print

1. **Beer depletions**: Growth rate in Modelo family and Corona -- consensus expects 5-7% depletion growth.
2. **Beer operating margin**: Has been expanding; continuation signals pricing power durability.
3. **FY2027 EPS guidance**: The market is focused on forward earnings power. Consensus FY2027 EPS is ~$14.50.
4. **Capital return update**: Buyback authorization status and dividend trajectory.
5. **Mexico supply chain/tariff commentary**: Any risk flagging on trade policy could pressure the stock.

### Scenario Grid

**Bull Case (Stock +8-12%)**
- Beer depletions 7%+ with share gains continuing
- Beer margins expanding
- FY2027 EPS guidance above consensus ($14.75+)
- Aggressive buyback announcement
- Tariff concerns dismissed or mitigated

**Base Case (Stock -2% to +4%)**
- Beer depletions 5-6%
- Margins stable
- FY2027 guidance in-line (~$14.50)
- Standard capital return program
- Measured tariff commentary

**Bear Case (Stock -8-12%)**
- Beer depletions decelerating below 5%
- Margin pressure from input costs or tariffs
- FY2027 guidance below consensus (<$14.00)
- Consumer trade-down to value brands
- Elevated Mexico/tariff risk language
