# STZ (Constellation Brands) — Pre-Earnings Notes
## Earnings Date: April 8, 2026 (After Close), Conference Call April 9 at 8:00 AM ET
## Quarter: Q4 FY2026 / Full Year (Year ending Feb 28, 2026)

---

### Set-up

Constellation Brands is a leading beverage alcohol company anchored by its dominant beer portfolio (Modelo Especial, Corona, Pacifico). The stock trades well off highs amid broader consumer staples weakness. This is a full fiscal year + Q4 report with CEO transition: incoming CEO Nicholas Fink will provide opening remarks before current CEO Bill Newlands and CFO Garth Hankinson lead Q&A.

Q3 FY2026 results were solid: EPS $3.06 beat consensus of $2.64 (+15.9%), though revenue of $2.2B was down -9.8% YoY. The beer business remains the growth engine while wine & spirits continues to be divested/optimized.

**Analyst consensus for Q4:**
- EPS: $1.68 (MarketBeat) / $1.73 (Zacks, updated Apr 1). Wide spread -- Zacks Most Accurate Estimate is below consensus (ESP -1.59%)
- Revenue: $1.84-1.89B (MarketBeat $1.84B, Zacks $1.89B -- down 12.6% YoY per Zacks)
- Full-year comparable EPS guidance: $11.30-$11.60 (company updated Sept 2025)
- Citi PT $175, Barclays PT $148, Needham PT $180, UBS PT $168, Bernstein PT $197, Wells Fargo PT $165
- Beer net sales guided to decline 4-2% for FY2026; Enterprise organic net sales decline 6-4%
- Operating cash flow guided $2.5-2.6B; FCF $1.3-1.4B
- CEO transition: Nicholas Fink becomes CEO April 13, just days after this report. His opening remarks will be closely parsed.

### Key Debates and Variants

1. **Beer depletion trends**: Modelo Especial has been the #1 beer brand in the U.S. Can volume growth sustain 6-8% in a softening consumer environment?
2. **Wine & spirits divestiture progress**: What's the timeline to fully exit or optimize the underperforming W&S segment? Any write-downs?
3. **CEO transition narrative**: Nicholas Fink's first public remarks as incoming CEO. What strategic priorities does he signal? Any shift in capital allocation?
4. **Tariff exposure**: Mexico-sourced beer is a key risk. How is STZ managing tariff mitigation and any potential cost pass-through?
5. **Capital return vs. deleveraging**: Balance sheet optimization. Dividend sustainability and buyback trajectory.

### What Matters This Print

1. **Beer Segment Revenue & Depletions**: Volume trends in Modelo, Corona, Pacifico. Pricing power vs. volume.
2. **Q4 EPS**: Consensus $1.71. Prior-year Q4 was $2.63, so a significant YoY decline is expected.
3. **FY2026 Full-Year Summary**: Total picture on how the fiscal year shaped up. Full-year EPS trajectory.
4. **FY2027 Guidance**: Critical — first guidance under incoming CEO Fink. Revenue, EPS, and capital allocation priorities.
5. **Wine & Spirits Update**: Divestiture timeline, write-down risk, margin drag.

### Scenario Grid

**Bull Case (Stock +6-10%)**
- Beer depletions >7% growth
- Q4 EPS beat >$1.85
- FY2027 guidance above consensus
- Clean W&S exit timeline
- Fink articulates compelling strategic vision
- Buyback acceleration

**Base Case (Stock -2% to +4%)**
- Beer depletions at 5-6%
- Q4 EPS in-line at $1.71
- FY2027 guidance in-line with street
- W&S optimization continues
- Constructive but cautious CEO remarks

**Bear Case (Stock -6-10%)**
- Beer depletions decelerate to <4%
- Q4 EPS miss <$1.60
- FY2027 guidance below consensus
- W&S write-down or restructuring charge
