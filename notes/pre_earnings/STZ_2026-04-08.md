# Constellation Brands (STZ) -- Q4 FY2026 Pre-Earnings Note
## Reports: Wednesday, April 8, 2026 (After Close); Conference Call April 9, 8:00 AM ET
## Days Until: 12

---

## Set-up
Constellation Brands reports full fiscal year and Q4 FY2026 results (fiscal year ending Feb 28, 2026). The company announced the reporting date on March 19. Analysts expect a weaker Q4 with EPS of $1.73, down 34.2% from $2.63 in the year-ago quarter. Q3 showed revenue of $2.2B, down 9.8% YoY, reflecting ongoing softness in the wine & spirits portfolio and deceleration in beer growth. The company continues to navigate a challenging consumer spending environment and a strategic portfolio review.

Key context:
- Q3 FY2026 revenue was $2.2B, down ~10% YoY
- FY2025 EPS was $13.39; FY2026 full-year consensus is ~$11.64 (down 13%)
- Beat consensus in 3 of last 4 quarters
- Beer segment (Modelo, Corona) remains the growth engine but faces deceleration
- Wine & spirits segment continues to drag overall results
- FY2027 consensus EPS of ~$12.40 implies 6.5% recovery

## Key Debates and Variants

1. Beer deceleration: Modelo Especial has been the US beer market share leader, but growth is slowing. How much is cyclical vs. structural? Is the brand reaching saturation?

2. Wine & spirits restructuring: Progress on divesting/restructuring the underperforming wine portfolio. Any update on strategic alternatives?

3. Margin trajectory: With input costs (barley, aluminum, logistics) and FX headwinds, can Constellation protect beer segment margins?

4. Capital allocation: Share buyback pace, dividend sustainability, and any M&A appetite given depressed valuation.

## What Matters This Print

1. Q4 EPS: Consensus $1.73 (down 34% YoY). Low bar but a miss would be painful.
2. Beer segment revenue growth: Key indicator -- is the deceleration stabilizing or accelerating?
3. Wine & spirits restructuring update: Any divestitures, write-downs, or strategic moves.
4. FY2027 guidance: Management outlook for the new fiscal year will set the tone. Consensus at $12.40 EPS.
5. Free cash flow: Capital allocation priorities and balance sheet health.
