### Key Narrative Heading into the Print

**1. Beer Segment: Plateauing at the Bottom?**  
Q3 FY2026 depletions fell -3.0% year-over-year — consistent with Q2's -3.6% — signaling that volume declines are moderating rather than accelerating. Management described Q4 exiting December as "roughly in line with expectations," and Christmas week delivered unusually strong sell-through. The critical question is whether the easier year-over-year compares in Q4 (lapping the initial Hispanic consumer demand shock from February 2025), combined with early signs of scanner data improvement, signal an inflection — or whether volume headwinds are structural. CEO Bill Newlands said on the Q3 call the company is "cautiously optimistic" about reaching a plateau ([Constellation Brands Q3 FY2026 Earnings Call, Jan 8, 2026](https://ir.cbrands.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/333/constellation-brands-reports-third-quarter-fiscal-2026-financial-results)).

**2. Hispanic Consumer: The Dominant Overhang**  
75% of Hispanic consumers have cited "significant concern" about the socioeconomic environment, per Constellation's proprietary omnibus survey. Immigration enforcement policy has had a pronounced, zip-code-level impact on consumption patterns — markets with >20% Hispanic population are underperforming those with <20% Hispanic representation. This is not a typical demand cycle; it is a politically contingent behavioral shift that is difficult to model. Management has leaned into price-pack architecture (7-oz cans) and adjusted pricing on Modelo Oro and Corona Premier to move down-market and capture value-oriented Hispanic consumers ([Reuters, Jan 8, 2026](https://www.reuters.com/business/retail-consumer/constellation-brands-warns-beer-sales-under-pressure-hispanic-consumers-cut-back-2026-01-08/), [eMarketer, Jan 8, 2026](https://www.emarketer.com/content/constellation-brands-immigration-crackdown-hispanic-consumers-sales)).

**3. Wine & Spirits Divestiture: Nearing Completion**  
The transformation of the Wine & Spirits business is essentially done. Constellation closed the sale of mainstream wine brands (Woodbridge, Meiomi, Robert Mondavi Private Selection, Cook's, SIMI, J. Rogét) to The Wine Group on June 2, 2025, and previously divested SVEDKA vodka. Q3 FY2026 wine & spirits net sales were only $213.1M — a 51% year-over-year decline — with the residual portfolio composed of higher-end brands (Kim Crawford, Meiomi's premium expressions, The Prisoner Wine Company, Casa Noble tequila). Management has guided wine & spirits organic operating income to decline 97–100% in FY2026, reflecting the near-complete reset. This segment is no longer a material earnings driver; the narrative going forward will be about what's left and whether it can be profitably managed ([The Spirits Business, Jan 8, 2026](https://www.thespiritsbusiness.com/2026/01/constellation-wine-and-spirits-sales-slashed-by-half/), [Constellation Brands Corporate, June 2, 2025](https://www.cbrands.com/blogs/press-releases/constellation-brands-closes-wine-transaction-with-the-wine-group-to-focus-on-a-portfolio-of-exclusively-higher-growth-higher-margin-brands)).

**4. Tariffs: Incremental but Manageable Headwind**  
President Trump's 50% Section 232 aluminum tariff (raised from 25% in June 2025) directly impacts STZ because ~41% of its Mexican beer packaging is aluminum. Management quantified the FY2026 full-year impact at ~$20 million, with Q4 carrying a disproportionate share due to (a) higher aluminum Midwest premium benchmark prices, (b) ongoing mix shift toward aluminum from glass, and (c) tariff timing — accrual upon import but P&L impact upon sale. The tariff headwind is known but the 2026 FIFA World Cup (which over-indexes Hispanic beer consumption) may provide a partial offset in FY2027 ([Bloomberg, July 2, 2025](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-07-02/constellation-sees-a-20-million-hit-from-aluminum-tariffs), [Chronicle-Journal, Jan 8, 2026](http://markets.chroniclejournal.com/chroniclejournal/article/marketminute-2026-1-8-tariffs-and-toasts-constellation-brands-beats-estimates-amid-surging-aluminum-costs)).

**5. Pacifico / Victoria: Structural Growth Offset**  
Pacifico depleted +15% and Victoria +13% in Q3 FY2026, continuing a multi-year run of double-digit gains as Modelo Especial (-4%) and Corona Extra (-9%) face headwinds. Pacifico is now #2 by volume in California, has a 1.5-point on-premise share gain in 12 months, and leads the portfolio on social media share of voice. Management's stated ambition is to double Pacifico volume and position it alongside Modelo and Corona as a third flagship. This is a real structural growth engine within the portfolio that offsets some Modelo/Corona softness ([Yahoo Finance, March 5, 2026](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/constellation-brands-seeks-growth-engine-011053035.html), [Simply Wall St, March 19, 2026](https://simplywall.st/stocks/us/food-beverage-tobacco/nyse-stz/constellation-brands/news/constellation-brands-puts-pacifico-at-center-of-2027-growth)).

## Key Debates & Variant Views

### 1. Beer Depletion Recovery: Inflection or False Bottom?

**Bull Case:**  
- Comparable periods get progressively easier through FY2027 (lapping the Feb/Mar 2025 Hispanic demand shock)
- Beer scanner data is improving entering spring, per Citi's March upgrade note
- On-premise momentum (Modelo #2 on draft vs. #5 a year ago) is not captured in scanner data — total depletions are outperforming tracked channels by ~100–150bps
- 2026 World Cup (hosted in North America) is a proven beer occasion that over-indexes the Hispanic/Latin community, directly amplifying STZ's core portfolio
- Pacifico and Victoria double-digit growth can offset Modelo/Corona softness structurally

**Bear Case (RBC, Barclays, B of A):**  
- Early 2026 data improvement may not signal a sustained trend — the Hispanic consumer is subject to policy-driven sentiment swings that are hard to forecast
- "Plateau" language from management is not the same as recovery; volume share gains in tracked channels (+0.4pp) don't compensate for category-level deceleration
- Alcohol moderation trends and competition from hard seltzer, RTD cocktails, and intoxicating hemp beverages represent secular headwinds independent of macro
- Distribution gains are increasingly difficult when velocity (sales per point of distribution) is declining

**Our View:**  
The most likely near-term path is a gradual depletion improvement in FY2027 — from approximately -3% in the back half of FY2026 toward -1% to flat — driven by easier compares and World Cup tailwind, not a fundamental recovery in Hispanic consumer sentiment. Any acceleration in immigration enforcement or deterioration in U.S.-Mexico relations (tariff escalation) could re-open the downside.

---

### 2. Beer Operating Margin: 38% Floor or Ceiling?

**Bull Case:**  
- Management's original FY2027–2028 margin target of 39–40% is achievable as volumes recover and fixed overhead absorption normalizes
- Cost savings initiatives (brewery efficiencies, procurement) are showing results — Q3 margins held at 38.0% despite volume deleverage and tariff headwinds
- Aluminum tariff drag (~$20M FY2026 total) is known and partially offset by pricing actions (+1.5% in Q3)
- Mix shift toward premium SKUs (Modelo Oro/Premier repositioning, new pack sizes) improves net revenue per case

**Bear Case (Bernstein, Morgan Stanley):**  
- Management explicitly walked back the 39–40% FY2027–2028 margin target on the Q3 call, noting "the macroeconomic environment has worsened since" April 2025 guidance — new targets will be set April 8
- Q4 FY2026 beer margins are guided to deteriorate sequentially from 38.0% to roughly 35–37%: headwinds include fixed cost deleverage (Q4 is only ~20% of annual volume), depreciation reversal as Veracruz brewery assets come online, higher aluminum pricing, and tariff timing roll-through
- Long-term capex commitment (~$1B/year in Mexico brewery expansion) creates elevated depreciation burden that will pressure margins for several years

**Q3 FY2026 Beer Margin Context:**

| Period | Beer Revenue | Beer Op. Income | Beer Op. Margin |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| FY2024 Q3 | $2,530M | $1,078M | 42.6% |
| FY2024 Q4 | $2,032M | $770M | 37.9% |
| FY2025 Q2 | $2,235M | $873M | 39.1% |
| FY2025 Q3 | $2,345M | $952M | 40.6% |
| FY2025 Q4 (=Q3 FY2026) | $2,010M | $764M | **38.0%** |

Beer margins have compressed ~250bps from peak levels, primarily from volume deleverage and tariffs. The 38% level appears durable in the near term but the path back above 40% requires volume recovery.

### 3. Wine & Spirits: Divestiture Done — What's the Residual Worth?

**Bull Case:**  
- The strategic review is effectively complete; Constellation now owns a curated portfolio of premium wine brands (Kim Crawford, The Prisoner Wine Company) and Casa Noble tequila
- Higher-end wine segment outperformed the U.S. wine industry in Q3 FY2026, per management
- Divestiture proceeds (use unclear, but partially fueling buybacks) reduce the "melting ice cube" drag on enterprise valuation
- Remaining portfolio could trade at attractive multiples if separated or sold

**Bear Case:**  
- The residual wine & spirits business is tiny (~$213M revenue in Q3) and unlikely to move the needle; dilutes management attention
- No announced timeline or buyer for remaining premium wine assets
- Q4 will see further year-over-year comparisons impacted by the ongoing portfolio reduction

---

### 4. Capital Allocation: Buybacks vs. Deleveraging

**Bull Case:**  
- Through Q3 FY2026, STZ returned ~$1.37B to shareholders ($827M in buybacks + $539M in dividends) — highly compelling yield at current market cap
- Share count reduction is accretive per-share as stock remains well below historical multiples
- FCF generation is robust ($1.45B YTD through Q3) despite elevated capex ($656M through Q3), supporting continued buyback pace

**Bear Case:**  
- Net debt remains elevated; brewery expansion in Mexico (modular buildout through FY2028 targeting +7M hectoliters) will continue to consume $1.0B+/year in capex
- A volume recovery that doesn't materialize would pressure FCF and force a choice between buybacks and deleveraging
- Dividend yield is modest (~3%) relative to the risk profile

---

### 5. Tariff Escalation: Tail Risk or Ongoing Bleed?

**Bull Case:**  
- Tariff impact is known and quantified (~$20M total FY2026); management has already priced in current 50% aluminum tariff
- Beer imported from Mexico under USMCA is tariff-exempt (only aluminum packaging is subject to Section 232) — so the full business is not at direct tariff risk
- Long-term brewery investments reduce cost basis over time

**Bear Case:**  
- Any escalation of USMCA tariffs to include the beer product itself (currently exempt) would be catastrophic — STZ's entire beer portfolio is 100% Mexico-sourced
- Political environment remains unpredictable; Trump administration has shown willingness to use tariffs as geopolitical leverage
- 50% aluminum tariff (benchmark Midwest Premium near $0.96/lb, record highs) has no domestic relief until Ball's Oklahoma smelter comes online in late 2026
- Seeking Alpha analysis (Feb 23, 2026) noted tariffs remain an issue even post-court rulings ([Seeking Alpha, Feb 23, 2026](https://seekingalpha.com/article/4873513-constellation-brands-tariffs-remain-an-issue-post-court-ruling))

## What Matters This Print (3–5 Metrics)

### 1. Beer Depletion Growth Rate  
**Why it matters:** This is the single most watched metric. Consensus expects approximately -2.5% to -3.5% (broadly in line with Q3's -3.0%). A print better than -2% would be read as an inflection signal and likely drive +5–8% stock reaction. A print worse than -4% would raise structural demand concerns.  
**Watch:** Modelo Especial trends specifically — at -4% in Q3, further deterioration vs. the overall -3.0% portfolio would be alarming given its ~60% of beer revenue.

### 2. Beer Operating Margin  
**Why it matters:** Q4 is the seasonally weakest quarter (20% of annual volume), so deleverage is expected. Management guided margin well below Q3's 38.0% — the debate is whether it lands closer to 35% (bear) or 37% (bull). The delta matters for FY2027 margin guidance credibility.  
**Watch:** Management's explicit commentary on whether aluminum tariff impact has peaked or will intensify in FY2027. Depreciation headwind from Veracruz assets is a known negative.

### 3. FY2027 Guidance — The Most Important Event of the Print  
**Why it matters:** Management explicitly deferred FY2027 and FY2028 guidance to the April earnings call (per Q3 transcript). This print contains the first formal multi-year financial targets since the April 2025 guidance was effectively abandoned when macro conditions deteriorated. Specifically:  
- Will FY2027 beer operating margin be reset below the original 39–40% target?  
- What volume growth assumption is embedded?  
- What FCF guidance is provided to frame buyback capacity vs. capex?  
The April 8 call is essentially an analyst day for FY2027 setup. This is the most meaningful new information in the print.

### 4. Wine & Spirits Divestiture Update / Remaining Portfolio Strategy  
**Why it matters:** Investors want to understand what STZ will do with the residual premium wine/Casa Noble assets — sell, hold, or invest. Any announcement of additional divestitures or a strategic partner would reduce conglomerate discount.

### 5. Free Cash Flow and Capital Return Guidance  
**Why it matters:** YTD FCF of $1.45B through Q3 is strong but Q4 FCF is expected to be modestly negative (consensus: ~-$66M) due to elevated capex timing and working capital seasonality. Full-year FCF guidance will reset expectations for the buyback pace in FY2027. Management has committed to being a consistent returner of capital, but the cadence depends on net debt trajectory and FCF conversion.

### World Cup Catalyst (FY2027 Setup)  
The 2026 FIFA World Cup (hosted across U.S., Canada, and Mexico) begins June 2026, squarely in STZ's FY2027 Q1. Modelo and Corona have historically received meaningful incremental lift during major soccer events, which over-index Hispanic viewership. STZ is not an official tournament sponsor but plans in-game media, TV campaigns, floor display activation, and promotional SKUs. This is a potential +100–200bps depletion tailwind in Q1 FY2027 that consensus models may be underweighting.
