# STZ -- Constellation Brands | Pre-Earnings Note
**Report Date:** April 8, 2026 (Q4 FY2026, ending Feb 28)  
**Conference Call:** April 9, 2026 at 8:00 AM ET  
**Updated:** March 25, 2026

## The Set-up

STZ trades at ~$152.68 (pre-market March 25), down roughly 27% from its 52-week high of $196.91 and up from its 52-week low of $126.45, implying the market has priced in a significant and sustained deterioration in the core beer franchise while finding tentative footing near what looks like trough valuation territory. At ~24x trailing P/E on depressed earnings (reported EPS of $6.37, heavily affected by non-cash items) and a forward P/E of ~12x on consensus FY2027 EPS of ~$12.40, the setup is classic "show me": the stock trades at a near-decade discount to its historical 19x average, but consensus has spent six months being revised downward. Market cap is approximately $26.6 billion against a net debt position of ~$10.5 billion (Q3 FY2026), yielding an enterprise value around $37 billion.

The path from ~$208 (early FY2026) to ~$148 tells the story clearly. The original FY2026 growth thesis — beer net sales +0-3%, reflecting Modelo's continued conquest of the domestic premium segment — began crumbling in Q1 FY2026 when Hispanic consumer spending abruptly decelerated. Management cited the combination of immigration enforcement activity (ICE deportations, detentions, and the broader chilling effect on undocumented communities), elevated unemployment within the Hispanic demographic, and general macro tightening of discretionary spending. By September 2025, the company issued a full-cycle guidance cut: beer net sales revised from +0-3% to (4)-(2)%, beer operating income from +0-2% to (9)-(7)%. The Enterprise organic net sales outlook moved to (6)-(4)%. [Comparable EPS was revised to $11.30-$11.60, down from $12.60-$12.90.](https://ir.cbrands.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/326/constellation-brands-updates-fiscal-2026-outlook)

Layered on top is a tariff environment that has added meaningful cost pressure. The administration doubled aluminum Section 232 tariffs to 50% in mid-2025, directly impacting STZ given ~41% of its Mexican beer is packaged in aluminum cans. Management estimated a ~$20 million FY2026 P&L hit from aluminum tariffs alone. There is also the specter of the broader 25% Mexico import tariff, which if applied to beer would represent an estimated $3.50/share earnings headwind on a gross basis (with partial offsets from pricing and cost actions) per [Evercore ISI analysis](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/why-mexico-trump-tariffs-could-hammer-this-beer-giant-172053291.html).

Q3 FY2026 (ended Nov 30, 2025) printed as a "beat-and-lower": revenue of $2.22B beat the $1.85B consensus substantially (partly due to seasonal timing dynamics), EPS of $3.06 beat $2.65 consensus by $0.41, [and the stock moved just +5% on the day](https://perplexity.ai/finance/STZ) — reflecting the market's focus on the negative FY2027 setup rather than Q3 execution. Beer operating margin held at 38.0% despite the volume deleverage, a result of pricing actions (+1.5% realized), cost savings programs, and a favorable depreciation timing benefit that CFO Garth Hankinson explicitly flagged would reverse into a headwind in Q4.

A notable recent development: [Constellation announced on March 13, 2026 that CEO Bill Newlands will step down and be succeeded by Nicholas Fink, effective April 13.](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/ceo-transition-amid-softer-demand-061237769.html) Fink takes the helm five days after Q4 earnings are released. Deutsche Bank characterized the appointment as neutral-to-slightly-negative, citing execution risk during a sensitive transition. The timing — right at the inflection point when Q4/FY2027 guidance sets the next 12-month narrative — adds an extra layer of uncertainty.

On the positive side of the ledger: Citi upgraded STZ to Buy on March 18, raising the price target to $175, arguing that beer volumes are approaching a cyclical trough and the April print could mark the beginning of a recovery narrative. [Citi analysts projected a return to positive depletions in Q4 and ~0.9% depletion growth over the following four quarters, aided by easier year-over-year comparisons.](https://www.tikr.com/blog/constellation-brands-nyse-stock-pops-following-citi-upgrade-to-buy) The 2026 FIFA World Cup (hosted in North America in June-July) was cited as a meaningful beer occasion that over-indexes with the Hispanic consumer base. Jefferies, meanwhile, had raised its price target to $162 (from $157) while maintaining Hold, acknowledging improving conditions but stopping short of conviction. The analyst community sits at 7 Buys, 6 Holds, and 0 Sells per current consensus, with an average price target of $165 and a high of $197 (Bernstein).

## Key Debates and Variant Perception

### 1. Beer Depletion Trajectory: Trough or Continued Deterioration?

This is the central debate. Over the course of FY2026, Modelo Especial depletions declined ~4% and Corona Extra ~9% YoY. [Total beer depletions ran at roughly (2.5)-(3)% through Q3, an improvement from the (7)-(8)% shipment declines of Q2 when distributor destocking amplified the consumer softness.](https://www.vinetur.com/en/2026011294666/constellation-brands-sales-fall-10-to-dollar222-billion-amid-steep-wine-and-spirits-decline.html) Management noted that by Q3, ships and depletes were largely re-aligned after the Q2 inventory correction.

The bull case (Citi, Needham, Bernstein) argues the worst is over. The original demand shock hit hard in February-April 2025 when ICE enforcement activity spiked; the consumer is now partially adapted, and easy year-over-year comps begin in earnest in Q1 FY2027 (March-May 2026). [Circana scanner data showed a brief improvement around Christmas 2025,](https://perplexity.ai/finance/STZ) and Bill Newlands on the Q3 call confirmed "December came in roughly where we expected." Portfolio bright spots — Pacifico (up >15%), Victoria (up >13%), Modelo Draft, Corona Non-Alcoholic — provide meaningful offsets and demonstrate the brand equity of the franchise.

The bear case (Jefferies Hold, Barclays Equal-Weight, TD Cowen Hold) argues the consumer pressure is structural, not cyclical. [Jefferies explicitly said it "underestimated the duration of demand headwinds" when downgrading in December 2025, citing a KFF-NYT immigration survey showing 22% of Hispanic immigrants personally knowing someone arrested, detained, or deported since January 2025 — nearly triple the 8% reported in April.](https://www.investing.com/news/analyst-ratings/jefferies-downgrades-constellation-brands-stock-to-hold-on-immigration-impact-93CH-4411884) If deportations and enforcement persist at current levels, the lapping argument is weaker because the ongoing macro environment for undocumented Hispanic consumers has fundamentally deteriorated. Management itself acknowledges it can see the problem in zip-code-level data: areas with >20% Hispanic representation dramatically underperform areas below that threshold. Bill Newlands: *"The performance in high Hispanic population areas is significantly worse than what is observed in the general market."*

For Q4 specifically, the seasonally weakest quarter (~20% of annual beer volume), consensus expects beer revenue of approximately $1.85B (total company Q4 consensus is ~$1.85B) and EPS of ~$1.73, down 34.2% from Q4 FY2025's $2.63. [Management's Q3 call framing implied beer shipments in Q4 would essentially track depletions, with no further destocking buffer.](https://perplexity.ai/finance/STZ)

### 2. Tariff Risk: Quantified but Not Resolved

All of STZ's beer is brewed in Mexico. This is not changing — the company's three-brewery system (Nava, Obregon, Veracruz under construction) is purpose-built for Mexican production and there is no domestic contingency. The tariff exposure has multiple layers:

- **Aluminum (50% Section 232 tariff):** ~41% of beer is in cans. The Midwest Premium for U.S. aluminum hit record highs near $0.96/lb in recent months. CFO Hankinson flagged aluminum as an incremental Q4 headwind, noting tariff timing (incurred at import, hit the P&L when inventory is sold) will create a catch-up effect in Q4. Management has guided toward "lightweighting" (less aluminum per can) and increased use of domestic scrap (largely tariff-exempt) as partial mitigants. The $20M FY2026 estimate for aluminum tariff impact has likely grown in Q4 given the ongoing can mix shift.

- **Mexico import tariff (25%):** As of the report date, the 25% tariff on Mexican goods remains a headline risk. [The timeline: tariffs were imposed in March 2025, a 90-day pause ensued, tariffs were reinstated with some modifications, and as of Q3 the company was navigating the active tariff environment.](https://stocktwits.com/news-articles/markets/equity/constellation-brands-jumps-on-us-tariff-pause/chfoCHxRbBO) STZ has emphasized it is lobbying both U.S. and Mexican governments and exploring operational mitigants, but has not guided to any full offset. Evercore ISI estimated a $3.50/share gross EPS headwind from a sustained 25% Mexico tariff, with $2.40/share after manageable offsets. With forward consensus EPS at ~$12.40 for FY2027, this tariff exposure represents a 20-30% earnings call option embedded in the stock.

- **Pricing power as mitigation:** STZ executed two pricing actions in FY2026 (spring and fall), realizing ~1.5% net price in Q3. Management reaffirmed 1-2% annual pricing guidance for FY2027. The strategic adjustment of Modelo Oro and Corona Premier pricing lower (to compete more directly with mainstream light beer) has shown early positive volume response, though it creates negative mix.

The bottom line: tariff risk is partially known and partially hedged through pricing, but any escalation — particularly an extension of the 25% Mexico tariff explicitly to beer imports — would require a meaningful FY2027 guide-down that the current consensus does not fully incorporate.

### 3. Wine & Spirits: Strategic Divestiture Largely Complete, Now a Drag on Comps

STZ spent FY2026 divesting its mainstream wine and spirits portfolio. Key completed transactions include the SVEDKA vodka divestiture (closed January 2025) and the [2025 Wine Divestitures — the sale of mainstream wine brands and associated infrastructure to The Wine Group, which closed June 2, 2025.](https://ir.cbrands.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/321/constellation-brands-closes-wine-transaction-with-the-wine-group-to-focus-on-a-portfolio-of-exclusively-higher-margin-brands) These removed ~$711M of net sales and ~$245M of gross profit less marketing from the FY2026 comparable base, explaining the massive organic W&S decline of (20)-(17)% and operating income collapse of (100)-(97)%.

Going forward, STZ retains a "higher-growth, higher-margin" wine portfolio skewed toward $15+ premium brands. For Q4, this segment is structurally noise relative to Q4 FY2025 comps (large divested asset base). The proceeds from these divestitures were deployed into share repurchases: STZ executed $604M in buybacks in H1 FY2026 alone under its 3-year $4B authorization. Debt has declined from ~$12.1B (Feb 2025) to ~$10.7B (Nov 2025), though leverage remains elevated.

**Variant perception:** Bulls see the W&S simplification as a long-term value unlock — STZ is now essentially a pure-play premium beer franchise with cleaner margins and higher returns on deployed capital. Bears note that the divestiture proceeds were used for buybacks while the balance sheet still carries ~$10.5B in net debt, leaving limited financial flexibility if the beer recovery takes longer than expected or tariff escalation materializes.

### 4. Valuation: Trough Multiple vs. Structural Re-rating Lower

At ~12x forward earnings, STZ is priced at a near-decade low vs. its historical 19x average, and below the beverage peer group. The bull case mathematically argues this is straightforward value: if beer depletions stabilize at flat-to-+1%, FY2027 EPS returns to $12+, margins expand back toward the guided 39-40% range, and free cash flow supports continued buybacks from the $4B authorization, the stock re-rates toward $175-$200 — implying 15-30% upside from current levels.

[The bear case notes that Jefferies explicitly said current fundamentals "lack support for an inflection" and that the business may be in a structurally slower growth regime.](https://www.investing.com/news/analyst-ratings/jefferies-downgrades-constellation-brands-stock-to-hold-on-immigration-impact-93CH-4411884) The 10-year average multiple includes the Modelo conquest growth era (2015-2023), when Modelo was taking 50+ bps of dollar share per year. That era may be over; the brand is now the #1 beer in the U.S. by dollars with 20% fewer points of distribution than domestic competitors — there is still whitespace, but the velocity tailwind is spent.

The forward P/E at ~12x also embeds a question: what is the earnings baseline? If FY2027 guidance disappoints (e.g., beer revenue down again vs. consensus expecting recovery), EPS could be $10-11 rather than $12.40, pushing the effective multiple to 14-15x with a weaker growth trajectory — not as cheap as it appears.

## What Matters This Print (Five Key Metrics)

### 1. Beer Depletions and Shipment Volume
The single most watched number. Q4 FY2025 beer depletions were approximately (2)-(3)%. For Q4 FY2026, the bull thesis (Citi) calls for depletions turning positive. The bear case expects continued mid-single-digit declines. **Watch:** whether the YoY trend on depletions is better or worse than Q3's (2.5)-(3)%, and whether management frames the cadence as "improving," "stable," or still "volatile." Any commentary on distributor inventory levels is critical — if distributors have been managing to lean, the Q4 depletion-to-shipment relationship could be slightly favorable. Also watch: Pacifico and Victoria growth rates as leading indicators of the portfolio's ability to offset Modelo/Corona softness.

### 2. Beer Gross Margin and Operating Margin
Q3 delivered a 38.0% beer operating margin — remarkable given the volume deleverage, but CFO Hankinson pre-warned Q4 would be worse due to: (a) seasonal volume absorption (20% of annual volume, maximum fixed-cost deleverage), (b) incremental aluminum tariff timing, (c) depreciation reversing from Q3 benefit to Q4 headwind as Veracruz brewery assets come online, and (d) ongoing product mix shift toward cans. [Consensus implies beer gross margins in Q4 of ~47%, materially below the ~53% Q3 level.](https://perplexity.ai/finance/STZ) If actual margins are above the low-bar consensus, that is the first signal that the Q3 cost structure was more durable than feared. If margins miss, it validates the bear case that tariff pass-through is incomplete and structural margin compression is underway.

### 3. FY2027 Guidance: The Critical Re-rating Catalyst
This is the most important deliverable of the entire print. Management explicitly deferred FY2027 guidance to the April earnings call (per Q3 call: *"the guidance that we provided last April was given under a different set of macroeconomic conditions, and the macroeconomic environment has worsened since that time"*). The FY2027 guidance will effectively set the earnings multiple for the next 12 months. Key sub-questions:
- Does beer revenue guidance return to growth (even +0-2%), or does management guide to another year of decline?
- What beer operating margin framework does management provide for FY2027 and FY2028? The original 39-40% targets look challenged given tariff headwinds.
- How does management quantify the tariff impact in FY2027 guidance, and what mitigation measures are embedded?
- Free cash flow guidance for FY2027 (original FY2026 guidance was $1.3-1.4B, revised down from $1.5-1.6B) — is the $4B 3-year buyback program still fully on track?

A "guidance beat" here (i.e., beer guidance at the higher end, margins better than feared, FCF above $1.5B) would be the catalyst for the +8-12% move in the bull case. A guidance miss would confirm the bear narrative.

### 4. Free Cash Flow, Debt Reduction, and Capital Allocation
Through Q3 FY2026, the company generated cumulative FCF of approximately $1.45B ($444M Q1 + $635M Q2 + $371M Q3), tracking toward the low end of the $1.3-$1.4B full-year guidance. [Net debt sits at ~$10.5B as of Q3, down from ~$12B at the start of FY2026, primarily from wine divestiture proceeds deployed toward debt and buybacks.](https://perplexity.ai/finance/STZ) Q4 FCF is typically negative seasonally (low volume, heavy CapEx for brewery construction). **Watch:** whether FY2027 CapEx guidance increases materially as Veracruz Phase 1 spending ramps, and how management frames the buyback pace given the leverage level. Any guidance suggesting they are pulling back on the $4B authorization would be a negative.

### 5. Hispanic Consumer Sentiment and Leading Indicators
Bill Newlands will almost certainly be asked about the latest zip-code data and the trajectory of Hispanic consumer spending. **Watch for:** any signal that the February-April 2025 shock is truly being lapped with less severe underlying trends, whether immigration enforcement activity has stabilized or intensified in recent months, and any commentary on early-Q4 Circana/Nielsen data through February 2026. The World Cup narrative — does management formally commit to elevated marketing spend and cite it as a volume catalyst for H1 FY2027? An explicit endorsement of the World Cup as a demand driver, with quantitative framing, would be incrementally bullish.

## Additional Context and Recent Developments

**CEO Transition Dynamics:** Nicholas Fink becomes CEO on April 13, making this Newlands' final earnings call and the first Q&A where investors will be looking for strategic directional signals from an incoming CEO who has not yet formally stepped in. This creates a potentially confusing dynamic — Newlands will present, but the institutional investment community will be parsing every word for what Fink intends to do differently. Fink's board-level experience and prior operating background will be scrutinized. Historically, incoming CEOs at distressed-multiple consumer staples names often get a 12-month honeymoon period if they can credibly articulate a recovery plan.

**Mexican Beer Category Context:** Per [Beverage Industry's 2026 Beer Market Report](https://www.bevindustry.com/articles/98128-2026-beer-market-report-imported-beer-contends-with-tough-comps-as-year-wrapped), Mexican beer was down 1.7% in dollars in 2025 after years of category leadership. This is a category-level headwind, not solely a STZ-specific problem. Heineken USA (Dos Equis, Tecate) and Molson Coors (Sol) have reported similar trends. The category-level decline context supports the recovery case: once macro conditions for Hispanic consumers improve, the entire Mexican premium segment should re-accelerate, and STZ holds the dominant brands (Modelo, Corona, Pacifico) to capture disproportionate share of that recovery.

**GLP-1 / Alcohol Secular Demand:** Piper Sandler recently lowered its price target citing GLP-1 weight loss drug adoption as an incremental secular headwind to alcohol consumption. While this is a real structural concern for the long-term, it is unlikely to be a near-term Q4 catalyst discussion item. The bigger debate is whether the cyclical headwinds (immigration, macro stress on Hispanic consumers, tariffs) are masking a stock that is genuinely cheap on a normalized basis.

**CapEx and Brewery Expansion:** Management reiterated on the Q3 call that the Veracruz expansion (7 million additional hectoliters through FY2028) will continue on its "modular" build cadence. However, Garth Hankinson acknowledged that long-lead equipment orders make short-term CapEx deferrals difficult. FY2026 CapEx is tracking above the ~$700-750M range due to brewery construction timing. Any FY2027 CapEx guidance above $750M while volumes remain depressed will be challenged by the analyst community as premature capacity build.

**Tariff Political Developments:** As of March 25, 2026, the broader 25% Mexico import tariff framework remains in effect with ongoing negotiations. [Seeking Alpha noted in late February 2026 that tariffs "remain an issue post-court ruling,"](https://seekingalpha.com/article/4873513-constellation-brands-tariffs-remain-an-issue-post-court-ruling) suggesting legal challenges have not provided relief. Any progress on a Mexico trade negotiation — which would reduce the specific tariff exposure on beer/aluminum — would be a significant positive catalyst outside the earnings print itself.

## Our View

The risk/reward at $152 is asymmetric enough to maintain the position into the print, but with tight position sizing. The forward P/E at ~12x represents a genuine discount to intrinsic value *if* the beer business stabilizes. The problem is that "if" is doing a lot of work.

The key incremental information this print will add: whether the depletion trend entered Q4 on a stable or deteriorating trajectory, and how management frames the FY2027 baseline under a still-uncertain tariff and immigration enforcement backdrop. Citi's upgrade last week suggests at least one major firm has visibility (or conviction) that the scanner data through February has been constructive. If they are right, the stock will trade toward $170-180 on a credible guide. If the bear case materializes — further volume deterioration, guide-down, new CEO without a clear recovery script — the stock tests $130.

The World Cup is real optionality that the market is not fully pricing. A genuine depletion inflection in the summer months could make Q1 FY2027 the quarter that breaks the negative streak. That catalyst is four months away; the April 8 print is the bridge.

**Position sizing note:** Given the CEO transition risk (Fink takes over five days post-earnings), the pending FY2027 guidance uncertainty, and the unresolved tariff overhang, entering into the print with a full-sized position requires high conviction on the base case. A half-sized position that can be built on the guide seems prudent.
