## Key Debates and Variants

1. **Beer depletions and volume health**: Modelo Especial is the #1 beer by volume in the US. The central debate is whether depletion trends (which lag shipments) are tracking at 5-7% growth or decelerating to 3-4%. Beer net sales guided to decline 2-4% for FY2026 (negative mix shift as portfolio premiumizes). Volume versus pricing is the key decomposition — pricing power holding but volume softness would signal broader consumer trade-down. Off-premise depletions were running below scanner data in Q3 helped by on-premise strength; Q4 (Feb quarter, winter) is seasonally soft. **Pacifico and Victoria brand expansion** is the incremental growth vector; watch for distribution gain updates.

2. **Tariff exposure -- Mexico beer is the entire moat**: 100% of STZ's beer is brewed in Mexico. With US-Mexico trade tensions and tariff escalation risk, the market wants a clear articulation of hedging strategy, cost pass-through ability, and multi-year CapEx plans for potential US brewing capacity. Any commentary signaling tariff headwinds above $100M/year would be a major negative. The ceasefire news on April 7 (Iran-focused, not Mexico) does not directly change the USMCA tariff calculus, but a constructive market tone at open slightly reduces volatility risk into the print.

3. **CEO transition narrative -- Nicholas Fink's opening remarks**: Fink joins as CEO on April 13 (just 5 days after the earnings report). His opening remarks on the April 9 call will be intensely parsed for strategic direction: Does he validate beer-only focus? Does he signal capital allocation shift (dividends, buybacks vs. debt reduction)? Does he provide a fresh framework for FY2027+ that the Street can build models around? He comes from Fortune Brands Innovations (security/digital products) — a non-beverage background that the market will scrutinize. The April 9 call is essentially his public debut.

4. **FY2027 guidance and capital allocation**: The first FY2027 guidance issued under/alongside Fink will be the most important datapoint. FY2027 consensus: EPS ~$14.00-14.95 (various sources). Finance estimates show Q1 FY2027 consensus EPS of $3.93 vs. Q1 FY2026 actual of $3.74 — implying +5.1% EPS growth resumption. Any guidance below $13.50 would disappoint; above $15.00 would be a positive catalyst. Management at Q3 warned that FY2027/2028 margin expectations are "under review" given the less favorable macro backdrop — this creates downside scenario risk.

5. **W&S wind-down -- write-down and structural drag removal**: The Wine & Spirits segment is expected to generate near-zero operating income this quarter (consensus: $2.4M). Once fully divested, this removes a persistent drag. Investors want a clear timetable and any update on residual asset sales or restructuring charges. The collapse in W&S (-57.6% revenue YoY) is well-telegraphed and largely a non-event for sentiment — the focus is entirely on beer.

## FINAL PRE-PRINT SUMMARY — Wednesday April 8, 2026

**2 days to print. Here is what matters most:**

### Top 3 Things to Watch Wednesday Night

**1. FY2027 Guidance — the entire bull thesis lives here.**
The stock is at 12x forward P/E on FY2026 earnings that reflect a transition year of divestitures and margin compression. The re-rating story requires FY2027 EPS guidance that confirms the beer recovery: specifically >$14.50 comparable EPS and any commentary on beer OI margin returning to the 38-40% range. Evercore ISI's Tactical Outperform thesis is premised on top-line upside driving operating leverage into FY2027. If guidance disappoints (below $13.50), the tactical call fails immediately.

**2. Nicholas Fink's opening remarks — his debut shapes the next 12 months of positioning.**
Fink is not a beverage industry veteran — he comes from Fortune Brands Innovations (home security, smart locks). Institutional investors will parse every word for: (a) does he endorse the beer-only strategic focus, (b) what is his capital allocation philosophy (buybacks/dividends vs. debt reduction given $10.3B in long-term debt), (c) does he signal any strategic review or M&A appetite, (d) does he have a credible 100-day plan. A vague, high-level speech with no specifics will disappoint. A crisp, operations-focused message with quantitative commitments will be rewarded. The April 9 conference call at 8:00 AM ET is the key event — results land Wednesday night but Fink speaks Thursday morning.

**3. Beer depletion vs. shipment dynamics — the volume quality test.**
Management guided in Q3 that "depletes and ships will be basically equal" over Q3+Q4 combined. The Q3 report showed minor variation; Q4 should normalize. Any statement that Q4 shipments trailed depletions materially (restocking or distributor destocking) flags inventory issues. The Modelo-specific consumer health commentary — particularly regarding Hispanic consumer trends (which drove the September 2025 guidance cut) — is the demand signal the market is most sensitive to. The World Cup begins in 2026 (North America host) — any positive sentiment from management about the World Cup tailwind for Modelo/Corona volumes would be a notable upside framing.

### Key Risk Asymmetries

- **Zacks ESP -1.59% = slight bearish lean** from most-accurate analyst estimates vs. consensus. The model does not predict a beat. Combined with Zacks Rank #3 (Hold), the quantitative signal is cautious.
- **Beat history supports upside**: 8.6% avg beat over last 4 quarters, including +15% in Q3. STZ has a pattern of managing expectations conservatively into the print.
- **Ceasefire tailwind is marginal but real**: Brent down from $115 to $108.42. Lower crude = lower aluminum cost expectations, slightly better consumer sentiment for discretionary purchases like premium beer. Not a major driver, but directionally positive.
- **April 7 macro open is favorable**: S&P +0.3% on ceasefire hopes. Consumer staples could outperform early in the session. A rising tide into Wednesday supports the setup.
- **Wide analyst PT spread ($135 Piper Sandler to $197 Bernstein)** reflects genuine disagreement on the FY2027 recovery thesis. The print will compress this spread dramatically in one direction.

### Positioning Summary
The risk/reward at $151 heading into the print is **approximately balanced with a slight bull lean** from Evercore's tactical call and the ceasefire macro improvement. The bear case (-8-12%) is fully in play given Zacks ESP and the Q4 structural headwinds (aluminum, depreciation, W&S drag). The bull case (+8-12%) is achievable if beer depletions hold and Fink impresses. The most likely outcome (50% base) is a small move in either direction as the market digests a messy quarter (W&S collapse, beer stable) and waits for a cleaner read on FY2027.
