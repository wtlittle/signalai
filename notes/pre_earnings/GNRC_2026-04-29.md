## 2. Key Debates and Variants

**Debate 1 — Backlog conversion timing and hyperscaler purchase order visibility**
This is the single most important debate. The $700M backlog is largely weighted toward 2027 deliveries, meaning Q1 2026 C&I data center revenue will be modest. The bull case holds that Generac will progressively receive binding hyperscaler purchase orders as it clears AVL (approved vendor list) qualification, validating the backlog and expanding it further. The bear case argues that the absence of a binding hyperscaler PO at the March Investor Day was a near-term negative, and that competition from Caterpillar, Cummins, and MTU (Rolls-Royce) may limit GNRC's hyperscaler penetration.

**Debate 2 — Residential recovery trajectory**
Management guided mid-teens home standby growth for FY2026, contingent on a "return to more normal outage levels." Outage hours in 2H 2025 were the lowest in a decade, creating an extremely easy comp. However, residential recovery is inherently binary and weather-dependent — a quiet storm season could push the home standby ramp into 2027. The new lead distribution system tested during January 2026's Winter Storm Fern showed improved dealer close rates, a structural positive.

**Debate 3 — Margin recovery credibility**
FY2025 Adjusted EBITDA margins were 17% on depressed volumes. Consensus estimates FY2026 EBITDA at ~$895M, implying margins of approximately 21% on ~$4.2B blended revenue. The Enercon Engineering switchgear acquisition (expected to close Q2 2026) adds >100 bps to C&I segment margins through vertical integration. The question is whether manufacturing absorption from the new Sussex, WI facility (targeting $1B+ domestic large-generator capacity by Q4 2026) materializes on schedule.

**Where consensus may be wrong:** Quarterly EPS estimates appear to embed relatively conservative C&I data center revenue — the Q1 consensus of $1.33 EPS implies a modest improvement from Q4's $1.61 (but Q4 included inventory/supplier settlement charges). The actual bridge from Q4 to Q1 involves: (a) significantly reduced residential volume given weak Q4 comp now partially lapping, (b) continued data center C&I ramp, and (c) seasonal sequential softness typical for Q1. The $1.33 estimate may understate C&I acceleration if backlog conversions began shipping early.
