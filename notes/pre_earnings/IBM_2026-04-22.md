## The Set-up

IBM enters Q1 2026 riding the strongest momentum in over a decade. FY2025 delivered $67.54B revenue (+7.6% YoY), $10.59B net income (+76% YoY), and $14.7B free cash flow -- a decade high. Management guided FY2026 to >5% constant-currency revenue growth and $15.7B FCF, above consensus expectations. The GenAI book of business stands at $12.5B cumulative (up from $9.5B QoQ), with software comprising >$2B and consulting >$10.5B. Q4 2025 was remarkable: revenue up 12% YoY (9% CC), software up 11%, infrastructure up 17% (Z17 mainframe cycle), and consulting grew modestly but with AI bookings accelerating. The stock has outperformed the S&P 500 by ~20 percentage points YTD, reflecting the successful pivot to a software-led hybrid cloud and AI platform company.

**Macro context (Slowdown regime):** Growth contracting, policy restrictive. IBM is well-positioned as a defensive tech name with recurring revenue (ARR $23.6B) and counter-cyclical consulting demand (enterprises invest in automation and cost-reduction during slowdowns). The weak DXY ($98.59) provides a FX tailwind for IBM's ~60% international revenue.

## Key Debates and Variants

1. **Software sustainability:** Can software maintain 10%+ growth in Q1 after Q4's 11%? Red Hat guided mid-single-digit growth -- any deceleration below 5% would concern investors. Conversely, AI-driven demand for watsonx and automation could sustain double-digit software growth.

2. **Consulting growth inflection:** Consulting grew only ~3% in Q4. Management expects GenAI to be a consulting accelerator. Q1 will test whether the $3.6B GenAI ARR in consulting translates to revenue acceleration or whether macro headwinds are slowing enterprise spending.

3. **Z17 mainframe cycle:** Q4 infrastructure was up 17% on Z17 strength, outpacing the Z16 cycle. Q1 typically sees strong carryover from a new mainframe launch. Any deceleration below 10% would raise questions about cycle longevity.

4. **Confluent acquisition integration:** IBM announced the acquisition of Confluent as a data-layer play. Integration costs and revenue contribution timing are unknowns. Market will watch for any deal-related charges or delays.

5. **FCF trajectory:** $15.7B FY2026 guide is ambitious given $14.7B in FY2025. Q1 FCF is seasonally weak -- the path to $15.7B depends on margin expansion and continued operational leverage.
