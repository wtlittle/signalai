# Morgan Stanley (MS) -- Pre-Earnings Note
**Earnings Date:** April 15, 2026 (~7:30am ET) | **Quarter:** Q1 FY2026 | **Days Until Report:** 10

---

## Set-up

MS closed at $165.77 on Apr 2, down approximately **8.9% YTD** from its Jan 2 close of $181.90, and nearly 14% off its 52-week high of $192.68. The 52-week range is $94.33–$192.68. P/E of ~16.3x trailing EPS ($10.20 TTM). Market cap: ~$263B.

MS reports on Apr 15, the day after JPM/C/WFC — which means the print will be interpreted in the context of the tone those three banks set. As of Apr 2, the stock has already absorbed a significant de-rating from its January highs, reflecting: (1) broad market anxiety about the tariff/Iran stagflation scenario; (2) AUM compression (equity market weakness in Q1 depresses Wealth Management fee accruals); and (3) softer-than-expected IB fee recovery expectations given the deal freeze.

MS's Q4 2025 was a blowout: $17.89B in revenues (+1% vs. $17.74B consensus) and EPS of $2.68 (beat $2.41 by $0.27), sending the stock up 5.8% — the best post-earnings reaction in the peer group. CEO Ted Pick highlighted the strength of the Institutional Securities trading franchise and early momentum in Investment Management. Q1 2025 actual was EPS $2.60 on revenues of $16.52B (slight revenue miss, solid EPS beat).

The macro context is a double-edged sword for MS. The Institutional Securities segment (trading + IB) should benefit materially from elevated volatility in Q1 — rate vol, equity vol, FX vol, commodity dislocations from Iran. MS's equity franchise is historically top-3 globally, and the massive investor rotation out of US tech into international and value in Q1 2026 created exceptional flow volumes for cash equities and prime brokerage. Conversely, MS's Wealth Management segment (~$6-7B quarterly revenue) is sensitive to equity market AUM levels — the Q1 equity market correction will have modestly compressed fee-based AUM. The ISG vs. Wealth dynamic is the central intra-quarter tension.

MS's CEO Ted Pick and strategy is explicitly focused on the "three businesses, one firm" model — Institutional Securities, Wealth Management, and Investment Management all working together. The Q1 environment tests whether the integration is additive.

---

## Key Debates and Variants

- **Wealth Management fee revenues — AUM compression vs. fee-rate stability:** MS's Wealth Management (WM) business generated ~$7.3B in Q4 2025 revenues. A portion of WM revenues are asset-sensitive (fee-based advisory accounts charged on AUM). With US equity markets declining meaningfully in Q1 (S&P 500 off ~10-15% from its February peak, though the quarter began strong), average AUM in Q1 was likely lower than Q4 2025 peaks. The debate: is WM revenue declining meaningfully (bear case) or are net new money flows and fixed fee structures insulating the revenue base?

- **Equity trading: the prime brokerage positioning question:** MS's equity franchise is where the most upside surprise could come. The Jan-March rotation — from US mega-cap tech (long positioning) to international, value, and defensive sectors — created massive flow volumes through prime brokerage, cash equities, and derivatives. MS's equity revenue was $3.3B in Q4 2025 and $2.8B in Q1 2025. A Q1 2026 print above $3.5B would be material upside. The risk: prime brokerage clients (hedge funds) that were caught long tech at the DeepSeek shock in January or long US assets into the tariff shock faced significant drawdowns, and deleveraging can reduce prime revenues.

- **FICC trading — rates and FX volatility monetized?** MS's FICC franchise ($2.18B in Q2 2025) is smaller than JPM/Citi/GS but still significant. The Iran-war-driven rate volatility and FX dislocations should have been positive for MS's macro and rates desks. The question is whether MS's relatively smaller fixed income book can generate enough flow revenue to offset any weakness in credit and securitization desks.

- **Investment Banking fees — advisory freeze impact:** MS has been rebuilding its IB franchise under Ted Pick. Management guided for an "M&A super-cycle" narrative at the start of 2026. The tariff/geopolitical freeze in Q1 disrupted this: advisory revenues likely disappointed the optimistic start-of-year guide. Watch for any clarity on pipeline conversion in Q2. DCM should have been reasonable given refinancing activity.

- **Investment Management (IM) — AUM flows and fee pressure:** MS's IM business is smaller than BlackRock but growing. In a volatile equity market with investors rotating into alternatives and fixed income, IM revenue dynamics depend on which asset classes are gaining inflows. Alternatives and fixed income are likely to have attracted capital, partly offsetting equity fund fee compression.

- **Capital returns — ROTCE and buyback pace:** MS targets 20%+ ROTCE in normalized conditions. Q4 2025 ROTCE was ~18%. Q1 2026 should track similarly if trading is strong. Buyback pace and any commentary on capital priorities is a secondary watch item.

## What Matters This Print

- **EPS:** $2.92 consensus (range: $2.60–$3.25). Q1 2025 actual was $2.60. The +12.3% YoY growth bar reflects assumed WM revenue growth and trading strength, partially offset by AUM compression.
- **Net Revenue:** $19.56B consensus. Q4 2025 actual was $17.89B; Q1 2025 was $16.52B. A ~18% YoY step-up from Q1 2025 levels is implied — high bar driven by trading.
- **Institutional Securities Revenues:** ISG revenues consensus ~$8.5-9.5B (trading + IB combined). FICC ~$2.3-2.6B; Equities ~$3.4-3.8B; IB fees ~$1.8-2.0B.
- **Wealth Management Revenues:** Consensus ~$7.0-7.5B. Q4 2025 was ~$7.3B. Watch for any fee rate compression from AUM decline and NNA (net new assets) trend.
- **Investment Management Revenues:** Consensus ~$1.5-1.8B. AUM flows and fee structure are key.
- **Provision for Credit Losses:** Relatively small for MS (~$100-200M). Not a primary swing factor.
- **CET1 Ratio:** ~15.5-16.0% expected. MS has been generating substantial capital; buyback cadence is a watch item.
- **ROTCE:** Target 20%+; Q1 should show ~18-20% depending on WM fee compression.
