## Key Debates and Variants

- **Wealth Management fee revenues — AUM compression vs. net new money strength:** MS's WM business generated ~$7.3B in Q4 2025 revenues. Q1 equity market weakness (S&P 500 off ~10–15% from its February peak) compressed average AUM levels. However, industry ETF/mutual fund inflows of $309B (double year-ago per Cyprys) suggest that net new money flows have been unusually strong — potentially partially offsetting market-level compression in fee revenues. The debate: is WM revenue declining meaningfully (bear case) or are strong NNA flows and fixed fee structures insulating the revenue base? The ceasefire-driven market stabilization on Apr 7 is a mild positive for WM AUM trajectory into Q2.

- **Equity trading: the prime brokerage positioning question:** MS's equity franchise is top-3 globally. The Jan–March rotation — from US mega-cap tech to international, value, and defensive — created massive flow volumes through prime brokerage, cash equities, and derivatives. MS's equity revenue was $3.3B in Q4 2025 and $2.8B in Q1 2025. A Q1 2026 print above $3.5B would be material upside. The risk: prime brokerage clients (hedge funds) caught long tech at the DeepSeek shock or long US assets into the tariff shock faced significant drawdowns, and deleveraging can reduce prime revenues.

- **FICC trading — rates and FX volatility monetized?** MS's FICC franchise ($2.18B in Q2 2025) is smaller than JPM/Citi/GS but still significant. Iran-war-driven rate volatility and FX dislocations should have been positive for MS's macro and rates desks. The question is whether MS's relatively smaller fixed income book can generate enough flow revenue to offset any weakness in credit and securitization desks.

- **Investment Banking fees — advisory freeze impact:** MS has been rebuilding its IB franchise under Ted Pick. The tariff/geopolitical freeze in Q1 disrupted the "M&A super-cycle" narrative from start-of-year. Advisory revenues likely disappointed the optimistic guide. DCM should have been reasonable given refinancing activity. Watch for any clarity on pipeline conversion in Q2 — the ceasefire narrative could accelerate this.

- **Investment Management (IM) — AUM flows and fee pressure:** MS Research's own sector note cut IM estimates (e.g., BlackRock –11.9% Q1 EPS), but noted ETF/mutual fund inflows of $309B — more than double year-ago. The flow environment is unusually strong even as market levels are lower, which is a relative positive for MS's IM revenues. Alternatives and fixed income attracted capital; equity fund fee compression partial offset.

- **Capital returns — ROTCE and buyback pace:** MS targets 20%+ ROTCE in normalized conditions. Q4 2025 ROTCE was ~18%. Q1 2026 should track similarly if trading is strong. Buyback pace and any commentary on capital priorities is a secondary watch item. FY2026 consensus EPS of $11.12 implies meaningful earnings power to support ongoing buybacks.

## What Matters This Print

- **EPS:** $2.92 consensus (Barchart/Moomoo; range $2.60–$3.25). Q1 2025 actual was $2.60. The +12.3% YoY growth bar reflects assumed WM revenue growth and trading strength, partially offset by AUM compression.
- **Net Revenue:** $18.99B consensus (Moomoo; Barchart ~$19.56B range). Q4 2025 actual was $17.89B; Q1 2025 was $16.52B. A ~15–18% YoY step-up from Q1 2025 levels.
- **Institutional Securities Revenues:** ISG revenues consensus ~$8.5–9.5B (trading + IB combined). FICC ~$2.3–2.6B; Equities ~$3.4–3.8B; IB fees ~$1.8–2.0B.
- **Wealth Management Revenues:** Consensus ~$7.0–7.5B. Q4 2025 was ~$7.3B. Watch for fee rate compression from AUM decline and NNA (net new assets) trend. Industry ETF inflows of $309B suggest NNA could be strong despite market level compression.
- **Investment Management Revenues:** Consensus ~$1.5–1.8B. AUM flows and fee structure are key. MS Research's BlackRock cut is a sector signal but MS's own IM mix may differ.
- **Provision for Credit Losses:** Relatively small for MS (~$100–200M). Not a primary swing factor.
- **CET1 Ratio:** ~15.5–16.0% expected. MS has been generating substantial capital; buyback cadence is a watch item.
- **ROTCE:** Target 20%+; Q1 should show ~18–20% depending on WM fee compression.
- **WM Net New Assets (NNA):** A positive NNA figure (especially if >$50B quarterly) would be a significant re-rating catalyst for the long-term WM fee revenue story.
