# JPMorgan Chase & Co. (JPM) -- Pre-Earnings Note
**Earnings Date:** April 14, 2026 (BMO) | **Quarter:** Q1 FY2026 | **Days Until Report:** 9

---

## Set-up

JPM closed at $294.60 on Apr 2 (after-hours), down approximately **9.5% YTD** from its Jan 2 close of $325.48 and nearly 13% off its 52-week high of $337.25. The stock is trading at ~14.7x trailing EPS ($20.03 TTM). Market cap: ~$795B. The 52-week range is $202.16–$337.25, and JPM has meaningfully underperformed its YTD peak — reflecting market anxiety about the tariff/Iran stagflation scenario that directly challenges bank NII and credit quality outlooks.

JPM's Q4 2025 print (Jan 13, 2026) was mixed: revenues of $45.80B slightly missed the $46.17B consensus, while EPS of $5.23 beat the $5.01 estimate by $0.22. The stock sold off 4.2% on the day despite the beat, reflecting: (1) Dimon's cautious macro commentary on tariff uncertainty; (2) NII guidance of $103B for FY2026 (some had hoped for upside revision); and (3) expense guidance of $105B for 2026 (up >9% YoY), the key overhang. Management guided IB fees to grow mid-to-high teens YoY, but that was before the February-March tariff escalation and Iran war spiral.

JPM is the first "must-watch" print of Q1 earnings season given its size, breadth, and Dimon's macro commentary function. Markets were closed Apr 3-5 (Good Friday); Monday Apr 7 opens with Liberation Day tariff fallout undigested. The macro setup — Brent ~$115/bbl, VIX ~28, 10Y UST yields elevated but volatile, gold at $4,910, deflation risk regime — is a mixed bag for JPM: FICC trading should be strong (JPM's COO guided to "low double digits" trading growth in mid-February), but recession probability is rising (Dimon flagged 50-50 odds in Q1 commentary), and IB fee conversion was disrupted by the deal freeze. The key narrative is whether JPM's fortress balance sheet and diversification let it shine when macro uncertainty is highest — the "flight to quality" bank.

---

## Key Debates and Variants

- **NII trajectory — the $95B ex-markets core NII guide:** JPM guided to ~$95B core NII (ex-markets) and ~$103B total NII for 2026, assuming two Fed rate cuts. With cuts now pushed out (futures pricing only one 2026 cut, 48% odds of zero cuts per Morningstar), the deposit margin compression thesis has reversed — NII should benefit from higher-for-longer. Bulls argue JPM will raise 2026 NII guidance at this print; bears worry about deposit repricing costs and loan growth softening as corporates defer capex/M&A in a tariff environment.

- **IB fees: January window vs. March paralysis:** JPM guided IB fees +mid-to-high teens YoY going into Q1. January saw strong deal activity; February and March froze as tariff escalation and Iran war disrupted CEO confidence. Dimon himself said "boardrooms are on pause." Advisory fees could disappoint the guide while DCM/bond underwriting held up better given rate-driven refinancing demand. The question is net IB fee number: consensus is ~$2.2-2.4B for the CIB IB fee line (vs. ~$2.0B in Q1 2025).

- **Trading revenues — can the CIB deliver?** JPM's COO Jennifer Piepszak guided "low double digit" trading revenue growth in mid-February, when the bar was ~$8-9B. With FICC boosted by Iran/rate volatility and equities driven by massive portfolio rotation, the actual figure could be meaningfully above guidance. FICC consensus is ~$5.5-6.0B (Q2 2025 actual was $5.7B; Q4 2025 was ~$5.0B). The bull case for trading is JPM's single biggest swing factor.

- **Credit quality — is the consumer credit cycle turning?** JPM saw modest reserve releases in recent quarters, but net charge-offs in consumer cards have been normalizing. With Dimon citing 50-50 recession odds, the buy-side watches provision for credit losses closely. Any incremental reserve build above $3.5B would read as a signal that JPM's credit team sees deteriorating conditions.

- **Expenses: $105B guide for FY2026 — can Q1 stay below $27B?** Expense discipline (or lack thereof) has been the persistent JPM bear thesis. In Q4 2025, expenses came in high. Q1 compensation accruals (bonus accruals based on strong trading) could push expenses toward the upper end. Efficiency ratio watch: JPM targets <60% for 2026.

## What Matters This Print

- **EPS:** $5.37–5.50 consensus (range: $5.32–$5.74). Q1 2025 actual was $5.07. The ~7% YoY growth bar is achievable if trading is strong and credit holds.
- **Net Revenue:** $48.61B consensus. Q4 2025 was $45.80B; Q1 2025 was $45.33B. The Q1 step-up is meaningful and trading-driven.
- **Net Interest Income (NII):** ~$23.5-24.5B quarterly implied (FY guide $103B / 4 + markets NII). Watch for any upward revision to the $103B FY2026 NII guide — a raise to $104-105B would be a meaningful positive given higher-for-longer rate expectations.
- **CIB Trading Revenue (FICC + Equities):** Total trading consensus ~$9.5-10.5B. FICC ~$5.5-6.0B; Equities ~$3.8-4.2B. Any combined print above $10.5B would exceed even the "low double digit" growth guide and drive significant upside.
- **Investment Banking Fees (Advisory + ECM + DCM):** Consensus ~$2.2-2.4B. Q1 2025 was ~$2.0B. The mid-to-high teens growth guide implies ~$2.3-2.4B.
- **Provision for Credit Losses:** Consensus ~$3.2-3.5B. Q4 2025 was ~$3.3B. Watch for reserve build signal on recession risk.
- **CET1 Ratio:** ~15.5% expected. JPM has been building capital; watch for any commentary on accelerating buybacks or dividend increases.
- **ROTCE:** JPM targets 17-18%; Q1 should track at ~19-20% if trading outperforms.
