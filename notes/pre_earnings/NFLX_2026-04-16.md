# NFLX (Netflix) -- Pre-Earnings Notes
## Earnings Date: April 16, 2026 (After Market, Thursday)
## Quarter: Q1 2026 (Jan-Mar)

---

### Set-up

Netflix is the dominant global streaming platform with approximately 300M+ paid subscribers. The stock trades around $96 (post-10:1 split), roughly flat over the past year but well below all-time highs. Market cap is approximately $425B.

Q4 2025 results were solid: EPS $0.56 beat consensus of $0.55 (+1.8%), following a mixed 2025 where the company beat in Q1 (+15.6%) and Q2 (+1.8%) but missed in Q3 (-15.4%). Full-year 2025 EPS was $2.53. The company no longer reports subscriber numbers quarterly, shifting focus to revenue and operating margins. Ad-supported tier adoption and pricing power are the new key debates.

Analyst consensus is firmly bullish: among 49 analysts, 31 rate Strong Buy, 5 Moderate Buy, and 13 Hold. Average price target is $114.86, implying ~19.5% upside. The stock has outperformed FAANG peers YTD thanks to perceived recession-resilience in entertainment spending.

### Key Debates and Variants

1. **Advertising revenue trajectory**: The ad-supported tier launched in 2022 and has been scaling steadily. Street expects ad revenue could reach ~$3B in 2026 (~6% of total revenue). Q1 is typically seasonally softer for ad spend. Any acceleration or commentary on ad ARPU versus subscriber ARPU will be critical.

2. **Margin expansion vs. content spending**: Netflix guided operating margins to expand further in 2026. Content spending decisions (live sports, games) vs. margin discipline is the key tension. FCF is expected around $11B for full year 2026.

3. **Subscriber growth without disclosure**: Since Netflix stopped reporting quarterly subscribers, investors must infer growth from revenue trends and management commentary. Any change in disclosure or color on subscriber trends will move the stock.

4. **Password sharing crackdown maturation**: The revenue uplift from paid sharing enforcement is now well into its second year. Is the incremental benefit fading? Are there new levers?

5. **Competitive positioning**: Disney+, Amazon Prime Video, and Apple TV+ continue to invest heavily. Is Netflix gaining or losing share in key markets? Warner Bros Discovery spinoff removes a distraction for analysis.

6. **Macro sensitivity**: In a slowing macro environment (current regime: Deflation Risk), entertainment spending is historically resilient. But if consumer pullback deepens, even Netflix could see engagement headwinds.

### What Matters This Print

1. **Revenue growth**: Street expects meaningful YoY revenue growth. Any deceleration below expectations would concern the market given the premium valuation.

2. **EPS**: Consensus $0.76 (up 15.2% from $0.66 in Q1 2025). Netflix beat Q1 2025 by +15.6%, so the bar for a meaningful surprise is higher.

3. **Operating margin**: The expansion trajectory is key. Netflix has been guiding margins higher; any slip would challenge the profitability narrative.

4. **Free cash flow**: Expected ~$11B for FY2026. Q1 FCF sets the trajectory. Investors want confirmation that content spending is being disciplined.

5. **Ad tier commentary**: Revenue contribution, ARPU trends, CPM pricing, advertiser demand. This is the fastest-growing revenue stream and the most uncertain.

### Scenario Grid

**Bull Case (Stock +5-10%, ~$101-106)**
- Revenue beats with acceleration
- EPS well above $0.76 (matching Q1 2025 pattern of big beats)
- Operating margin expansion confirmed or raised
- Strong ad revenue commentary -- ARPU inflecting higher
- Positive comments on engagement trends and international growth
- FY2026 guidance maintained or raised

**Base Case (Stock -2% to +3%, ~$94-99)**
- Revenue and EPS roughly in line ($0.76 +/- $0.03)
- Operating margin stable to slightly expanding
- Standard commentary on ad tier growth
- No major guidance changes
- Typical post-earnings drift

**Bear Case (Stock -5-10%, ~$86-91)**
- Revenue miss or deceleration
- EPS below $0.76, especially if margin pressure emerges
- Weak ad revenue commentary -- CPMs declining, advertiser pullback
- Content spending surprises to the upside (margin compression)
- Negative commentary on engagement or churn
- Lowered guidance for FY2026
