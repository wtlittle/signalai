## Key Debates and Variants

1. **AI demand sustainability and CoWoS capacity**: TSMC projected AI accelerator revenue growing at least 50% annually through 2029. The core debate is whether Q1 2026 data confirms this trajectory or shows any early signs of capex moderation. CoWoS advanced packaging for HBM+GPU stacks remains capacity-constrained; any commentary on CoWoS expansion timelines is a direct read-through to Nvidia, AMD, and Broadcom revenue visibility.

2. **Gross margin trajectory -- can 65%+ be structural?**: Q4 2025 gross margin was strong; Q1 2026 guidance is 63-65%. With N3/N3E now a larger revenue percentage, the favorable mix should support margin expansion. Bears worry that Arizona fab ramp-up (higher labor and logistics costs vs. Taiwan) will structurally dilute margins as US capacity scales from 2026-2028. Management's commentary on cost normalization in US fabs is a critical swing factor.

3. **US tariff and export control overhang**: The US government has imposed (and may further tighten) restrictions on advanced chip exports to China. TSMC's China revenue has already declined to ~7% of total sales (from 9% prior, per April 2025 data), while North America rose to ~77%. US-Taiwan tariff deal announced Jan 15 cut reciprocal tariff from 20% to 15% on Taiwanese goods -- a tailwind vs. prior trajectory. The current Brent-at-$108 environment (vs. $115 wartime peak) reduces logistics cost anxiety for Arizona fab ramp. TSMC CEO C.C. Wei has publicly stated AI demand is "endless" and that no changes in customer behavior have been observed.

4. **N2 ramp timeline and customer commitments**: The 2nm node (N2) is expected to drive the next wave of revenue growth. Apple is the anchor customer (A20 chip); Nvidia, AMD, and Qualcomm are likely follow-on customers. Any update on N2 yield improvement, tape-out schedules, or customer commitments would be a positive catalyst. IDC projects TSMC revenue up 25% in 2026 in USD terms.

5. **Capex commitment vs. returns**: $52-56B capex for 2026 (potentially up 37% YoY) is an enormous capital commitment. Free cash flow generation is the primary concern -- at $55B capex and ~$88B revenue, FCF margins compress significantly. The long-term bull case requires sustained >30% revenue growth to generate the FCF needed to justify this investment level. Any softening in Q2 guidance that implies deceleration below 30% growth would challenge the capex thesis.

6. **Customer concentration -- Apple (AAPL) and Nvidia (NVDA) dependency**: Apple represents ~25% of TSMC revenue; Nvidia is growing rapidly and likely 15-20% of revenue. Q2 iPhone cycle dynamics (any iPhone 18 pre-production signals) and Nvidia's H200/Blackwell ramp status will be embedded in Q2 guidance implicitly.

## What Matters This Print

1. **Q1 2026 Revenue**: Guided $34.6-35.8B; consensus $35.25B. Jan-Feb monthly data suggests tracking toward high end. A beat above $36B would be a clear positive surprise.

2. **Gross Margin**: Guided 63-65%. Sustainability above 63% with US fab costs rising is the structural question. Any print above 65% challenges the bear case on margin dilution.

3. **Operating Margin**: Guided 54-56%. Leverage on AI-driven revenue growth is the core value creation story.

4. **Q2 2026 Revenue Guidance**: The single most important forward-looking datapoint. Street will want to see sequential growth above 3-5%. If TSMC guides to flat-to-down QoQ, it would signal AI demand is entering a digestion phase. If it guides $37B+, it validates the acceleration narrative.

5. **Full-year 2026 revenue growth outlook**: Management guided "close to 30%" growth at Q4. Given USD strengthening and tariff headwinds, any revision below 25% -- or a hold at 30% -- will move the stock significantly.
