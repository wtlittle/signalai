# TSM (TSMC) — Pre-Earnings Notes
## Earnings Date: April 16, 2026 (Before Open, Online Conference)
## Quarter: Q1 2026 (Jan-Mar)

---

### Set-up

TSMC is the world's dominant advanced semiconductor foundry, fabricating chips for Nvidia, Apple, AMD, Broadcom, and virtually every major AI chipmaker. The stock trades at ~$326 (ADR), up significantly over the past year on the AI infrastructure supercycle. Market cap is approximately $850B. TSMC occupies a unique position as the chokepoint of the entire AI buildout -- every advanced AI chip flows through its fabs.

Q4 2025 was a blowout: revenue of NT$1.046T ($33.7B), up 20.5% YoY, with net income of NT$505.7B (+35% YoY) and diluted EPS of NT$19.50. For full year 2025, revenue grew 31.6% to NT$3.81T, with earnings of NT$1.72T (+46.4%). The company guided Q1 2026 revenue of $34.6-35.8B, implying +4% sequential and +38% YoY -- defying typical seasonal weakness.

Capital expenditure guidance for 2026 is $52-56B, and management expects full-year 2026 revenue to jump "close to 30%." Six analysts rate TSM a "Buy" with an average PT of $401.67 (+23% upside). TSMC is expanding US manufacturing with new Arizona fabs.

### Key Debates and Variants

1. **AI demand sustainability**: The central question for TSMC. After 3 consecutive quarters of 25-35% YoY growth, is AI infrastructure spending showing any signs of moderation? Key signals: N5/N3 utilization rates, advanced packaging (CoWoS) capacity constraints.
2. **Pricing power trajectory**: TSMC has successfully raised prices on advanced nodes. Can pricing increases continue into 2026, or are customers pushing back? Gross margin trajectory is the tell.
3. **US fab economics**: Arizona fabs carry higher costs than Taiwan. As US production ramps, does it dilute margins? Any update on government subsidies (CHIPS Act funding) is important.
4. **Geopolitical risk premium**: Taiwan Strait tensions, US-China tech restrictions, and the potential for export controls tightening. These risks are well-known but any new development is a catalyst.
5. **Capex intensity**: $52-56B capex for 2026 is massive. Are returns on this investment materializing as expected? What's the timeline for 2nm (N2) revenue contribution?
6. **Customer concentration**: Nvidia, Apple, and a few others dominate revenue. Any shift in ordering patterns from top customers is signal.

### What Matters This Print

1. **Revenue**: Guided $34.6-35.8B (midpoint ~$35.2B). Any beat above the high end ($35.8B+) would signal demand acceleration. Consensus likely at or near the high end of guidance.
2. **Gross Margin**: Guided 63-65%. Q4 2025 was strong. Sustainability above 60% validates structural margin improvement (not just cycle). Higher-than-guided margins would be very bullish.
3. **Operating Margin**: Guided 54-56%. Leverage on the revenue growth is the story.
4. **Q2 2026 Guidance**: The forward-look matters most. Sequential acceleration would confirm sustained AI demand; any deceleration would trigger concerns.
5. **Advanced Node Mix**: What percentage of revenue is N5 and below? Growth in N3/N3E signals Apple and AI chip adoption. CoWoS packaging commentary for HBM/AI chips.

### Scenario Grid

**Bull Case (Stock +5-10%, ~$340-360)**
- Revenue beats high end of guide ($36B+)
- Gross margin >65%
- Q2 guide implies continued sequential acceleration
- Management raises full-year 2026 revenue growth outlook above "close to 30%"
- CoWoS capacity expansion ahead of schedule
- Strong commentary on N2 ramp and customer commitments

**Base Case (Stock -2% to +3%, ~$320-336)**
- Revenue within guided range ($34.6-35.8B)
- Gross margin at 63-65%
- Q2 guide shows ~3-5% sequential growth (seasonal)
- Full-year 2026 outlook maintained
- Standard capex commentary, no surprises

**Bear Case (Stock -5-10%, ~$293-310)**
- Revenue at or below low end of guide (<$34.6B)
- Gross margin compression below 63%
- Q2 guide softer than expected (flat or declining sequentially)
- Any commentary about AI demand "normalizing" or customer pushbacks
- Geopolitical escalation (trade restrictions, Taiwan tensions)
- US fab cost overruns impacting margin outlook
