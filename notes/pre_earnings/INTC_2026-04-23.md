## The Set-up

Intel enters Q1 2026 at a critical inflection point. Q4 2025 delivered a surprise beat -- revenue $13.7B (beat $13.4B consensus), non-GAAP EPS $0.15 (beat $0.08) -- but stock fell 12% over two sessions on weak Q1 guidance: revenue $11.7-12.7B (midpoint below the $12.55B consensus) and non-GAAP EPS of $0.00 (break-even). Management cited supply constraints from the 18A process ramp and low available die supply as the binding constraint, with improvement expected in Q2 and beyond. The stock has recovered significantly since the January selloff, trading around $54 with a 42% YTD gain, partly fueled by renewed confidence in the x86 ecosystem's relevance in AI workloads and the 18A process node attracting external foundry customers.

**Macro context (Transition regime):** Mixed signals across pillars. Growth neutral, inflation rising (CPI just printed 3.3% YoY), sentiment risk-on. The semiconductor cycle is a wild card -- AI-driven demand remains robust, but supply chain disruptions from the Iran conflict created component shortages in Q1, particularly for advanced packaging materials. The weak DXY ($98.59) provides a modest tailwind for Intel's international revenue.

## Key Debates and Variants

1. **Revenue recovery vs. supply constraint:** Management guided $11.7-12.7B, with supply being "at its lowest level in Q1 before improving in Q2." If supply constraints ease faster than expected, there could be upside. If they persist, even guided revenue could be at risk. This is the single biggest variable.

2. **18A foundry pipeline:** Intel Foundry Services (IFS) is the long-term strategic bet. Any updates on 18A customer wins, yield improvements, or foundry revenue pipeline would be catalysts. The Altera divestiture cleans up the P&L -- watch for any contribution from external foundry customers.

3. **Client Computing Group trajectory:** CCG was $7.6B in Q1 2025 (-8% YoY). PC replacement cycle dynamics and AI-PC adoption rates are key. Microsoft's Copilot+ push could drive a refresh cycle that benefits Intel's client silicon.

4. **Data Center and AI:** DCAI grew +8% YoY in Q1 2025 to $4.1B. The competitive pressure from AMD (EPYC) and NVIDIA (Grace) is intense. Gaudi AI accelerator performance vs. NVIDIA H100/H200/B100 is the critical benchmark.

5. **Margin trajectory:** Q1 2026 GAAP gross margin guided 32.3%, non-GAAP 34.5%. These are depressed levels reflecting the ramp costs. Any upside to margins would be a significant positive catalyst.
